The answer.

Bagels are still the lead lane.

Limited SKU, breakfast repeat, visible line/sellout cues, small-box potential, and premium pricing. Layla, Bart's, Starship, Nervous Charlie's, and Gables are cleaner Dan-fit than already-scaled PopUp or Jeff's.

Primary lane

Retail can beat food on CoC.

Scandy Candy and Goodies have smaller boxes, lower labor, and cleaner inventory operations than food. The diligence question is repeat purchase after novelty, not whether the store can open cheaply.

Trend durability test

Dessert works only with systems.

FIGO, Cafe Panna, Holey Grail, Sidecar, and Smoosh are attractive gross-margin categories, but cold chain, skilled production, freshness promises, and founder taste are the scaling traps.

Proof sprint.

Two-week no-outreach sprint

  1. Exact current unit map and opening timeline.
  2. Google/Yelp rating snapshot by unit.
  3. Menu and price scrape.
  4. Peak-hour throughput from reviews, social, delivery availability, and map traffic cues.
  5. Box-size and likely rent estimate.

Decision outputs

  1. Production model: in-store, commissary, hub-and-spoke, imported, or cold-chain dependent.
  2. Founder/operator and capital-status check.
  3. Category comp set and franchise/FDD benchmarks where available.
  4. Cash-on-cash hypothesis with capex, AUV, margin, labor, and payback sensitivity.
  5. Promote, comp-only, watch, or kill.
