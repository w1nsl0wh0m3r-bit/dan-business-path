1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? An independent insurance agency sells and services P&C, personal lines, commercial lines, life/health, or benefits policies and earns new-business plus renewal commissions. A “book of business” deal can be asset purchase/commission-rights transfer rather than buying a whole operating company.
  • Who are the customers? Customers are households, small businesses, contractors, landlords, real-estate investors, and benefits clients. Commercial lines are stickier and more valuable; personal lines can be retention-rich but carrier-market exposed.
  • Demand drivers: mandatory coverage, annual renewals, premium inflation, small-business formation, cyber/umbrella demand, and carrier complexity that keeps advisory value relevant. NAIC and III data show P&C premiums are large and recurring.
  • Geographic relevance: national, but NE/NY/CT/NJ attractive for commercial/property complexity; SFL has property-insurance chaos that creates need but also carrier/retention risk.
  • Market size: enormous insurance premium pool; target underwriting should focus on commission revenue, retention, carrier mix, and local niche specialization, not TAM.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Buy $500K-$2M commission revenue, $250K-$750K SDE, 10+ years, 85%+ revenue retention, diversified carriers, commercial niche or affluent personal-lines niche, account managers retained, clean E&O, and modern AMS data. Avoid books dependent on one producer, one carrier, Florida property concentration, weak documentation, or artificially inflated contingency commissions.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Franchising is less central. Options include Goosehead, Brightway Insurance, Estrella, We Insure, Pronto, and GlobalGreen-style networks/aggregators; current FDDs needed. For Dan, an independent book/agency acquisition is likely superior to paying royalties unless appointments/tech justify it.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Local independent agencies, Acrisure, Hub, Gallagher, Brown & Brown, AssuredPartners, BroadStreet Partners, Highstreet, Alera, Patriot Growth, World Insurance Associates, Goosehead, Brightway. PE-backed consolidation is intense for larger EBITDA, but sub-$500K SDE books can still be sourced locally. Moat is renewal retention, carrier appointments, niche expertise, producer/account-manager relationships, and data hygiene.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Common structures: asset purchase of expirations/renewal rights, 20%-40% seller note or earnout tied to retention, producer non-solicit, carrier consent/appointment plan, and E&O tail. SBA can finance intangible-heavy agency acquisitions if cash flow/retention are documented. A $1.31M base deal needs ~$131K down; year-one cash-on-cash can be strong but seller earnout/working capital must be modeled. Buy at 3-4x SDE / roughly 1.5-2.5x commission depending mix; do not compete with PE for platform agencies.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map local competitors, licenses/regulatory requirements, broker listings, franchise FDD requests, and 20-30 possible operators/partners.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build acquisition model, diligence 3-5 targets, verify customer/revenue retention, speak with lenders, and test operator partner fit.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit LOI on first target or reject category; include seller transition, retention/earnout mechanics, and diligence gates.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • Retention below ~85% or poor renewal data
  • Carrier appointments cannot transfer/continue
  • Seller/producer relationships drive most accounts
  • High E&O claims/compliance issues
  • Book concentrated in one carrier, niche, or catastrophe-exposed property market
  • AMS data messy enough to impair billing/service
