Handyman / Home Repair Subscription — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Verdict: GO

Strong acquisition/franchise candidate if positioned as professional, reliable small-jobs infrastructure with membership maintenance plans. The bottleneck is labor recruiting and dispatch quality, not demand.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Branded handyman company handling small repairs, punch lists, seasonal maintenance, drywall, carpentry, fixture installs, minor plumbing/electrical via licensed subs, and optional home-maintenance memberships.
  • Who are the customers? Mostly B2C homeowners, seniors, landlords; B2B property managers, realtors, HOAs, and small commercial accounts.
  • Demand drivers: Aging housing stock, DIFM shift, skilled-trade scarcity, homeowners avoiding unreliable solo contractors, recurring seasonal maintenance, and aging-in-place needs.
  • Geographic relevance: Excellent in NE/NY/CT/NJ affluent suburbs with older homes; SFL also works due to retirees, condos, storm prep, and second homes.
  • Market size: Residential home services is widely estimated at hundreds of billions nationally; handyman is a fragmented subset. Use $400B-$600B home-services market as broad estimate and verify locally.

Key assumptions:

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down; SBA says 7(a) can fund changes of ownership and caps at $5M.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite post-manager cash flow, not owner heroics.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $900K-$2.5M with 4-12 techs and dispatcher.
  • SDE range: $200K-$500K after paid GM/dispatcher.
  • Years in business: 3+ years with repeat customer cohort data.
  • Route density / customer count: 1,000+ active customers, 4+ jobs/tech/day for small jobs, <25-minute average drive time.
  • Key assets to verify: technician roster, W2 vs 1099 compliance, licenses/subcontractor agreements, CRM, repeat rate, reviews, insurance claims.
  • Red flags to avoid: unlicensed trade work, owner is chief estimator/dispatcher, no job costing, high callbacks, tech churn, weak background checks.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Ace Handyman Services: franchise fee ~$70K standard; royalty 6%, national brand fee 2%; investment ~$132K-$224K; FDD summaries cite ~$700K+ average revenue.
  • Mr. Handyman: Neighborly brand; franchise fee ~$65K-$67K; royalty ~7%, brand/ad ~2%; investment ~$143K-$215K.
  • TruBlue Total House Care: handyman + senior modifications + house care; royalty often ~6%; potentially best subscription fit.
  • Handyman Connection: franchise fee often ~$60K; royalty around 5%-6%; contractor network model.
  • FDD highlight: verify technician utilization, call-center economics, local marketing requirements, and Item 20 closures.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: Ace Handyman, Mr. Handyman, TruBlue, Handyman Connection, TaskRabbit, Thumbtack pros, Angi leads, local solo handymen, property managers' preferred vendors.
  • PE/activity: Neighborly and Authority Brands/Empower-like home-service platforms show sponsor appetite for branded home services.
  • Fragmentation: extremely fragmented; solo operators dominate and are often unreliable.
  • Moat: recruiting, trust/background checks, reviews, dispatch, standardized pricing, memberships, and B2B referral relationships.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for unverifiable add-backs; stock purchase only if licenses/contracts require it and liabilities are clean.
  • SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if profitable, owner-operated or manager-run with qualified operator, and no speculative real estate component; SBA 7(a) supports ownership changes up to $5M.
  • Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus working capital; for a $700K-$1.4M enterprise value, plan $90K-$250K cash need.
  • Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on equity after debt service.
  • Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, manager bench, clean reporting, and repeat revenue are proven.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Request Ace/Mr. Handyman/TruBlue FDDs; map broker listings; interview 5 operators about tech productivity and callback rates.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Recruit potential GM/ops partner; model technician ramp; design membership offer for seasonal maintenance.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on acquisition with clean tech roster or franchise territory decision if hiring pipeline is validated.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • No clean job-level profitability by technician.
  • Tech churn above 35% or no background-check process.
  • Callback/rework above 8%-10% of jobs.
  • Unlicensed plumbing/electrical exposure.
  • Paid lead dependence above 35% with low repeat.
  • Owner personally does most estimating/dispatch.
