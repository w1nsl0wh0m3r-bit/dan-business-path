Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

Insurance-driven emergency demand, strong tickets, and real consolidation interest make this attractive, but only with certified field leadership, referral diversity, and disciplined AR controls. Prefer acquisition of a clean independent over paying franchise royalties unless the brand has proven carrier relationships.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • Provides emergency water extraction, structural drying, mold/fire adjacent restoration, rebuild coordination, and documentation for homeowners, property managers, insurers, and commercial facilities.
  • Customers skew B2C claims via insurers, plumbers, roofers, property managers, and commercial accounts; B2B referral relationships drive durable lead flow.
  • Demand is non-discretionary after leaks, storms, frozen pipes, appliance failures, and hurricanes; NE winter freeze risk and SFL storm/humidity risk are both relevant.
  • Market size is best treated as tens of billions for U.S. damage restoration; IBISWorld and public restoration franchisors confirm a large fragmented category, but exact local TAM should be broker-built by county.

Key assumptions:

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan’s scale ($200K-$500K SDE), not platform multiples.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - annual debt service) / down payment; figures are directional and before taxes.
  • Operator partner assumption: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; model includes manager/clinical/GM reality where relevant.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

  • Good target: $1M-$4M revenue, $200K-$600K SDE, 5+ years, IICRC-certified team, 24/7 dispatch, documented equipment list, carrier/plumber/property-manager referrals, clean AR aging, and no single referral source over 25%.
  • Avoid owner-as-only-estimator, weak documentation, big unpaid insurance receivables, unlicensed mold work, and revenue spikes from one storm year.
  • Franchise candidates to diligence: SERVPRO, PuroClean, ServiceMaster Restore, Paul Davis, 1-800 WATER DAMAGE. Pull FDDs for fees, royalty, ad fund, territory, vendor restrictions, and Item 19; public pages are not enough.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • SERVPRO says it has more than 2,000 locations, so national brand competition is real, but local independents still win through speed, relationships, and reviews.
  • Other major competitors/roll-ups: BELFOR, ATI Restoration, BluSky, ServiceMaster Restore, Paul Davis, PuroClean, and regional independents.
  • Moat is response time, IICRC credentials, estimator/adjuster credibility, referral relationships, equipment utilization, and claims documentation discipline.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Structure as asset purchase with seller note/earnout tied to referral retention and AR collectability; exclude stale receivables unless discounted.
  • SBA 7(a) can finance changes of ownership up to $5M; at 10% down, a $1.24M base purchase needs roughly $124K plus closing costs and working capital.
  • Base payback is ~2.5-3.5 years after debt if manager-adjusted SDE is real. Multiple arbitrage is modest: 3.5-4.0x in, maybe 4.0-4.5x out with systems and scale.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Days 1-30: map Boston/Providence/CT/NY/NJ/SFL competitors, pull FDDs, speak with insurance/restoration brokers, and build a plumber/property-manager referral map.
  • Days 31-60: identify operator partner or GM, review 5 CIMs/P&Ls, verify IICRC bench, equipment, AR, and job-level gross margin.
  • Days 61-90: submit IOI on one independent or kill if manager-adjusted returns fall below hurdle.

8. Kill Criteria

  • No transferable estimator/field supervisor bench.
  • AR older than 90 days is material or undocumented.
  • Seller cannot prove job-level margins and referral sources.
  • Purchase price above 4.5x verified SDE.
  • Franchise royalties/vendor restrictions crush debt-service coverage.
