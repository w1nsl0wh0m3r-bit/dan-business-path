## 1. Category Overview

**What it is:** Residential and commercial roofing companies install, repair, and replace roofs — asphalt shingle, metal, flat/low-slope (TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen), tile, and slate. Revenue spans one-time replacement projects, repair work, and increasingly, commercial maintenance contracts.

**Revenue model:**
- **Residential reroof/replacement:** $8K-$25K per project. One-time revenue with potential for referrals. Weather and storm driven.
- **Commercial maintenance/re-roof:** $20K-$500K+ per project. Multi-year service contracts create recurring revenue. Property management and REIT relationships.
- **Repair/service calls:** $300-$3,000 per visit. Lower ticket but higher frequency, fills schedule between big jobs.
- **Storm restoration/insurance claims:** $10K-$50K per insurance job. Revenue spikes after hail/wind events but is non-recurring and heavily scrutinized by buyers.

**Market size:** U.S. roofing contractors market is ~$50-55B annually (IBISWorld). ~100,000 roofing contractors nationwide. The market grew significantly 2020-2024 due to storm activity, home price appreciation, and aging housing stock.

**Demand drivers:**
- **Aging housing stock:** Median U.S. home age is 40+ years. Roofs last 15-30 years — massive replacement cycle underway.
- **Storm frequency:** Hail and wind events in FL, TX, CO, OK, and Midwest drive insurance-claim replacement revenue. NE/NY has lower storm activity than Sun Belt.
- **Commercial building maintenance:** Property managers and REITs need scheduled roof maintenance to protect asset value.
- **Insurance requirements:** Many insurers now require roof inspection/replacement as a condition of coverage, especially in FL.

## 4. Regulatory and Compliance Moat

**Moderate regulatory moat — primarily through licensing and bonding.**

- **State contractor licensing:** Most states require a roofing contractor license:
  - **CT:** Requires Home Improvement Contractor registration ($$)
  - **MA:** Requires Construction Supervisor License (CSL)
  - **NY:** No statewide roofing license (local permits only) — LOW barrier
  - **FL:** Requires Certified Roofing Contractor license (CRC) — significant barrier with exam, experience, and financial requirements
  - **RI:** Requires Contractor Registration
- **Bonding capacity:** Commercial work requires bonding (performance/payment bonds). Bonding capacity is tied to the company's financials and track record — it doesn't transfer automatically to a buyer. New owners start with lower bonding limits, constraining commercial project size.
- **Workers' comp insurance:** Roofing has one of the highest workers' comp rates in construction (~15-25% of payroll in high-risk states). Experience modification rate (EMR) follows the entity, not the owner.
- **OSHA compliance:** Fall protection is the #1 OSHA citation in construction. Roofing businesses face strict safety compliance requirements and significant liability exposure.
- **License transfer:** Most states require the buyer to hold or hire a qualifying individual. This is a gating item in deal structure.

**Compliance moat score: MODERATE.** Licensing creates entry barriers (especially FL, MA), but NY has minimal barriers. Bonding and insurance requirements add complexity but don't create the type of compliance-driven recurring revenue Dan prefers.

## 5. Competitive Dynamics

**Fragmentation:** High. ~100,000 roofing contractors, most with revenue under $2M. Top 100 contractors hold only ~15-20% of market share.

**PE roll-up activity — EXTREME:**
- This is one of the most active PE consolidation categories in home services
- PE firms are acquiring a roofing platform every 48 hours (BusinessExits data)
- 134 disclosed deals expected in 2025 alone
- Active platforms: Service Logic, CentiMark (Audax), Tecta America (Altas), Flynn Group, plus regional rollups backed by Bain Capital, Audax, Gryphon, Berkshire Partners
- $800M+ in disclosed deal volume tracked

**Consolidator arbitrage:** A roofing company that trades at 2.5-3.5x SDE standalone can be worth 6-8x EBITDA inside a PE platform. This spread drives aggressive buyer competition for quality targets above $1.5M EBITDA.

**Franchise presence:** Limited. Roofing is primarily an independent contractor business. Some franchise concepts (LeafFilter, Power Home Remodeling) touch roofing adjacent work but aren't pure-play roofing franchises.

**Storm-region dynamic:** Storm-restoration operators in FL, TX, CO, OK face a 0.5-1.5x multiple discount because insurance-claim revenue is non-recurring. Buyers normalize against a 3-5 year non-storm baseline.

## 8. Risk Factors

- **Storm revenue normalization:** If 60%+ of last 3 years' revenue came from a single storm event, buyers will normalize downward. A $5M business becomes a $1.5M baseline + temporary windfall.
- **Insurance crisis in FL:** FL property insurance market is in crisis — some insurers are dropping coverage or requiring roof replacements as a condition of renewal. This drives demand but also creates regulatory/policy instability.
- **Labor scarcity:** Roofing is physically demanding work with an aging workforce. Finding and retaining crews is the #1 operational challenge. Painter/roofer scarcity in 2026 means crew retention commands a premium.
- **Workers' comp exposure:** Highest WC rates in construction. A serious injury or fall can devastate a small company.
- **Weather dependency:** NE/NY roofing season is April-October (weather permitting). Winter revenue drops significantly unless the business has commercial maintenance or interior work.
- **Bonding transfer:** Commercial bonding capacity doesn't transfer to a new owner. A buyer starts with lower limits, constraining project size for 12-24 months.
- **OSHA liability:** Fall protection violations carry heavy fines and create workers' comp exposure.
- **Material cost inflation:** Asphalt shingle prices are petroleum-linked. Price volatility compresses margins on fixed-price contracts.

## 9. Verdict: WATCH (Confidence: MEDIUM)

**Why:** Roofing has the best PE exit market of any category in the definitive 100, but the operational profile doesn't match Dan's buy box. It's project-based, weather-dependent, labor-intensive, and safety-risk-heavy. Dan prefers "boring" recurring revenue with compliance tailwinds — roofing is the opposite: exciting (storm-driven), unpredictable, and physically risky.

**The bull case:** If Dan is willing to operate through a partner/manager, a commercial-focused roofing business with maintenance contracts in MA or FL (both have strong licensing barriers) could generate $200K-$400K SDE with an exit to a PE consolidator at 5-7x EBITDA. The consolidator arbitrage is real — buy at 3x, sell at 6x.

**The bear case:** Dan doesn't have roofing experience. The labor model is brutal. Workers' comp and OSHA exposure are significant. The "boring" criterion fails — roofing is weather-gambling and crew-whispering. And at Dan's target price point ($500K-$750K), most targets are residential shops with no recurring revenue.

**Pursue IF:** Dan identifies a commercial roofing platform with maintenance contracts in MA or FL (licensed states), $250K+ SDE, and a strong #2 operations manager who stays post-close. The PE exit optionality is a genuine value driver. Otherwise WATCH.
