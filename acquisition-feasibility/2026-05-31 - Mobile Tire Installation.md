# Mobile Tire Installation — Acquisition Feasibility Memo

## Category #16 from Definitive 100 List

---

## 1. Category Overview

**What it is:** Mobile tire installation businesses bring tire mounting, balancing, rotation, flat repair, and seasonal changeover services directly to customer locations — homes, offices, fleet yards, and roadside. Unlike traditional tire shops, there is no fixed retail location; the service is delivered from outfitted vans or trucks.

**Revenue model:** Service fees per job ($25-$200 per visit) plus tire markup when selling tires directly. The economics split into two models:
- **Consumer/residential:** Premium pricing for convenience. $75-$200 per visit. High acquisition cost, irregular repeat.
- **Fleet/B2B contracts:** Volume pricing ($30-$60 per tire), but consistent scheduled volume. One fleet account with 20 vehicles needing quarterly service = ~80 calls/year from one relationship.

**Market size:** The mobile tire installation market was valued at ~$0.68B in 2026 and projected to reach $1.55B by 2035 (CAGR ~9.5%). Broader mobile tire services market estimated at $2.1B in 2025, growing at ~7.8% CAGR.

**Demand drivers:**
- EV tire consumption: EVs wear tires ~20% faster than ICE vehicles (Michelin data)
- Fleet growth: Amazon, FedEx, UPS last-mile delivery contractors need fast on-site tire response
- Seasonal tire changeovers in NE/NY climate (winter/summer swaps)

## 2. Acquisition Landscape

**Typical deal size:** $150K-$850K asking price for established mobile tire businesses.

**Broker activity:** Moderate. Mobile tire is a newer category — fewer broker listings than traditional tire shops (83 tire stores on BizBuySell). Most deals happen through direct outreach or franchise resales.

**Multiple range:**
- Sub-$250K SDE: **2.0-3.0x SDE** (IBBA Market Pulse Q1 2026)
- $250K-$500K SDE: **2.5-3.5x SDE**
- Multi-van fleet operations with contracted revenue: up to **3.5-4.0x SDE**

**Who is buying:** SBA-financed individual buyers, search funders exploring auto services, adjacent auto service operators. No significant PE roll-up activity yet — category too small for platform plays.

## 4. Regulatory and Compliance Moat

**Limited regulatory moat — this is a weakness.**

- **No specialized license required** in most states. Unlike inspection stations (DMV-licensed) or auto repair shops (ASE certified), mobile tire installers operate with just a business license and insurance.
- **Insurance requirements:** General liability + garage keepers liability + commercial auto. ~$2K-$5K/year per van.
- **Low equipment cost:** $12K-$33K total startup cost per van. Anyone with a van and $15K can start competing.
- **Entry barrier verdict:** Very low. No licensing, compliance, or capital moat.

---

## 5. Competitive Dynamics

**Fragmentation:** Extreme. Thousands of one-van operators. No dominant national brand.

**PE roll-up activity:** Essentially zero at the mobile tire level. Traditional tire retail consolidation (Mavis at $6B EV, Sun Auto at 12-14x EBITDA) is happening at the storefront level.

**Threat from incumbents:** Major tire retailers (Discount Tire, Mavis, Monro) could launch mobile service at any time using existing infrastructure. This is the existential risk.

## 8. Risk Factors

- **No moat:** Low entry barrier means constant new competition
- **Incumbent threat:** Discount Tire, Mavis, Monro can launch mobile service at any time
- **Weather/seasonal exposure:** Revenue concentrates around seasonal changeover months
- **Tire supply chain risk:** American Tire Distributors filed Chapter 11 in Oct 2024
- **Labor dependency:** Each van needs a trained technician
- **Liability exposure:** Working on customer vehicles at their location
- **Low SDE ceiling:** Solo van caps at ~$60K-$100K SDE

---

## 9. Verdict: SKIP (Confidence: HIGH)

**Why:** Fails Dan's core buy box on every important dimension — no regulatory moat, no compliance tailwind, limited recurring revenue, no barrier to entry. Anyone with $15K and a van is a competitor.

**Kill criteria:**
- No regulatory or licensing barrier to entry
- Incumbent tire retailers can vertically integrate mobile at any time
- SDE ceiling too low for standalone acquisition
- Fleet contracts not locked in by compliance

**One scenario where this makes sense:** Bolt-on to an existing auto services platform that already has fleet relationships. Standalone? No.
