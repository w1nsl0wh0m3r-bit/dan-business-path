## 1. Category Overview

Window and door replacement companies measure, sell, and install replacement windows, entry doors, and patio doors in existing homes. It's a marketing-and-sales-led business, not a pure trades business — the revenue engine is lead generation, in-home sales appointments, and consumer financing, with installation crews executing the contracted work.

**Revenue Model:**
- Replacement window installation: **$1,200–$2,500 per opening** (2025–2026 per Modernize/HomeAdvisor)
- Premium operators: **$1,800–$3,500+ per opening** (Renewal by Andersen, Pella ProDealer)
- Entry/patio door replacement: **$1,500–$5,000 per door**
- Average installed job size: **$9,000–$15,000** (4–8 windows), lifting to **$20,000–$35,000** with consumer financing (GreenSky, Synchrony)
- Warranty service: **cost center**, not a profit center

**Revenue is project-based and one-time.** Like siding, once windows are replaced, the customer is gone for 25+ years. However, the business model is fundamentally different — it's a sales-driven machine, not a trades-driven workshop.

**Market Size:**
- US replacement window market: **~$20–25B annually** (installed)
- 14,000+ US window installation establishments (IBISWorld 2025)
- Top 50 control under 20% of market — highly fragmented
- Median US home age: 42 years (Census ACS) — structural replacement demand
- IRA Section 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: up to $600/year for ENERGY STAR certified windows through 2032 — taxpayer-funded demand subsidy

| Revenue per installer crew/day | $3,000–$8,000 |
| Capex per crew | $25K–$50K (truck, tools) |

| SDE range (franchise-affiliated, $2M+ revenue) | $300K–$700K |
| SDE range (platform-grade, $3M+ revenue) | $500K–$2M+ |

**Consumer Financing Impact (CT Acquisitions):**
GreenSky, Synchrony, Service Finance, and Foundation Finance now underwrite a massive share of replacement jobs. Same-as-cash promotional periods (12, 18, 24 months) and 84–144 month amortizations have shifted the median ticket from $9–15K to $20–35K. That ticket lift is 5–9 percentage points of gross margin on financed jobs. **Financing is the single biggest unit economics driver in this category.**

**Recurring Revenue:** **Effectively zero.** Replacement windows last 25+ years. No annual service cycle. No mandated inspection. The only recurring touch is warranty callbacks (cost center).

## 4. Regulatory & Compliance Moat

**Weak-to-moderate moat:**

- **Contractor licensing** required in most states (home improvement contractor license)
- **EPA RRP certification** — required for pre-1978 homes (lead paint disturbance). One-day course.
- **OSHA compliance** — fall protection, glass handling
- **IRA Section 25C** — federal tax credit up to $600/year for ENERGY STAR certified windows through 2032. This creates a taxpayer-funded demand subsidy, but it's not a mandate — it's an incentive.
- **Energy code requirements** — some jurisdictions are tightening window energy code requirements (U-factor, SHGC), which forces replacement of non-compliant windows in renovation work. But this is building-code-driven, not a recurring compliance mandate.
- **No mandated inspection cycle** — nothing requires a homeowner to inspect, service, or replace windows on any schedule

**Bottom line:** No compliance-driven captive demand. The regulatory environment creates demand tailwinds (energy codes, tax credits) but no recurring mandate.

## 5. Competitive Dynamics

**Fragmentation:** Extremely high. 14,000+ establishments, top 50 under 20% share. Perfect conditions for roll-up.

**PE Roll-Up Activity:** Very active. This is one of the hottest home services consolidation plays:
- Great Day Improvements (Littlejohn) aggressively adding
- Cornerstone Building Brands (CD&R) — commercial + residential exterior
- Audax, Trilantic, Morgan Stanley CP — mid-market regional platforms
- Renewal by Andersen franchise network consolidation — independent operators being acquired by franchise consolidators

**Franchise Presence:** Significant and growing:
- **Renewal by Andersen (RBA)** — ~120 franchise dealers; premium product, premium valuations, strong brand
- **Window World** — 110+ franchise locations; value/mid-tier; high volume
- **Pella ProDealer** — Pella ESOP-owned dealer network; premium brand

Franchise affiliation adds 1–2 turns of EBITDA multiple vs. unaffiliated independents (CT Acquisitions).

**The competitive dynamic is sales-process-driven, not trade-skill-driven.** The operators who win are the ones with the best lead-gen engines, highest set rates, and best financing integration. It's closer to a consumer finance business than a trade business.

## 8. Risk Factors

- **Zero recurring revenue** — the core Dan-fit problem. No annual service, no mandated inspection.
- **Sales-process dependency** — the business lives or dies on set rate, lead cost, and finance attach. This is a consumer marketing business wearing a contractor's hat.
- **Consumer financing risk** — if GreenSky/Synchrony tighten underwriting or raise promotional costs, ticket sizes compress and margins drop.
- **Lead cost inflation** — digital ad costs for home services are rising fast. Cost per qualified lead at $150–$450 is already squeezing margins.
- **Installer labor** — replacement window installation requires skill and precision. Poor installation = warranty claims and reputation damage.
- **Cyclical demand** — tied to housing turnover, renovation spending, and interest rates.
- **Seasonality (NE)** — winter installation is possible but slower. FL is year-round.
- **Franchise risk** — if buying a franchise resale, franchisor approval, transfer fees, and territory restrictions add complexity.
- **Warranty exposure** — window manufacturers offer 20-year+ warranties. Installation warranty is on the contractor. Long-tail liability.

## 9. Verdict: SKIP (Medium-High Confidence)

Window/door replacement has strong PE consolidation momentum and real acquisition market depth. But it fundamentally mismatches Dan's buy box:

- ❌ No recurring revenue (25-year replacement cycle)
- ❌ Not route-based (project-based installation)
- ❌ No compliance mandate
- ❌ Sales-and-marketing-led, not operations-led (Dan's edge is operations)
- ❌ Consumer-financing-dependent (credit cycle exposure)

The SBA Pre-Approved target at $650K / $247K SDE is a legitimate deal. But the business model is a consumer sales engine, not a compliance-route service. Dan would be managing salespeople, lead-gen funnels, and financing partnerships — not routes, compliance, and operations.

**The one angle worth noting:** If Dan were building a broader home services platform and wanted an acquisition engine with strong PE exit potential, window/door is one of the better categories. The 7–10x EBITDA platform exit multiples are real. But that's a different thesis than "buy boring recurring routes."

**Action:** None for now. Park unless Dan pivots toward a home services platform thesis where sales-driven replacement businesses make sense.

**Kill criteria:** Skip unless Dan explicitly wants a sales-and-marketing-led home improvement business. The core recurring-route thesis doesn't apply here.
