# Premium / Mobile Pet Grooming — Deep Dive

*Generated: 2026-05-25 ET | Rank: #15 | Blended Score: 3.67 | Confidence: Medium-High*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **CONDITIONAL GO, best as route-density acquisition**

Mobile pet grooming is a solid lifestyle-plus-services category: recurring demand, affluent customer base, convenience premium, and relatively simple local marketing. It fits Dan’s consumer/service intuition. But it has one giant constraint: groomer labor. Good groomers are scarce, can churn to self-employment, and capacity is capped by vans + people.

**Verdict:** attractive if buying route density, groomer retention, and repeat bookings. Risky as a de novo franchise unless the franchisor materially solves recruiting/training.

## 6. Operator Economics

A 5-van business at $240K/van = $1.2M revenue. At 18% SDE, pre-manager cash flow is $216K. A dispatcher/GM costs $70K-$100K; true manager-run cash flow is **$115K-$150K pre-debt**, before replacement capex. If vans are financed and groomers churn, cash flow compresses quickly.

Best structure: acquire 3-8 vans with recurring routes, keep seller through transition, implement centralized scheduling, membership/prebook discipline, and groomer retention bonuses.

## 7. Acquisition Targets

**Look for:**
- 70%+ customers prebooked for next appointment
- Groomer tenure >18 months
- Van-level P&L and utilization
- Dense routes in affluent ZIPs
- Review count/quality by groomer
- Low no-show/cancellation rate

**Multiples:** 2.5x-3.5x SDE for clean multi-van operators; lower for one/two-van owner-groomers where the owner is the asset.

## 10. Kill Criteria

- Owner is primary groomer or dispatcher with no bench.
- Groomer churn >25%-30% annually.
- No van-level P&L/utilization data.
- Customer repeat/prebook rate below 50%.
- Purchase price ignores near-term van replacement.
