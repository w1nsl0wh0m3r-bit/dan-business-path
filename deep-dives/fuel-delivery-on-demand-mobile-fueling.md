# Fuel Delivery / On-Demand Mobile Fueling — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: NO-GO
Interesting B2B route concept, but hazardous-material compliance, working-capital exposure, thin fuel gross margins and venture-backed competitors make it a poor fit at Dan’s small acquisition scale unless a very specific local fleet route is available cheaply.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Mobile fueling operators deliver gasoline, diesel, renewable diesel or DEF directly to commercial fleets, generators, construction equipment or consumers. The investable version is B2B fleet fueling with contracted delivery windows, not consumer app-based emergency gas delivery.
- Who are the customers? Primarily B2B fleets: delivery vans, landscaping, construction, municipalities, rental equipment, logistics yards, marinas/generators. B2C on-demand fuel is secondary and usually unattractive.
- Demand drivers: Fleet downtime reduction, labor savings from avoiding gas stations, centralized fuel reporting, emergency generator resilience and outsourced compliance. Demand is real, but economics depend on dense gallons/drop and disciplined fuel procurement.
- Geographic relevance: SFL has year-round fleet/generator/marine demand; NY/NJ/CT and Boston have dense fleets but heavy permitting, environmental and traffic constraints. NE winter diesel/generator use helps but regulation is harder.
- Market size: Use broad downstream fuel distribution and fleet-management markets as proxies; no reliable public niche TAM for small mobile fueling routes. Booster, EzFill and CAFU validate demand, while EzFill SEC filings illustrate that scale does not automatically mean profitability.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1.5M-$4M, but underwrite gross profit per gallon/drop, not headline revenue.
- SDE range: $150K-$400K after compliance/admin and truck maintenance.
- 5+ years with clean environmental/safety record.
- Contracted dense routes: 20+ fleet customers, low miles per gallon delivered, no customer >20%.
- Verify hazmat permits, tank/truck compliance, insurance, fuel supply terms, AR aging, customer contracts, driver certifications and spill history.
- Avoid consumer app model, scattered emergency drops, high AR, thin spread over wholesale, old trucks/tanks, environmental liabilities.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Few classic franchises; benchmarks include Booster, EzFill, CAFU, local diesel delivery, generator fueling and fleet fuel management companies.
- If a franchise/dealer appears, demand Item 19, fuel supply obligations, territory, insurance, environmental responsibility and customer ownership.
- Most value is route density and contracts, not brand. Franchise royalty can be fatal in a low-margin fuel spread business.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Booster Fuels, EzFill, CAFU, Mansfield Energy, Shipley Energy, RelaDyne, local petroleum jobbers and diesel delivery companies. PE is active in fuel distribution/jobbers at larger scale, but small mobile fueling is operationally brittle. Moat is permits, route density, customer contracts and safety record; margins are not naturally protected.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset purchase only, with environmental indemnities, seller note and escrow for spill/compliance claims. SBA may finance but DSCR must be built on gross profit after fuel-price sensitivity, not revenue. Base deal could require $60K-$125K down plus meaningful working capital for fuel inventory/AR. Multiple arbitrage is weak unless route density creates a contracted local platform.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map fleet yards, generator/marine demand and local petroleum jobbers in SFL and NY/NJ; collect permit/insurance requirements.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Talk to operators, environmental counsel and lenders; model gross margin per gallon, truck utilization and AR/fuel working capital.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Only pursue LOI if contracted routes show >20% gross margin on service component and clean compliance; otherwise no-go.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Any unresolved spill/environmental liability.
- Consumer on-demand model rather than contracted fleets.
- Gross profit per drop too low after driver/truck time.
- AR/fuel working capital consumes cash-on-cash return.
- Insurance or permitting prevents SBA finance.
