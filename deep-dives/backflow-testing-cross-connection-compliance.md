# Backflow Testing / Cross-Connection Compliance — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Category rank: 23 | Blended score: 3.53*

## 1. Executive Verdict — GO, IF ROUTE-DENSITY ACQUISITION EXISTS
Backflow testing is one of the better under-the-radar compliance services: annual/periodic mandated testing, fragmented local providers, low equipment intensity, and sticky customer lists. It is not sexy, but that is the point. The risk is that many businesses are too small, owner-technician dependent, and municipal rules vary by jurisdiction.

**Dan-specific take:** This is a **strong buy-and-build candidate** if he can acquire a book of 1,000+ recurring commercial devices/customers and put a licensed operator in charge. Dan’s PE diligence edge helps: recurring revenue quality, route density, tech productivity, municipal compliance relationships.

## 2. Market Sizing
- EPA’s cross-connection control materials describe the public-health need to prevent contaminants from entering drinking water systems through backflow/cross-connections ([EPA drinking water/cross-connection resources](https://www.epa.gov/dwreginfo)).
- Backflow prevention rules are implemented locally/statewide; many commercial properties must test devices annually and submit reports to water authorities.
- Market size is best built bottom-up: number of commercial/multi-family/industrial properties with backflow devices × annual test fee. A New England metro with 20,000 testable devices at $125/test implies **$2.5M annual testing revenue** before repairs/installations.

## 3. Unit Economics
| Metric | Range | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Annual test ticket | $75-$200/device | Commercial and multi-device sites improve economics. |
| Repair ticket | $250-$1,500+ | Rebuild kits, valves, freeze damage. |
| Devices/day/tech | 8-20 | Depends on routing and reporting friction. |
| Gross margin | 55-75% testing; 40-60% repair | Labor productivity is everything. |
| Revenue/tech | $180K-$350K | Dense routes + repairs lift top end. |
| SDE margin | 20-35% | High if owner-tech not overcounted. |
| Entry multiple | 2.5-4.0x SDE | Recurring list quality supports premium. |
| Payback | 3-4 years | Better than many trades if retention is real. |

**Example:** 1,500 annual tests at $135 = $202.5K base recurring revenue. Add 35% repairs/install = $273K. One tech can cover this with routing/admin support. A 4-tech platform can do $900K-$1.4M revenue and $220K-$400K SDE.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **Local certified backflow testers** — plumbers, irrigation firms, fire-safety companies, and dedicated testers.
- **Backflow Solutions, Inc. / BSI Online** — compliance management platform used by municipalities/utilities; can influence contractor reporting workflows.
- **HydroCorp** — cross-connection control program services for utilities/municipalities.
- **Fire protection companies** — often test fire-line backflow devices as part of inspection routes.
- **Plumbing contractors** — compete on repairs/installations, less focused on annual route efficiency.

## 5. Franchise Comp Table
There is no obvious national consumer franchise path comparable to garage doors/locksmithing. This is primarily an **independent acquisition + route roll-up** strategy. Franchise FDD diligence is not the gating item; certification/licensing and local water-authority compliance workflows are.

## 6. Operator Economics
This is operator-friendly if the GM is licensed or can manage licensed techs. Budget **$75-100K** for an operations lead with field credibility. Because testing has a compliance calendar, Dan can manage by dashboard: scheduled tests, completion %, failed devices, repair conversion, AR days, route density.

A $300K SDE owner-operated business may still produce **$180-240K** after operator replacement if the owner add-back is not entirely field labor. If owner is the only certified tester, haircut aggressively.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
**Look for:** recurring annual device list, customer/device database, municipal portal access, route density, mix of commercial/multi-family/industrial accounts, certified techs, repair attach rate, low churn, documented renewal process.

**Avoid:** residential irrigation-only seasonal books, owner-only license, paper records, no device-level data, customers acquired through one municipal relationship at risk of rebid.

**Multiple:** 3-4x SDE is acceptable for clean recurring commercial list; 2-2.5x for owner-tech microbusiness.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No device-level recurring list.
- Owner is only certified tester and unwilling to transition.
- Revenue is mostly one-time installs, not annual testing.
- Route density too low: >45 minutes average windshield time.
- Municipal platform/rules create commoditized lowest-bid market.
