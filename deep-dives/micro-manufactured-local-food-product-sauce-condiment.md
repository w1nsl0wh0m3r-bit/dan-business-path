# Micro-Manufactured Local Food Product / Sauce/Condiment — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: NO-GO
Fun brand-building category, but for Dan this is mostly a working-capital and distribution trap unless buying a profitable B2B/private-label niche manufacturer, not a cute local hot sauce brand.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Small-batch sauces, condiments, dressings, marinades, salsa, hot sauce, jams, or local specialty foods sold through farmers markets, DTC, wholesale grocery, foodservice, and private label.
- Who are the customers? B2C specialty food buyers plus B2B retailers, restaurants, distributors, co-pack/private-label customers.
- Demand drivers: Premiumization, local/ethnic flavor discovery, specialty grocery, gifting, and foodservice menu differentiation; recurrence exists but shelf velocity is hard.
- Geographic relevance: Best in Boston/NE affluent suburbs, NY/CT/NJ density, and SFL where property/consumer demand supports premium pricing.
- Market size: Specialty Food Association reports a large U.S. specialty food market; sauces/condiments are tracked by Grand View/Statista-style market reports, but local brand SAM should be based on wholesale doors and reorder velocity.

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE), not platform multiples.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down; assumes acquisition loan, not real estate-backed 25-year debt.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment; estimates use annual debt service of roughly 16.5%-16.8% of principal at 10 years / 11%.
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; the model deducts enough management burden to avoid buying a job.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $800K-$3M only if profitable and repeat wholesale
- SDE range: $150K-$400K
- Years in business: 3+ with reorder history
- Route density / customer count: 100+ active wholesale doors or sticky private-label customers
- Key assets to verify: Recipes/IP, FDA/food safety, co-packer/manufacturing, gross margin by SKU, customer velocity, distributor terms
- Red flags to avoid: Founder is brand/sales engine, negative gross margin after freight/promos, slotting fees, obsolete inventory

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
Not a franchise category. Consider acquisition of co-packer/private-label shop instead of consumer brand.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors / roll-ups: Local specialty brands, Stonewall Kitchen-type premium brands, private-label manufacturers, grocery private label, online hot sauce/sauce brands
- PE activity: Consumer packaged food sees strategic/PE activity, but small brands without velocity rarely receive attractive multiples.
- Fragmentation level: Extremely fragmented at micro-brand level.
- Moat: Retail velocity, repeat wholesale accounts, defensible recipe/process, food safety, distribution, gross margin discipline.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note/holdback, transition services, employee retention, and working-capital peg.
- SBA eligibility and terms: Usually eligible if profitable, transferable, and clean books; use 10-year / ~11% / 10% down.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: $60K-$180K for a $600K-$1.8M EV target.
- Payback period at base case: Base case 3-5 years.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3-4x SDE, professionalize, and exit around 4x if growth/manager depth is proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Screen 50 local brands/co-packers; request SKU-level margins and reorder reports.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview specialty food distributors/retail buyers and validate chargebacks, freight, slotting, spoilage.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Kill unless a profitable B2B manufacturer/private-label asset appears at a low multiple.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Gross margin below 35%-45% after freight/promos
- No SKU-level velocity data
- Founder-led sales with no repeat reorder base
- Inventory write-offs or food safety gaps
- Needs national retail expansion to work
