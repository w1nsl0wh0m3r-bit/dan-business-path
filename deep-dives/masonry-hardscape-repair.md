# Masonry / Hardscape Repair — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
This is one of the better operator-partner trades: fragmented, referral-driven, high ticket, recurring repair demand from aging patios/walls/chimneys, and less franchise froth than trendy wellness/food.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Repair and replacement of masonry, pavers, patios, steps, retaining walls, chimneys, walkways, stoops, drainage-adjacent hardscape, and small commercial masonry.
- Who are the customers? Primarily B2C homeowners/HOAs; B2B property managers, municipalities, landscapers, builders, and commercial facilities.
- Demand drivers: Aging housing stock, freeze/thaw damage in NE, stormwater/drainage issues, outdoor living spend, trip hazards, and insurance/home-sale repairs.
- Geographic relevance: Best in Boston/NE affluent suburbs, NY/CT/NJ density, and SFL where property/consumer demand supports premium pricing.
- Market size: IBISWorld tracks U.S. masonry contractors as a multi-billion-dollar industry; local market size must be estimated by permits, Google Maps density, and housing stock age.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1M-$3M
- SDE range: $200K-$500K
- Years in business: 5+ with crew leaders, not just owner mason
- Route density / customer count: 2-4 crews within 45-minute radius; 500+ completed customer records
- Key assets to verify: Crew leaders, trucks, mixers/saws, photo portfolio, estimator, permit history, reviews
- Red flags to avoid: Owner is only estimator/foreman, undocumented cash jobs, injury/safety issues, warranty backlog

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
Few pure masonry franchises; adjacent brands include Archadeck/outdoor living, System Pavers in some markets, CertaPro-like remodel adjacencies. Prefer independent acquisition over franchise.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors / roll-ups: Local masonry contractors, landscapers adding hardscape, paver installers, chimney companies, deck/outdoor living remodelers
- PE activity: Limited PE roll-up activity at this small scale; larger landscape/outdoor services platforms may tuck in hardscape.
- Fragmentation level: Very high mom-and-pop fragmentation.
- Moat: Crew quality, estimating discipline, reviews, design/repair knowledge, warranty trust.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note/holdback, transition services, employee retention, and working-capital peg.
- SBA eligibility and terms: Usually eligible if profitable, transferable, and clean books; use 10-year / ~11% / 10% down.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: $60K-$180K for a $600K-$1.8M EV target.
- Payback period at base case: Base case 3-5 years.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3-4x SDE, professionalize, and exit around 4x if growth/manager depth is proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map 100 contractors, permits, review leaders, and brokered listings in NE/NY/SFL.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Meet 3 operators, validate crew wages/backlog, model estimator + crew-leader structure.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on target with retained foreman/estimator and seller transition.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- No non-owner crew leader
- Backlog depends on underpriced jobs
- Workers comp/safety/liability problems
- Revenue cannot be reconciled to bank/tax records
- Warranty callbacks >5%-8% of jobs
