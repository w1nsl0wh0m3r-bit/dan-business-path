# Sump Pump Installation / Maintenance — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Useful bolt-on/home-services niche but too narrow as a standalone unless bundled with basement waterproofing, drains, battery backups, and monitoring. Attractive in wet/old-basement NE towns with a strong emergency-response operator.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Sump pump companies install/replace pumps, basins, discharge lines, check valves, battery backups, Wi-Fi monitors, drainage tile, and annual maintenance plans. Customers are homeowners with basements/crawlspaces, landlords, property managers, and light commercial buildings. Demand is driven by heavy rainfall, groundwater, old basements, finished-basement value, power outages, and insurance anxiety. NE/NY/NJ are very relevant; SFL less so because basements are rare, but stormwater/pump work exists in different form. Market size should be treated as a local waterproofing/plumbing submarket, not a clean standalone national category.
- Who are the customers? Mix varies, but target underwriting should separate residential emergency demand from B2B/property-manager/municipal accounts.
- Demand drivers: needs-based failure, compliance/inspection triggers, aging infrastructure, and recurring maintenance or consumables where applicable.
- Geographic relevance: NE/Boston exurbs and NY/CT/NJ are primary for this batch; SFL is category-specific and weaker for basement/private-well models.
- Market size: use cited national installed-base indicators where available; otherwise treat as local serviceable-market estimate and do bottom-up ZIP/county mapping.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue $1M-$2M, SDE $200K-$450K, 5+ years, 30%+ revenue from backups/maintenance/waterproofing, fast dispatch, electrician/plumber relationships, documented warranties. Avoid pure emergency lead-gen shops, low-review contractors, no licensed plumbing/electrical coverage, or broad waterproofing claims without job costing.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Basement Systems/Groundworks-style basement waterproofing networks, Mr. Rooter/Roto-Rooter, and local plumbing franchises are adjacent. Current FDDs required; Item 19 should prove lead cost, close rate, average ticket, and warranty reserves.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Groundworks and regional waterproofing platforms, Basement Systems dealers, local plumbers, Roto-Rooter/Mr. Rooter, and drainage contractors. Fragmentation is high, but serious waterproofing brands are professionalizing. Moat is emergency response, reviews, financing options, install quality, and maintenance/monitoring base.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset purchase preferred with warranty reserve and normalized marketing spend. SBA can finance if business is established; startup franchise risk is lead-cost/seasonality. Base $1.0M deal needs ~$100K down; payback 3-4 years if maintenance and backup attach rates hold. Avoid paying waterproofing-platform multiples for a pump-only business.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Days 1-30: map flood-prone basement ZIPs, review competitors, request basement/drain franchise FDDs, talk to insurance/restoration sources. Days 31-60: operator/lead tech search, verify lead sources, job margins, warranty callbacks, seasonality. Days 61-90: LOI only if scope expands beyond pump replacement into recurring/backup systems.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Pump-only revenue with no maintenance/backups
- Seasonality creates cash crunch
- High warranty/callback rate
- Lead costs hidden in owner referrals
- SFL-only thesis without basement demand
