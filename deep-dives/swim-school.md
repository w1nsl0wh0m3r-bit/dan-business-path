# Swim School — Deep Dive

*Generated: 2026-05-25 ET | Rank: #14 | Blended Score: 3.70 | Confidence: Medium*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **CONDITIONAL GO / Franchise preferred over tiny acquisition**

Swim schools have a legitimately attractive consumer proposition: safety-driven demand, recurring lessons, strong parent willingness to pay, and brandable local experience. Dan’s PE/consumer pattern recognition fits. But the economics are highly site-dependent: pool buildout, rent, instructor labor, utilization by lane/hour, and local demographic density.

**Verdict:** pursue as a franchise or acquisition only in wealthy family-heavy suburbs with proven utilization. Do not buy a subscale owner-instructor swim school and assume a manager can run it profitably.

## 6. Operator Economics

A mature $1.5M revenue school at 18% EBITDA = $270K. A strong GM costs $80K-$120K; add regional support/owner oversight and true passive cash flow may be **$130K-$180K pre-debt**. For new builds, debt service and ramp losses can wipe out early cash flow. This is not a low-capital first business unless using host-pool model.

## 7. Acquisition Targets

**Look for:**
- 70%+ prime-time lane utilization
- Waitlist by age cohort
- Instructor retention and training system
- Parent NPS/reviews
- Renewal/retention metrics
- Facility lease assignability and pool maintenance history

**Multiples:** strong recurring consumer schools can price above generic local services; stay disciplined at 3x-4x SDE unless there is multi-unit expansion proof.
