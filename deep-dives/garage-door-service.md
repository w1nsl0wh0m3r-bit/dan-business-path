# Garage Door Service — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Category rank: 21 | Blended score: 3.55*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
Garage door service is a good “boring local services” candidate **if acquired**, not started greenfield. Demand is recurring-ish through repair/emergency work, tickets are high enough to support paid search, and the work is easier to technicianize than many trades. The problem: lead-gen competition is brutal, private-equity-backed consolidators and franchises are already sophisticated, and a subscale single-territory franchise can become a paid-search treadmill.

**Dan-specific take:** attractive if he can buy a 3+ truck New England operator with dispatcher, Google Business Profile history, and low owner-dependence at **2.5-3.5x SDE**. Avoid if revenue is mostly installation/new construction or if “marketing” equals Angi/HomeAdvisor.

## 2. Market Sizing
- U.S. garage door installation/repair is a niche within building equipment/home services; IBISWorld maintains a dedicated “Garage Door Installation & Repair” industry report, which is the right paid source for exact national revenue and establishment count ([IBISWorld search/industry catalog](https://www.ibisworld.com/)).
- The broader home-improvement repair backdrop is favorable: U.S. housing stock is aging, garage doors have safety-critical moving parts, and emergency replacement/repair is less deferrable than cosmetic remodeling.
- New England is structurally decent: older housing, winter freeze/thaw damage, and high labor rates. But density matters; rural territories kill routing efficiency.

**Working market estimate:** a local 1M-population metro can support dozens of independent garage door firms; a competent 4-8 truck operator can plausibly reach **$1.5-4.0M revenue** without multi-state expansion.

## 6. Operator Economics
This can be manager-run, but the first hire needs to be a **service GM/dispatcher hybrid**, not just an office admin. Budget **$80-110K base + bonus** in New England. A $250K SDE shop can become a $120-170K passive-ish cash-flow asset after replacing owner labor. Dan should underwrite to **no more than 50-65% of stated SDE** after operator replacement unless the seller already has a GM.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
**Look for:** 3-8 trucks, >5-year Google Business Profile, >4.7 rating, revenue split repair/replacement >70%, no customer concentration, CRM/job costing, in-house techs, clean inventory, phone recordings, seller willing to transition 6 months.

**Avoid:** new-construction exposure, subcontractor-only labor, paid lead dependency >35% of revenue, undocumented cash sales, warranty liabilities, spouse doing free dispatch/bookkeeping.

Listings will likely show at **2.5-4.0x SDE**. Pay the high end only for manager-in-place and diversified organic lead flow.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- Paid leads/marketplaces >35% revenue.
- Owner personally sells/closes >50% of jobs.
- No CRM, no call tracking, no technician-level profitability.
- Install/new construction >40% revenue.
- Required purchase price >4x SDE for a single-location shop.
