# Estate / Property Management — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium*

## Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
Standard residential property management is mediocre unless bought cheap with real staff and contracts. HNW second-home / estate management in New England is more interesting: relationship-driven, sticky, premium, and operationally local. Dan should pursue estate management only if he can acquire reputation/customer relationships, not start cold.

## Market Sizing
- IBISWorld search result cites U.S. Property Management market size of $136.9B in 2024, 330,395 businesses, and low concentration, under NAICS 53131 ([IBISWorld market-size page referenced in web search](https://www.ibisworld.com/united-states/market-size/property-management/1356/)).
- New England estate-management SAM is smaller and localized: Nantucket, Martha's Vineyard, Cape Cod, Newport, coastal Maine, Fairfield County, ski markets, Boston suburbs.
- Estate management is not cleanly tracked as a NAICS category; treat market size as estimate based on second-home density and HNW household concentration.

## Unit Economics
- Standard residential PM revenue: 8%-10% of collected rent plus leasing, renewal, maintenance coordination, inspection, and project fees.
- Estate management revenue: monthly retainers, seasonal opening/closing, vendor coordination, emergency response, project oversight, concierge, security checks; pricing can be materially higher than standard PM but is relationship-specific.
- Valuation: web search summary found property management SDE multiples commonly around 2.5x-3.0x; BizBuySell sold-data range cited 1.99x-3.23x with average ~2.70x; premium relationship firms can trade higher but Dan should underwrite 2.5x-3.5x, not platform multiples.
- Example target: $900K revenue, $300K SDE at 3.0x = $900K EV. Hire/retain GM at $90K-$120K; manager-adjusted cash flow $180K-$210K before debt.
- Gross margin: service coordination model can be asset-light; labor-heavy concierge/maintenance reduces margin but increases control.
- Payback: attractive only if bought <=3.0x SDE and seller/client retention is strong. Otherwise cash yield disappears after operator salary.

## Competitive Landscape
- Vacasa / Evolve / AvantStay — vacation rental management, not direct estate-management comps but compete for second-home services.
- Local Cape/Nantucket/Newport estate managers — strongest incumbents; brand is personal trust.
- Traditional property managers using AppFolio/Buildium/Yardi.
- Facilities maintenance / home-watch companies.
- Family offices and personal assistants for ultra-HNW homes.

## Franchise Comp Table
Franchise path is not recommended. Property-management franchises exist, but HNW estate management depends on local reputation and trust. Royalty drag is hard to justify.

## Operator Economics
This is operator-led by necessity. Clients expect responsiveness, discretion, vendor judgment, and emergency handling. A GM/estate manager is not optional; Dan's role would be acquisition, finance, SOPs, vendor network, and add-on services. A strong operator can manage 20-50 high-touch homes depending on service scope; a weak one creates immediate churn.

## Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- Owner retiring with 25-100 HNW homes under management.
- Written management agreements assignable on sale.
- Vendor network documented.
- Low client concentration or at least long-tenured top clients.
- Recurring retainer revenue >60% of revenue.
- Opportunity to add maintenance/project-management margin.
Deal sources: local brokers, BizBuySell/BizQuest, direct outreach to “home watch,” “estate management,” “property caretaker,” “second home management” companies in New England.

## Dan Fit — 3.8/5
Good but fragile. Dan's PE background helps with contracts, add-on acquisitions, pricing, and professionalization. The risk is that goodwill is personal and churn can spike after founder exit. This is a relationship business masquerading as a recurring-revenue business.

## 90-Day Roadmap
1. Map top 100 estate/home-watch/property-care firms in New England wealth corridors.
2. Call local realtors, luxury brokers, estate attorneys, and insurance agents to identify trusted operators.
3. Speak with 10 owners; look for succession issues.
4. Build diligence checklist: contract assignability, client tenure, churn, revenue by client, vendor margin, emergency obligations.
5. Mystery-shop premium second-home management offerings.
6. Underwrite operator-led model with one GM + admin + vendor network.

## Kill Criteria
- No written assignable contracts.
- Founder is the sole relationship holder and clients can leave instantly.
- Revenue mostly one-off projects, not recurring retainers.
- Top 5 clients >40% revenue.
- Gross margin depends on underpaid owner labor.
- Requires 24/7 owner responsiveness with no dispatch process.
