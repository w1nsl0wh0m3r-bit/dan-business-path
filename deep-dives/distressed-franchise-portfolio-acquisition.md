# Distressed Franchise Portfolio Acquisition — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Attractive only as Avenue 1: partner with a real operator and buy cash-flowing units at distressed-but-not-broken prices. Avoid turnaround fantasies; this works when distress is seller/capital-structure driven, not brand/unit-economic rot.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
Acquire 2-10 existing franchise units from stressed owners, lenders, or undercapitalized franchisees. Customers are the end-market buyers of the acquired concept; Dan’s customer is effectively the franchisor/lender/seller needing a clean successor. Demand driver is franchisor pressure to keep units open and transfer weak operators; NE/NY/NJ/CT has dense franchise territory supply. Market size is an estimate because no clean public market exists; IFA says franchising spans 806k+ U.S. establishments and $893.9B output in 2024, creating a huge pool of resale candidates. Source: https://www.franchise.org/franchise-information/franchise-business-outlook

| Case | Revenue | SDE | SDE Margin | Entry Price | Multiple | Cash-on-Cash Year 1 |

| Bear | $1.5M | $150K | 10% | $525K | 3.5x | negative to 10% |

| Bull | $5.0M | $750K | 15% | $3.2M | 4.25x | 35%-60% |

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — do not penalize lack of direct experience

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $3.0M base target; avoid subscale unless cheap.
- SDE range: $200K-$500K+ preferred; normalize for paid manager/operator.
- Years in business: 3+ years with tax returns, POS/CRM, lease/territory control.
- Route density / customer count: dense local territory; recurring or repeat customers documented.
- Key assets to verify: tax returns, bank deposits, customer cohorts, leases/territories, equipment, licenses, staff retention.
- Red flags to avoid: seller-as-business, unverifiable add-backs, declining traffic, capex cliff, regulatory ambiguity, franchisor transfer issues.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
Targets are resale portfolios, not new franchises. Screen Neighborly, Authority Brands, Premium Service Brands, Ace Handyman, Mathnasium/Kumon, Dunkin/sub shops only if FDD Item 19 and store-level P&Ls are clean. Franchise fees often $40K-$75K and royalties commonly 5%-8%; verify current FDDs at https://www.franchimp.com/ and state FDD repositories.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
Strategic franchisees, multi-unit operators, franchisor workout teams, lenders/SBA special assets, private buyer groups, Unbridled Capital, FRANdata/Franchise Times deal intermediaries, and PE-backed platforms such as Neighborly/Authority Brands/Empower Brands. Fragmentation is high below 10 units. Moat is franchisor approval, operator bench, local density, and clean transfer execution.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase where possible, seller note/earnout for transition risk, working-capital peg, non-compete and training agreement.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: SBA 7(a): eligible for changes of ownership up to $5M; source: https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans/7a-loans
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus diligence/legal and working capital reserve.
- Payback period at base case: usually 2.5-5 years if bought at 3.5-4.0x verified SDE and manager cost is real.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: underwrite ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; do not assume institutional roll-up multiples.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping in Boston/NE first; scrape brokers/franchise resale listings; request FDDs where applicable; build source-of-truth comp table.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator partner profile; review 5-10 actual P&Ls; site visits/customer mystery shops; build 3-case model.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Pick LOI target or franchise signing decision; negotiate seller note/training; run legal/regulatory review before exclusivity.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- No clean tax-return support for SDE or revenue.
- Paid-manager model fails debt service.
- Customer demand is fad-driven with weak repeat behavior.
- Transfer requires franchisor/landlord consent on unattractive terms.
- Required capex exceeds modeled reserve or creates >5-year payback.
- Legal/compliance structure is unclear or dependent on aggressive interpretation.
