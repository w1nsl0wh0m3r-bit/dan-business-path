# Pool Leak Detection / Repair — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
GO: this is a strong Dan-scale route/service acquisition in SFL and affluent suburbs — recurring pain, technical equipment, fragmented operators and credible 3-4.5x SDE entry points.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A specialty service finding and repairing swimming pool leaks using pressure testing, dye tests, electronic listening equipment, diver inspection and light structural/plumbing repair.
- Who are the customers? Residential pool owners, HOAs, property managers, hotels/clubs and pool-service companies that outsource leak work.
- Demand drivers: aging pool stock, water bills, plaster/deck failures, insurance/home sale issues, SFL pool density and recurring referrals from pool maintenance routes.
- Geographic relevance: Best in South Florida; also relevant in affluent NY/CT/NJ/NE seasonal pool markets with higher seasonality.
- Market size: PHTA reports 10.7M+ residential pools/spas in the US; pool service/repair is a large fragmented trade, but leak detection TAM should be modeled locally by installed pool base.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $600K-$2.0M with 2-5 crews/techs and documented repair mix.
- SDE range: $200K-$600K normalized manager-run SDE; below that is too owner-job-like.
- Years in business: 4+ years, with at least 3 years of tax returns/POS data.
- Route density / customer count: 3+ jobs/day per crew in pool-dense counties; 25+ active pool-service/property-manager referral partners.
- Key assets to verify: leak detection equipment, pressure rigs, vehicles, repair licenses/subs, call logs, referral sources, reviews.
- Red flags to avoid: all technical knowledge held by seller, unlicensed structural/plumbing work, seasonal NE revenue with no winter plan, no job costing.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: American Leak Detection (franchise fee/royalty per FDD), LeakTronics dealer/training ecosystem, Red Rhino Leak Detection as brand analog; verify current FDD/territory economics.
- FDD highlight: Item 19/job volume and territory size matter; equipment/training in Item 7 is central.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only; if Item 19 is absent, validate with franchisee calls and treat franchisor examples as marketing.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top 5-10 competitors or roll-ups active in this space: American Leak Detection, Red Rhino Leak Detection, Leak Science, Pinch A Penny/service departments, Leslie’s, local pool repair contractors.
- PE activity: Pool service has active consolidation (e.g., National Pool Partners, Pool Troopers); leak detection remains more technical and fragmented.
- Fragmentation level: High, with many local specialists and pool-maintenance companies that refer out complex leaks.
- Moat: trained technicians, equipment, response time, referral relationships, review density and repair capability beyond detection.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to retention; inventory/equipment and lease assignments diligenced separately.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if tax returns, lease/licensing and cash flow are clean; underwrite 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $75K-$250K plus working capital for a $750K-$2.0M entry price.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on manager-run SDE; walk above 5 years.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned by professionalizing reporting, de-risking staff/customer concentration and adding repeat revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build NE/NY/SFL competitor and pricing grid.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits; request tax returns, POS/customer cohorts, lease/equipment schedules and labor roster.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if manager-run SDE, debt service coverage and local whitespace survive diligence.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Manager-run SDE below $175K after replacing owner labor.
- Asking price above 4.5x verified SDE without exceptional recurring revenue.
- Seller cannot reconcile P&L to tax returns/POS/bank statements.
- Lease, license, medical/regulatory, insurance or equipment dependency cannot transfer cleanly.
- Seller cannot show repeatable tech training or more than one person capable of accurate leak diagnosis.
