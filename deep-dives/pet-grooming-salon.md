*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: High for market/franchise data; Medium for local acquisition multiples.*

## 1. Executive Verdict — GO FOR ACQUISITION, CONDITIONAL FOR FRANCHISE
Pet grooming is a better acquisition thesis than greenfield franchise thesis for Dan. Demand is durable, capacity is constrained by skilled groomer supply, and good shops have repeat behavior. But traditional salons are labor-constrained and often owner-groomer dependent. Dan should buy a shop with non-owner groomers and a waiting list, then professionalize scheduling, pricing, membership bath plans, and add-on retail.

## 2. Market Sizing

## 3. Unit Economics
**Independent salon:**
- Revenue: $300K-$1.0M depending on groomer count and pricing.
- Groomer pay: often 40-55% commission or equivalent hourly/productivity comp.
- Gross margin after direct labor: 40-55%.
- SDE: $75K-$250K common for small local shops; $250K+ requires multi-groomer scale and owner not doing all production.
- Entry multiple: 2.0-3.0x SDE; 3.0-3.5x for clean books, manager, and low owner dependency.
- Payback: 2.5-4 years if bought well and no groomer loss.

**Franchise comps:**
- Woof Gang Bakery & Grooming search summaries cite 2025 FDD investment of **$184,420-$506,620**, **$49,900** franchise fee, **7% royalty**, **2% marketing**, and FY2024 average gross revenue of **$640,601** across 161 full-year locations.
- Scenthound FDD data: investment **$328K-$550K**, AUV **$452.7K**, and 6% royalty plus marketing obligations ([Franchise Chatter](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2025/06/27/scenthound-franchise-review-2025-costs-fees-news-average-revenues-and-or-profits/)).

## 6. Operator Economics
A manager-run salon needs either a shop manager or lead groomer paid $55K-$85K plus incentives. The real scarce asset is groomers, not customers. Dan’s cash flow after operator is attractive only if the business has at least 3-4 productive groomers and owner is not the main production engine. Example: $650K revenue x 18% EBITDA before GM = $117K; after $70K manager leaves $47K. That is not enough unless there is expansion runway. At $900K revenue and 22% EBITDA before GM, post-GM cash flow can reach ~$125K.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- 3+ groomers plus bathers; owner does <25% of grooming hours.
- Booking backlog of 2+ weeks.
- Pricing below market with room for 10-20% uplift.
- Customer database with SMS/email and rebooking behavior.
- Lease assignability and landlord consent.
Avoid shops where “SDE” is really owner grooming wages.

Deal sources: BizBuySell, local business brokers, grooming schools, supplier reps, Facebook groomer groups, retiring owner outreach.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- Owner is top groomer and >35% of revenue walks out with them.
- Groomers are 1099 when economics depend on employee control; legal reclassification risk.
- No customer list/rebooking data.
- EBITDA after fair manager and fair owner replacement wage below $75K.
- Lease renewal uncertain within 24 months.
