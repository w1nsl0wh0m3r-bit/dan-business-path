# Dirty Soda / Beverage Bar — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
CONDITIONAL — viral TikTok-driven beverage category with real unit economics but unproven longevity outside Mountain West / LDS-adjacent markets. Underwrite franchise resales at 3.0-4.0x SDE; independent builds only in high-foot-traffic NE/NY/SFL locations with $400K+ SDE proof.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- **What is this business?** A drive-through or walk-up beverage shop selling custom "dirty sodas" — branded sodas (Coke, Dr Pepper, Mountain Dew) mixed with flavored syrups, creams, coconut cream, fruit purées, and lime. Limited food menu (cookies, pretzel bites). High-ticket impulse beverage, $5-$8 average drink.
- **Who are the customers?** B2C — teens, young adults, families, social-media-driven consumers. Heavy skew toward suburban women 18-35. LDS communities historically core but expanding to mainstream via TikTok/Hulu virality.
- **Demand drivers:** Social media virality (#dirtysoda hit 700K+ TikTok videos by April 2022), drive-thru convenience, low-ticket treat economy ($5-$8 vs $8-$12 coffee alternatives), alcohol-free social beverage positioning. Olivia Rodrigo viral moment (Dec 2021) + Hulu's "Secret Lives of Mormon Wives" (2024) drove national awareness.
- **Geographic relevance:** Originated Utah/Mountain West. Swig now in 16 states, targeting FL, TX, Carolinas, TN, AR, MO. Independent dirty soda bars viable in NE/NY suburbs and SFL where drive-thru beverage culture exists but soda shops are novel. Boston proper less natural fit — limited drive-thru infrastructure. Suburban CT/NJ/Long Island viable.
- **Market size:** No clean NAICS code. Estimate: dirty soda specialty shops generate ~$500M-$800M US revenue (estimate based on Swig 164 locations × ~$800K AUV + Sodalicious, Fiiz, Thirst, Quench It! independents). Custom beverage market (boba, smoothie, specialty coffee, dirty soda) is $15B+ US (IBISWorld estimate).

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- **Swig** — 164 locations (April 2026), majority owned by Larry H. Miller Company, PE-backed (Savory Fund minority). Aggressive national expansion plan (500 stores in 5-7 years). Drive-thru model.
- **Sodalicious** — Utah-based competitor, ~30 locations, privately held. Not currently franchising aggressively.
- **Fiiz Drinks** — ~70 locations, franchising model, Western US focus. Lower cost entry than Swig.
- **Sonic Drive-In** — Added dirty soda customization nationally in 2024. This is the real competitive threat — Sonic has 3,500+ locations and can mainstream the product at scale.
- **Independents** — Low barriers to entry. Anyone can open a soda bar with $50K-$100K in equipment. Fragmented and growing fast in UT, ID, AZ, TX.
- **PE activity:** Savory Fund and LHMCO backing Swig signals institutional interest, but no major roll-up platform yet.
- **Moat:** Brand (Swig has it), drive-thru real estate, social media following, proprietary recipes. Weaker moat than coffee — any QSR or convenience store can copy the drink. Differentiation is brand + experience + location.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: Asset purchase for independents; franchise transfer for branded locations. Seller note common (20-30%).
- SBA eligibility: Yes for profitable franchise or independent operations with 2+ years of tax returns.
- Down payment at Dan's scale: $50K-$110K (10% of $500K-$1.1M entry).
- Payback period at base case: 3-4 years.
- Multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out if you build a 2-3 unit platform and sell to a regional consolidator.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- **Sonic / national QSR mainstreaming kills the specialty play.** If Sonic or Starbucks scales dirty soda nationally, independent shops lose their differentiation. Monitor Sonic menu commitment.
- **Category is a fad, not a category.** TikTok virality does not guarantee 5-year demand. If same-store sales decline >10% YoY at Swig, the trend is fading.
- **NE drive-thru scarcity.** Most New England towns restrict drive-thru construction. If you can't get drive-thru permits, the model breaks — walk-up dirty soda has much lower throughput.
- **SDE below $120K after manager.** If the best available location or franchise territory can't produce $120K+ SDE with a paid GM, it's a job, not an investment.
- **Brand dilution from over-franchising.** If Swig signs 500 franchise agreements too fast, territory protection weakens and unit-level economics compress.
