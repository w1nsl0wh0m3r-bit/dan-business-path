# State inspection / emissions station — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: NO-GO
Do not prioritize at Dan's acquisition scale. The decision hinges on transferable cash flow, a real operator/foreman layer, and whether the category has repeat/contract revenue rather than just owner heroics.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? State inspection / emissions station is a local service business where demand is generated by convenience, compliance, repair urgency, or home upkeep. The attractive version is systematized enough for an operator partner to run day-to-day.
- Who are the customers? Mix of B2C households and B2B/commercial accounts depending on category; the best targets have some repeat commercial/property-manager/fleet component.
- Demand drivers: aging assets, convenience expectations, regulatory requirements, weather exposure, and high-trust local reputation.
- Geographic relevance: Only relevant in inspection states: MA and NY are relevant; Florida is a hard no because statewide emissions testing was eliminated.
- Market size: Market size is regulated fee times vehicle volume, not TAM fantasy. MA charges $35 for annual safety/emissions inspection; NY fees vary by region/test type, with NYMA OBD II emissions at $27 plus safety inspection.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: see buybox below; generally $500K-$4M revenue depending on ticket size.
- SDE range: $100K-$500K, with $200K-$500K preferred.
- Years in business: 3-5+ years minimum with reviews and repeat sources.
- Route density / customer count: enough density that a paid manager/dispatcher improves utilization rather than adds dead overhead.
- Key assets to verify: tax returns, job-level gross margin, CRM/call logs, reviews, licenses, insurance, equipment condition, employee/subcontractor retention.
- Red flags to avoid: owner-only technical delivery, unverifiable cash, poor reviews, unsafe/licensing gaps, and revenue that disappears when seller exits.

Only buy as an auto repair shop with inspection as lead-gen, not as a standalone lane. Target $700K-$1.5M revenue, $125K-$300K SDE, 3+ licensed inspectors, visible road frontage, documented failure-to-repair conversion. Avoid regulatory concentration and equipment obsolescence.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: Few pure inspection franchises. Better comps are Midas, Meineke, AAMCO, Christian Brothers, Grease Monkey/Valvoline-type service centers where inspection is a traffic source. Fees/royalties vary; request FDDs and verify Item 19 by state.
- FDD highlight: request the current FDD, focus on Item 7 investment, Item 19 financials, Item 20 unit closures/transfers, and required local marketing spend.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use FDD data only as a starting point; normalize for local labor, ad costs, ramp time, and manager salary.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
Local licensed inspection stations, dealerships, quick-lube chains, Midas/Meineke, independent repair shops. PE interest exists in broader auto service (Driven Brands, FullSpeed, Mavis/Express Oil style platforms), not pure inspection lanes. Moat is license, location and repair conversion.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, 10%-20% seller note when possible, earnout only for retained customers/contracts/backlog, working-capital peg for inventory/AR-heavy models.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA 7(a)-financeable if legal, licensed, insurable and cash-flowing; model 10-year amortization at ~11% with 10% down.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $35K-$225K for $350K-$2.25M entry prices.
- Payback period at base case: often 2-4 years before growth, but only if the operator salary is already burdened or realistically added.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; do not underwrite sponsor-level exits.

If pursued, structure as asset purchase of repair shop with inspection license continuity as closing condition. Base $100K SDE at 3.5x = $350K price, $35K down, ~$60K annual SBA debt service; thin cushion unless repair gross profit is real.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map 50 local operators across Boston/NE, NY/CT/NJ and SFL; pull reviews, licenses, franchise FDDs where relevant, broker listings, and owner role indicators.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview 3-5 operators/franchisees, benchmark job-level margins, identify operator-partner profile, and build an SBA downside model with paid manager burden.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one disciplined IOI/LOI only if tax-return SDE, customer transferability, and operator coverage are clean; otherwise archive the category.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Standalone inspection lane with <$100K SDE
- Florida market exposure
- State fee caps leave no pricing power
- No repair conversion data
- License transfer or inspector staffing uncertain
