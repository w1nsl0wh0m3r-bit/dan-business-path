# Pilates / Barre Boutique Fitness — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Strong fit for Avenue 2: membership-based, affluent female customer base, manager-operable with operator partner, and franchise templates exist; diligence must stress-test churn and instructor dependency.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A local operator selling specialized recurring service in pilates / barre boutique fitness. Revenue comes from service visits, memberships/contracts, product/equipment add-ons, and selective one-time projects.
- Who are the customers? Primarily affluent B2C households or consumers, with some B2B/referral demand from builders, brokers, physicians, landlords or wellness partners depending on category.
- Demand drivers: recurring maintenance, habit/membership behavior, premiumization, aging housing stock, affluent local density, and convenience.
- Geographic relevance: Boston/NE and NY/CT/NJ first; South Florida works where wealth density and seasonal-home demand are high.
- Market size: use cited sector proxies below; local TAM must be built bottoms-up by households, units, visit frequency and ticket.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $450K-$900K; SDE range: $110K-$240K; clean tax returns and POS/CRM data.
- Years in business: 5+ years, transferable manager or operator partner available.
- Route density / customer count: concentrated ZIPs; repeat customer base; low owner-key-person risk.
- Key assets to verify: lease/territory, customer list, reviews, staff retention, equipment condition, source of leads.
- Red flags to avoid: owner as sole rainmaker, underdocumented cash, aggressive add-backs, weak retention, lease cliff, paid-search dependence.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top brands/benchmarks: Club Pilates, Pure Barre, BODYBAR Pilates, JETSET Pilates, Barre3, The Bar Method, Solidcore, CorePower Yoga.
- FDD highlight: request Items 5-7, 19 and 20; verify royalties, closure rates, transfer economics, required local marketing and technology fees.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only when disclosed; otherwise validate with franchisee calls.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors/roll-ups active in this space: Club Pilates, Pure Barre, BODYBAR Pilates, JETSET Pilates, Barre3, The Bar Method, Solidcore, CorePower Yoga.
- PE activity: sponsor interest is highest in medspa/beauty, fitness franchisors and restaurant platforms; local independents remain fragmented.
- Fragmentation level: high; most local markets include a few chains plus many owner-operated independents.
- Moat: memberships/contracts, reviews, technician/instructor/provider retention, local referral networks, certification/compliance and operational throughput.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for retention; avoid stock deals unless liabilities are diligence-clean.
- SBA eligibility and terms: 7(a) can finance goodwill-heavy service/restaurant deals if cash flow supports debt.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10-20% of enterprise value plus working capital.
- Payback period at base case: 2.5-4.5 years if seller note and SBA sizing are disciplined.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy at ~3-4x SDE, build systems/manager bench, exit around ~4x if de-risked.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map top 30 local competitors, scrape reviews, request FDDs where relevant, interview 3 operators.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build market model, identify operator partner, review broker listings and validate margins.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one disciplined LOI or pass; for franchise route, complete FDD/franchisee validation.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- No clean financials or customer-level retention data.
- Required owner involvement exceeds 20 hours/week after transition.
- Debt service coverage below 1.25x after manager salary.
- Rent/royalty/labor structure leaves no margin for error.
- Negative reviews point to service quality, staff churn or compliance issues.
