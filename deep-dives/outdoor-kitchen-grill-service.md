## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL, INSTALL-TO-MAINTENANCE FUNNEL PLAY
Outdoor kitchen and grill service sits at a sweet spot: high-ticket installs ($5K-$50K+) feed a growing maintenance/replacement revenue stream. The category benefits from the pandemic-era outdoor living boom (patio kitchens, built-in grills, fire pits) that created a large installed base needing ongoing service. Dan should acquire an established outdoor kitchen installer with a maintenance book, not start from scratch. The path is install-to-maintain — use high-margin project work to build a recurring service annuity.

**Dan-specific take:** This is a **strong operator-partner play** in SFL and coastal NE, where outdoor kitchens are ubiquitous in affluent ZIPs. Dan's PE diligence applies: recurring maintenance contract quality, install pipeline visibility, labor productivity, and referral channel economics. The installer needs trade skills (gas plumbing, electrical, masonry) that Dan doesn't have — operator partner is essential.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

**Operator types:**
- Specialty outdoor kitchen installers (3-15 employees) — primary targets
- Landscape contractors with outdoor kitchen divisions
- Pool builders who add outdoor kitchens as upsell
- Big-box (Home Depot, Lowe's) referral networks with independent installers
- Mobile grill repair services (independent operators)

**PE / M&A activity:**
- **Limited direct PE** — category too small and local for traditional PE
- **Adjacent:** pool company roll-ups (Fluidra, POOLCORP) occasionally absorb outdoor living
- **Middleby Corporation** — owns Lynx, Viking, TurboChef; equipment manufacturer, not service
- **Weber-Stephen Products** — grill manufacturer; acquired by BDT Capital Partners
- Most transactions are owner-to-owner at Dan's scale

**Fragmentation:** Very fragmented. Most markets have 5-20 outdoor kitchen specialists plus landscape/pool cross-sell. No national service brand.

**Moat:** Trade skills (gas, electrical, masonry), supplier relationships (cabinet and grill brands), referral network (landscape architects, designers, pool builders), and geographic route density for service calls.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- **SBA eligible:** Yes — specialty contractor with 2+ years history
- **Typical structure:** Asset sale (equipment, client list, contracts, goodwill). Verify license transferability.
- **Multiple range:** 2.5-4x SDE. Higher end for businesses with >35% recurring service revenue.
- **Capital discipline:** Verify maintenance contract retention rate by calling 20+ clients. Separate install revenue from service revenue — installs are project-based and volatile. Value the service book separately. Deduct owner's project management labor at $75-90K/yr.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Maintenance/recurring revenue below 20% of total (install-only = project business, not investment)
- Install pipeline cannot cover fixed costs in off-season
- Gas/electrical licensing not transferable to new operator
- Owner performs >60% of install project management (non-delegable)
- Supplier/brand dealer agreements non-transferable
- Revenue concentrated in >5 customers (residential referral dependency)
- Service territory has <5,000 installed outdoor kitchens (insufficient addressable maintenance market)
- Insurance costs (general liability, workers comp) exceed 8% of revenue
