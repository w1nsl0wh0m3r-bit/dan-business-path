# Premium Pet Retail / Food / Supplements — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Attractive consumer category with durable pet-humanization tailwinds, but pure retail is margin-thin unless the store owns local trust, subscriptions, grooming/wash adjacencies or a private-label wedge. Buy a profitable neighborhood institution, not inventory plus vibes.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- Specialty pet stores selling premium food, treats, supplements, toys, grooming products and sometimes self-wash/grooming services.
- Customers are B2C pet owners; B2B is minor except breeders, trainers and local rescue partnerships.
- Demand drivers: APPA reports U.S. pet spending above $150B; owners continue trading up in food, health and wellness.
- Geographic relevance: strong in affluent NE/NY/SFL urban/suburban neighborhoods with high dog ownership and walkability.
- Market size: APPA is the best directional source for U.S. pet spending; local SAM should be built by households with pets and specialty-food penetration.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1.2M-$3.0M, stable same-store sales, defensible neighborhood.
- SDE range: $200K-$450K after paid store manager.
- Years in business: 5+ years with transferable lease and vendor relationships.
- Route density / customer count: 3,000+ active loyalty customers; top 100 customers not overly concentrated.
- Key assets to verify: POS SKU margins, inventory aging, lease terms, loyalty database, subscription/delivery book, vendor rebates, reviews.
- Red flags to avoid: Amazon/Chewy price wars on core SKUs, stale inventory, no manager layer, landlord reset, revenue inflated by pandemic cohort.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Pet Supplies Plus, Woof Gang Bakery & Grooming, EarthWise Pet, Pet Wants, Hollywood Feed-style regional chain/franchise.
- FDD focus: Item 19 gross sales, rent buildout, inventory opening package, royalty/marketing fees and grooming labor model.
- Franchise fees often ~$40K-$60K with royalties around 5%-6%; verify current FDD.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: Petco, PetSmart, Chewy, Amazon, Pet Supplies Plus, Hollywood Feed, local independents, vet clinics and DTC supplement brands.
- PE activity: Pet retail and services have seen strategic/PE interest, especially grooming/boarding/vet adjacency; food retail alone is less prized.
- Fragmentation: independents still meaningful in premium neighborhoods.
- Moat: local trust, staff advice, curated assortment, grooming/wash traffic, loyalty data, same-day convenience.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal: asset purchase with inventory counted separately at cost/discount, seller note, lease assignment contingency.
- SBA eligibility: generally yes; lender will focus on rent coverage and inventory quality.
- Down payment required: ~10%-15% plus inventory and working-capital cushion.
- Payback period at base case: 3.5-5 years unless services/private label lift margins.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy 3.0-3.75x; exit 3.5-4.0x if recurring/subscription and manager layer are real.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map affluent pet ZIPs; scrape independents; request FDDs; benchmark rent and Chewy competition.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Review POS cohorts, SKU margin, inventory aging and lease; test private-label/grooming upside.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI only if paid-manager EBITDA and lease assignment support debt service.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Gross margin below 35% without clear mix-shift path.
- Inventory aging/shrink is uncontrolled.
- Lease renewal resets economics.
- No loyalty/subscription data and weak repeat frequency.
- Business depends on owner as community personality.
