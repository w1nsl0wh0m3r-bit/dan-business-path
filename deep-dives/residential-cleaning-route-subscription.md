# Residential Cleaning Route / Subscription — Deep Dive Investment Memo
*Generated: 2026-05-26 | Category rank: ~60 | UW Score: 3.20*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL, BUY ROUTE DENSITY NOT BRAND
Residential cleaning is one of the largest and most fragmented home-service categories in the US. The economics work when you own recurring weekly/biweekly subscription customers routed tightly by geography. The category fails when you buy a brand or franchise premium without the route density to support it. Dan should acquire a mature, manager-run cleaning route with 200+ recurring clients, not build from scratch.

**Dan-specific take:** This is a **classic operator-partner play**. Dan's PE diligence edge applies directly: recurring revenue quality, churn analysis, labor cost benchmarking, and route density mapping. The key risk is labor — turnover in cleaning runs 50-100% annually and is the primary margin killer.

## 2. Market Sizing
- US residential cleaning services market estimated at **$12-20B** (IBISWorld, Statista estimates for maid/housekeeping services). Highly fragmented — no single company holds >1% national share.
- ~890,000 maids and housekeeping cleaners employed in the US per BLS (Occupational Outlook Handbook, 2024).
- Demand drivers: dual-income households, aging population, Airbnb/STR turnover cleaning, post-COVID hygiene awareness.
- Recurring subscription models (weekly, biweekly) generate 60-75% of revenue for established route operators.

## 3. Unit Economics
| Metric | Range | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Average ticket (per cleaning) | $120-$250 | Biweekly 2BR/2BA standard; deep cleans $300-$500+ |
| Revenue per cleaner/day | $500-$800 | 3-5 homes/day at route density |
| Gross margin | 50-65% | Labor is 35-50% of revenue |
| SDE margin | 15-25% | After paying cleaners, supplies, dispatch, insurance |
| Recurring revenue % | 60-75% | Weekly/biweekly subscriptions; remainder one-time deep cleans |
| Customer churn (annual) | 20-35% | Higher in price-sensitive segments |
| Cleaner turnover (annual) | 50-100% | The #1 operational risk |
| Entry multiple | 2-3.5x SDE | Route quality drives premium; 2x for loose books, 3.5x for tight subscription list |
| Payback | 3-4 years | At 3x SDE with 25% down |

**Example:** 250 recurring biweekly clients × $160 avg ticket × 26 cleanings/yr = $1.04M revenue. At 20% SDE margin = $208K SDE. Entry at 3x = $624K. SBA 7(a) with 10% down = $62K cash. Debt service ~$85K/yr. Cash-on-cash year 1: ($208K - $85K) / $62K = **198%**.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox

**Acquisition target criteria:**
- Revenue: $400K-$1.2M
- SDE: $100K-$300K
- Recurring clients: 200+ with documented service history
- Route density: clients clustered within 15-min drive radius
- Manager or lead cleaner who can run day-to-day
- CRM/scheduling system in place (Jobber, ZenMaid, Launch27, or similar)
- 3+ years operating history with tax returns

## 5. Competitive Landscape

**National platforms/franchises:** Molly Maid (Neighborly portfolio), The Maids, MaidPro, Two Maids & A Mop, CottageCare, Homeaglow (tech-enabled marketplace), Handy/ANGI Homeservices.

**PE / roll-up activity:**
- **Neighborly** (owned by Harvest Partners / KKR-backed) — owns Molly Maid and 30+ home service brands
- **Molly Maid** franchisees occasionally consolidate territories
- **Flywheel Brands** and similar home-service holding companies
- Fragmented PE activity — no dominant national roll-up yet

**Fragmentation:** 90%+ of the market is independent local operators with <5 employees. This is a buy-and-build dream category IF you can solve labor.

**Moat:** Route density + recurring subscription relationships + local reputation. Low capital barriers mean low moat individually, but a 250-client recurring book is hard to replicate quickly.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- **SBA 7(a) eligible:** Yes — cleaning services are standard SBA borrowers
- **Typical structure:** Asset sale, seller note 10-20%, SBA 7(a) 80-90%
- **Multiple range:** 2-3.5x SDE for independent routes; franchises sometimes trade at 3-4x but with royalty drag
- **Down payment at Dan's scale ($200K SDE):** $60-70K at 3x with 10% down
- **Capital discipline:** Never pay >3.5x SDE. Verify recurring revenue by calling 20+ clients. Deduct owner labor at market rate ($55-65K) before calculating true SDE.

**Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI or kill decision**

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Cannot find acquisition target with <35% owner-dependency at 3.5x SDE or below
- Cleaner wage floor exceeds $22/hr in target geography with no pricing power to offset
- Customer churn exceeds 40% annually in acquired book
- Route density below 3 stops per cleaner per day (unprofitable windshield time)
- Recurring revenue below 50% of total (too much one-time/spot work)
- Manager salary ($60-80K) erodes SDE below $100K post-acquisition
- Labor market too tight — cannot recruit/retain cleaners within 90 days
