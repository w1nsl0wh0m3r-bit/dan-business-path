## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL, ECOMMERCE-FIRST NICHE PLAY
The 40+ athlete supplement niche sits at the intersection of two durable trends: aging athlete health awareness and DTC supplement growth. The economics can work as a lean ecommerce/playbook brand, but retail footprint is a trap — margin compression from GNC, Amazon, and direct-to-consumer incumbents is fierce. Dan should evaluate this as a niche ecommerce acquisition or brand build, not a bricks-and-mortar retail play.

**Dan-specific take:** This is more of a **brand/consumer thesis** than Dan's typical compliance-route sweet spot. The diligence edge still applies (customer LTV, CAC, margin structure, repeat purchase rate), but the operating model is fundamentally different from his service-route framework. Operator partner must have supplement/nutrition industry experience.

## 2. Market Sizing

- Sports nutrition segment: ~$15-20B of the total
- 40+ athlete / masters athlete segment: growing but fragmented; no clean public market sizing. Estimate $2-5B based on the ~35M Americans 40+ who exercise regularly × $50-150/year supplement spend (estimate, no single authoritative source)
- Key sub-categories: protein powder, creatine, collagen, omega-3, joint support (glucosamine/chondroitin/MSM), electrolyte/recovery, testosterone support, adaptogens

**Incumbents:** GNC (bankruptcy/restructuring 2024), Vitamin Shoppe (Franchise Group), Amazon private label, Walmart/EQUATE, Costco/Kirkland

**PE / M&A activity:**
- **Unilever** acquired Onnit (2024) — signals CPG interest in fitness supplements
- **Nestlé Health Science** — Atrium Innovations (Garden of Life, Pure Encapsulations)
- **KKR** — acquired Nature's Bounty (2017, later merged with Nestlé)
- **Byward Capital, M13, Penguin Ventures** — active in DTC supplement space
- **Hain Celestial, Church & Dwight** — strategic acquirers

**Fragmentation:** Highly fragmented at the niche/indie brand level. 1,000+ small supplement brands selling on Shopify/Amazon. Most have < $1M revenue. Consolidation opportunity exists but requires brand-building expertise.

**Moat:** Brand trust, NSF/Informed Sport certifications, subscription stickiness, proprietary formulations (iffy moat — supplements are easily copied).

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- **SBA eligible:** Yes for ecommerce/retail supplement businesses with 2+ years history
- **Typical structure:** Asset sale (inventory, brand, customer list, domain, Amazon seller account)
- **Multiple range:** 2.5-4x SDE for ecommerce brands; 1.5-2.5x for retail locations
- **Capital discipline:** Verify repeat purchase rate by pulling Shopify/Amazon data directly. Deduct owner salary. If LTV:CAC < 2.5:1, the marketing engine is broken — walk away. Never pay for "brand potential" — pay for demonstrated cash flow.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- LTV:CAC ratio below 2.5:1 on trailing 6 months
- Subscription/repeat rate below 25% of revenue
- FDA warning letters or regulatory compliance issues in target's history
- Single manufacturing source with no backup (supply chain risk)
- Amazon account has policy violations or suppressed listings
- Owner is the brand (personal following) — non-transferable
- Gross margin below 50% (commodity reseller, not brand)
- CAC trending up >15% QoQ with no path to improvement
