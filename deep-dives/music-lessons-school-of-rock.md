# Music Lessons / School of Rock Model Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium-High — School of Rock data found via search summary; verify official FDD before action.*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
Music lessons are a solid recurring local services category, and School of Rock’s group-performance model is more differentiated than generic one-on-one tutoring. The catch: the business is talent/culture-driven and often depends on local school leadership. For Dan, the best play is buying or backing an operator-led music school with a strong community brand — not blindly paying a franchise premium unless Item 19 confirms manager-run NOI after full fees.

## 2. Market Sizing
- The U.S. market for private music instruction/music schools is fragmented; IBISWorld and similar industry datasets track “Music Schools” but headline figures are paywalled. Treat exact TAM claims as **source-limited estimates** unless IBISWorld is purchased.
- Demand drivers: affluent parents, enrichment spending, homeschool/after-school programming, and recurring subscriptions.
- Comparable category logic: youth enrichment behaves like tutoring/dance/gymnastics — local density and instructor quality matter more than national TAM.

**Independent music school economics:**
- Revenue: $250K-$1.0M.
- Instructor pay: 35-55% of lesson revenue depending W2/1099 model and group mix.
- SDE: $75K-$250K, with high owner-dependency common.
- Entry multiple: 2.0-3.0x SDE for small studios; 3.0-4.0x for manager-run school with recurring enrollment and documented churn.
- Payback: 3-5 years; franchise buildout can push longer unless sales ramp to $800K+.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **School of Rock** — performance-based group music education; strong national brand.
- **Bach to Rock** — music lessons, camps, parties, DJ programs; franchise competitor.
- **Music & Arts / Guitar Center lessons** — national retail-linked lesson provider.
- **Local music schools** — fragmented, often founder-led.
- **Private instructors** — low overhead, price competition, but less community/camp programming.

## 6. Operator Economics
A great general manager/music director is non-negotiable. Budget $65K-$100K plus bonus. A $900K School of Rock-type unit at 22% NOI already includes management labor and imputed royalties in the cited summary, so manager-run cash flow could be attractive if local performance matches. Independent schools with $150K SDE owner-operated may fall to $50K-$80K after replacing the owner.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- 150+ active students, low monthly churn, recurring autopay.
- Instructor roster not dependent on founder.
- Group classes/camps/performances, not just private lessons.
- Lease with rehearsal/performance-friendly zoning/noise terms.
- Strong local school relationships.
Deal sources: BizBuySell, local enrichment brokers, retiring owner outreach, music teacher networks.

## 8. Dan Fit — 3.7 / 5
Moderate-good fit. Dan can underwrite recurring revenue, cohorts, pricing, and local density. But music school success is cultural and community-led — less purely operational than pet grooming or youth sports. Needs a passionate operator.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- Unit economics depend on founder teaching or personal community reputation.
- Instructor turnover breaks lesson continuity.
- Rent exceeds 12-14% of revenue without proven enrollment density.
- Franchise total fee load >12% with no demonstrable lead-gen advantage.
- No recurring autopay/churn data.
