# Laser Hair Removal / Medspa Treatment — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Good repeat-treatment economics in affluent markets, but only with licensed clinical compliance, utilization discipline, and provider labor under control.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Laser Hair Removal / Medspa Treatment is a local services / consumer operating business built around paid, repeatable service delivery rather than a pure ecommerce product.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C women and men 18-55; secondary trans/gender-affirming, athletes, brides/events; some cross-sell into injectables/skin.
- Demand drivers: Demand from permanent hair reduction, subscription packages, aesthetic normalization, lower pain/faster devices, and medspa consolidation. NE/NY/SFL fit is strong in affluent dense markets. Market size: Grand View Research/Allied Market Research describe global laser hair removal as multi-billion with high-single-digit CAGR; U.S. medspa is also multi-billion per AmSpa.
- Geographic relevance: NE/Boston and NY/CT/NJ are primary screens where density/income support premium service; SFL is relevant where weather, demographics, or affluent consumer behavior supports demand.
- Market size: cited sources below or explicitly labeled estimates; no fake precision.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Good target: $1.0M-$2.2M revenue, $200K-$500K SDE after clinical director/manager, 3+ years, 4+ treatment rooms, device ownership/leases clear, state medical-spa compliance clean, low refund/package liability. Avoid Groupon dependence, expiring device leases, illegal corporate practice of medicine structure, one injector/laser tech driving the business. Franchises: LaserAway (large medspa chain; request FDD), Milan Laser (company-heavy), SEV Laser (franchise expansion), Simplicity Laser, BodyBrite/LightRx-style concepts. Verify Item 19, royalty/ad fund, required devices, medical director rules.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- See named franchise systems above; request current FDD, Item 19, closure history, royalty/ad fund, territory rules, and required capex before any commitment.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: LaserAway, Milan Laser, SEV Laser, Ideal Image, Simplicity Laser, local derms/plastic surgeons, medspas. PE is active in aesthetics/dermatology/medspa platforms. Fragmentation remains high. Moat: compliance, reviews, provider retention, device quality, package conversion, financing, and safe outcomes.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset/equity deal depends on medical-license structure; require healthcare counsel. SBA can work but lender diligence is heavier. Base down payment ≈$98K plus working capital/device reserves. Payback ~2-3 years if utilization holds. Avoid paying strategic medspa multiples; at Dan scale underwrite 3-4.5x SDE, not platform EBITDA.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): map state CPOM/laser rules in MA/NY/FL, request FDDs, collect menu/package pricing.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): interview operator partner/clinical director candidates; model room utilization and device leases.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI only if compliance clean and package liability/reviews/provider roster check out.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- NO-GO if licensing structure is uncertain, adverse-event history is hidden, >25% revenue is discount marketplace, device leases consume cash flow, paid clinical oversight kills SDE, or customer acquisition depends on constant paid ads.
