# Well Pump Service — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Strong rural/suburban emergency-service niche with recurring testing/service adjacency and limited national franchise competition. Works best as an acquisition of a trusted local well-service/drilling shop with an operator partner.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Well pump service installs, repairs, and replaces submersible/jet pumps, pressure tanks, controls, wiring, well caps, water lines, and often cross-sells water testing/treatment. Customers are homeowners and farms/small commercial properties on private wells. Demand is needs-based and urgent: no pump means no water. NE exurbs/rural NY/CT/NJ are attractive; SFL is weaker for private wells except select rural pockets. EPA says about 43 million Americans use private wells, and owners are responsible for testing/maintenance because EPA does not regulate private wells directly.
- Who are the customers? Mix varies, but target underwriting should separate residential emergency demand from B2B/property-manager/municipal accounts.
- Demand drivers: needs-based failure, compliance/inspection triggers, aging infrastructure, and recurring maintenance or consumables where applicable.
- Geographic relevance: NE/Boston exurbs and NY/CT/NJ are primary for this batch; SFL is category-specific and weaker for basement/private-well models.
- Market size: use cited national installed-base indicators where available; otherwise treat as local serviceable-market estimate and do bottom-up ZIP/county mapping.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue $1M-$2.5M, SDE $250K-$500K, 10+ years, licensed well/pump staff, strong reviews, 24/7 emergency capability, equipment/rigs in good condition, customer database with well depths/service history, water-treatment cross-sell. Avoid owner-only license, long drive-time geography, undocumented cash, or heavy drilling capex masquerading as service revenue.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- No dominant pure well-pump franchise. Adjacent options: Mr. Rooter/Roto-Rooter for plumbing, Culligan/Kinetico for water treatment, and local well-driller associations. Franchise path is weak unless paired with water treatment; acquisition is the better path.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Local well drillers/service shops dominate. Adjacent competitors include plumbers, water-treatment dealers, Roto-Rooter/Mr. Rooter in some territories, and regional groundwater contractors. Fragmentation is high because licensing/geology/local trust matter. Moat is emergency response, local aquifer knowledge, service records, licensing, and the ability to solve pump + pressure + water quality together.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset purchase with equipment appraisal, seller note, and transition for licenses/relationships. SBA works but separate service EBITDA from drilling rig capex. Base $1.24M deal needs ~$124K down; payback 2.5-3.5 years if emergencies and treatment cross-sell are real. Buy 3.0-3.75x; only pay 4.5x for management depth and documented recurring testing/treatment book.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Days 1-30: map private-well density using county health/state data, list top-reviewed shops, talk to groundwater associations and SBA lenders. Days 31-60: operator partner search, license transfer review, inspect equipment, verify well-record database. Days 61-90: LOI with equipment adjustment, seller transition, and treatment/testing growth plan.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Owner is only licensed technician
- No documented service history/well-depth database
- Drive times too long for emergency response
- Rig/equipment replacement cliff
- Revenue mostly drilling projects with low repeat service
