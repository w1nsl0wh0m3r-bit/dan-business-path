# Drainage / Yard Water Management Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Category rank: #29 | Blended score: 3.48 | Confidence: Medium-Low*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
This is a **conditional GO** and probably better as an add-on thesis to landscaping, basement waterproofing, or hardscaping than as a standalone first acquisition. Demand is real in New England: older homes, heavy rain events, grading issues, basements, sump systems, French drains, dry wells, catch basins, downspout routing, and erosion control. But market data is fragmented; most published market-size numbers are broader landscaping, stormwater management, basement waterproofing, or drainage systems rather than “yard drainage contractors.”

Best version: acquire a small drainage/waterproofing contractor with high-ticket residential leads and bolt on to landscaping/hardscape capabilities.

## 2. Market Sizing
Because “yard drainage contractor” is not a clean NAICS market, use triangulation:
- **Landscaping services market:** Grand View Research estimated global landscaping services at **$330.58B in 2024** and **$484.79B by 2030**; yard drainage sits inside/adjacent to this spend ([GVR](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/landscaping-services-market-report)).
- **Stormwater management systems:** public market research firms size global stormwater management in the tens of billions, but this includes municipal/infrastructure systems, not small residential contractors. Treat as directional only.
- **Basement waterproofing/foundation repair:** adjacent competitor pool includes Groundworks, B-Dry, EverDry, Basement Systems, and local waterproofers; these operators often sell interior drains/sump systems rather than exterior yard drainage.

## 3. Unit Economics
**Standalone local drainage contractor estimate:**
- Revenue: **$800K-$3M**.
- Average ticket: **$3K-$15K** for French drains/regrading/downspout solutions; larger waterproofing/drainage projects can exceed $25K.
- Gross margin: **35%-55%** if self-performing excavation/drainage; lower with subcontracted excavation.
- SDE/EBITDA: **12%-22%** for well-run local contractor.
- Entry multiple: **2.0x-3.2x SDE**.
- Payback: **3-4.5 years**.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **Groundworks** — national foundation repair/basement waterproofing consolidator; competes in high-ticket basement/drainage adjacent work.
- **Basement Systems / Contractor Nation** — dealer network for basement waterproofing, crawlspace, sump, and drainage systems.
- **B-Dry / EverDry Waterproofing** — waterproofing franchise/dealer style competitors in some markets.
- **Local landscapers/hardscapers** — often install exterior French drains, grading, catch basins.
- **Excavation contractors** — compete for larger exterior drainage jobs.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- Drainage/waterproofing contractor with documented lead sources and close rates.
- Crew leader + estimator not equal to seller.
- Before/after photo library, reviews mentioning water problems solved.
- Ticket-level gross margin and callbacks.
- Cross-sell potential into landscaping, grading, gutter/downspout, sump, crawlspace, and hardscape.

Avoid:
- Business is just a landscaper occasionally doing drains.
- No engineering/diagnostic competence; high callback/warranty risk.
- Heavy reliance on one rain-season surge.
- Excavation equipment capex hides true earnings.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No estimator/technical operator available.
- Warranty/callback rate >5%-8% of jobs without reserves.
- Gross margin below 35%.
- Equipment replacement needs exceed 50% of purchase price.
- Lead flow only appears after storms and cannot be systematized.
