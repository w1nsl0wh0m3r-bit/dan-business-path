# Batting Cage / Baseball-Softball Training — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Conditional: youth sports demand is real, but fixed rent and coach dependence can wreck returns; buy an existing profitable facility with memberships/teams, not a ground-up cage hobby project.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? An indoor or outdoor baseball/softball facility selling cage rentals, private lessons, clinics, teams, camps, memberships and sometimes retail/equipment.
- Who are the customers? B2C families and youth athletes, plus B2B team rentals, clubs, schools, trainers and tournament/program partners.
- Demand drivers: youth sports specialization, off-season training, travel-ball intensity, weather-constrained NE practice demand and recurring lesson/membership packages.
- Geographic relevance: Strong in NE/NY/CT/NJ because weather creates indoor demand; SFL has year-round baseball but more outdoor competition.
- Market size: SFIA and Aspen Institute show baseball/softball among major youth participation sports; local market should be modeled by youth athletes, travel teams and indoor facility supply.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $600K-$1.8M with proven cage utilization and diversified coaches/programs.
- SDE range: $200K-$600K normalized manager-run SDE; below that is too owner-job-like.
- Years in business: 4+ years, with at least 3 years of tax returns/POS data.
- Route density / customer count: 500+ active athletes/year, 100+ members or recurring lesson clients, 20+ team/club rental relationships.
- Key assets to verify: lease/HVAC, pitching machines, turf/netting, scheduling/POS, coach contracts, camp rosters, waivers, insurance, reviews.
- Red flags to avoid: lease too short for buildout, seller is star coach, underutilized weekday hours, safety/waiver gaps, travel-ball politics.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: D-BAT, HitTrax-enabled facilities/franchises, Extra Innings-style independents, Baseballism retail is only an adjacent analog; verify current FDDs.
- FDD highlight: Item 7 buildout/real estate and Item 19 member/lesson economics are make-or-break.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only; if Item 19 is absent, validate with franchisee calls and treat franchisor examples as marketing.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top 5-10 competitors or roll-ups active in this space: D-BAT, HitTrax facilities, local indoor academies, town cages, travel-ball clubs, school facilities and private coaches.
- PE activity: Youth sports facilities are attracting platform capital, but small batting-cage assets remain local and fragmented.
- Fragmentation level: High; most markets have independents with uneven digital ops.
- Moat: coach roster, team relationships, off-season indoor access, utilization software, reviews and safety/process discipline.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to retention; inventory/equipment and lease assignments diligenced separately.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if tax returns, lease/licensing and cash flow are clean; underwrite 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $75K-$250K plus working capital for a $750K-$2.0M entry price.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on manager-run SDE; walk above 5 years.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned by professionalizing reporting, de-risking staff/customer concentration and adding repeat revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build NE/NY/SFL competitor and pricing grid.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits; request tax returns, POS/customer cohorts, lease/equipment schedules and labor roster.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if manager-run SDE, debt service coverage and local whitespace survive diligence.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Manager-run SDE below $175K after replacing owner labor.
- Asking price above 4.5x verified SDE without exceptional recurring revenue.
- Seller cannot reconcile P&L to tax returns/POS/bank statements.
- Lease, license, medical/regulatory, insurance or equipment dependency cannot transfer cleanly.
- Base-case rent plus payroll consumes more than 65% of revenue or seller-coach revenue cannot transfer.
