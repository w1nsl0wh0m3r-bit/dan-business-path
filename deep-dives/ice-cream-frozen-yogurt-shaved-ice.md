# Ice Cream / Frozen Yogurt / Shaved Ice — Deep Dive Investment Memo
*Generated: 2026-05-26 | Category rank: ~75 | UW Score: 3.20*

## 1. Executive Verdict — NO-GO AS ACQUISITION, CONDITIONAL AS SEASONAL POP-UP / TEST
Ice cream and frozen dessert shops are a notoriously tough category: high seasonality, low margins after rent and labor, product commoditization, and fad-driven consumer preferences (frozen yogurt peaked and crashed 2015-2019). The economics rarely support a manager-run acquisition at Dan's scale. The one path worth testing: a seasonal shaved-ice or soft-serve cart/truck with minimal fixed costs, positioned in high-traffic coastal NE or SFL locations.

**Dan-specific take:** This category scores well on "fun factor" but poorly on the metrics that matter for Dan's framework — recurring revenue is low, seasonality is extreme (NE), and margins get squeezed by rent in the exact locations where foot traffic justifies the concept. **Shelve or test cheap only.**

## 2. Market Sizing
- US ice cream and frozen dessert market: **$25-30B** (IDFA, Statista, 2024 estimates)
- Ice cream shop / scoop shop segment: ~$5-8B of total
- Shaved ice / Hawaiian shave ice: ~$200-400M niche (estimate; no clean public data)
- Frozen yogurt: declined from ~$2B peak (2013) to ~$800M-$1B (2024 estimate); store count dropped ~60% from peak
- Demand drivers: hot weather, tourism, family outings, impulse purchases
- Growth segments: artisan/gelato, rolled ice cream (fad fading), shaved ice (growing in SFL/SE)

**Example:** Established ice cream shop in Cape Cod tourist town. Revenue: $350K (May-October). Rent: $48K (year-round lease). COGS: $70K. Labor: $105K (seasonal staff). Other: $40K. SDE: $87K (25% margin). Entry at 2x = $174K. SBA 7(a) 10% down = $17.4K. Cash-on-cash: ($87K - $24K) / $17.4K = **362%**. BUT: 6 months of near-zero revenue, and the model breaks if one bad summer (weather, tourism downturn).

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox

**Acquisition target criteria (shop):**
- Revenue: $200K-$500K
- SDE: $60K-$120K
- Location: owned or long-term lease at below-market rent
- 5+ years operating history
- Strong seasonal traffic (tourist area, beach town, boardwalk)

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- **SBA eligible:** Yes for established shops with 2+ years
- **Typical structure:** Asset sale (equipment, lease assignment, goodwill). Landlord approval critical.
- **Multiple range:** 1.5-3x SDE. Reflects thin margins and seasonality.
- **Capital discipline:** Never pay >2.5x SDE for a seasonal ice cream shop. Verify 3 years of tax returns. Deduct owner's counter labor. Confirm lease terms and renewal options. Walk away if rent >15% of revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap

**Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market assessment & cheap test planning**
- Map 20+ ice cream/frozen dessert shops in target geographies (Cape Cod, Newport, SFL beach towns)
- Interview 3 shop owners about seasonal economics
- Research mobile vendor permits in target towns (costs, availability, restrictions)
- Evaluate Kona Ice franchise FDD ($25K franchise fee, 6% royalty)
- Scout 5 high-traffic locations for mobile cart/truck placement

**Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Mobile cart test or acquisition evaluation**
- If pursuing mobile path: secure permits, purchase/lease cart ($10-20K used), test at 3 locations
- If pursuing acquisition: request financials from 3 established shops
- Build 3-case model for each path
- Validate tourist traffic data (local chamber of commerce, STR data)

**Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Decision**
- Mobile test: evaluate first 30 days of revenue. If <$300/day average, kill.
- Acquisition: if no target clears buybox at ≤2.5x SDE with transferable lease, shelve.
- This category likely belongs on the **watchlist** unless a killer deal surfaces.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- SDE margin below 15% (rent/labor squeeze)
- Revenue concentration >75% in any single quarter (NE seasonality too extreme)
- Annual revenue below $150K (not enough to justify management overhead)
- Mobile cart daily revenue below $300/day during peak season
- Rent/lease above 15% of revenue
- Location dependent on single-event traffic (festival, boardwalk season only)
- Frozen yogurt concept (secular decline, not recovering)
- Owner operates counter 30+ hours/week (job, not investment)
