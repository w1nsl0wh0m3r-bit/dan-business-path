## 2. Market Sizing
- **U.S. exam prep/test prep:** secondary research sources commonly estimate roughly **$3B-$4B** in the U.S. mid-decade; exact IBISWorld figures require paid access.
- **Fragmentation:** Navagant summary citing IBISWorld says no company has more than 5% share and online services were 63% of revenue in 2023; verify with source report before underwriting.
- **Broader tutoring:** demand supported by learning loss, college admissions pressure, private-school competition, and parent willingness to spend; pressured by AI homework tools, online platforms, and SAT/ACT policy volatility.

## 3. Unit Economics
**Center-based tutoring franchise / independent center:**
- Revenue per center: **$400K-$1.2M** typical range; elite territories may exceed this.
- Gross margin: **45%-65%** after tutor labor, before rent/admin/royalties.
- EBITDA/SDE: **10%-25%** if owner-managed; manager-run often lower.
- SDE target: many centers produce **$75K-$250K**, making Dan's $200K-$500K SDE target possible only with multi-center or premium test prep.
- Entry multiple: **2.0x-3.5x SDE** for small independent centers; franchises vary with transfer approval and brand strength.
- Payback: **3-5 years**, but manager-run economics can disappoint.

## 6. Operator Economics
Example: acquire $800K revenue / 18% owner-operated SDE = **$144K SDE** at **2.5x = $360K EV**.
- Center director/operator: **$60K-$90K + bonus**.
- Remaining pre-debt owner cash: **$55K-$85K**.
- This is the core problem: paying an operator often consumes the very SDE Dan is buying.

To hit Dan's target after operator, he likely needs either:
1. multi-center platform with $2M+ revenue, or
2. premium test-prep boutique with high tutor utilization and non-owner sales.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
If pursued despite NO-GO:
