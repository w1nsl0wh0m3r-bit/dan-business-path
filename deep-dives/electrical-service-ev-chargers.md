# Electrical Service / EV Chargers Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium-High (market growth public; small-contractor economics require CIMs)*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **GO, BUT DO NOT UNDERWRITE AS “EV ONLY”**
Electrical service is a real, durable local-services category. EV chargers are a strong lead magnet and growth wedge, but a standalone EV-charger installer is too cyclical and policy/vehicle-adoption exposed. **Best path:** buy a residential/light-commercial electrical contractor with service revenue, panel upgrades, generator/EV charger capability, and a licensed operator. EV chargers should be one revenue line, not the whole thesis.

## 2. Market Sizing

## 6. Operator Economics
A $2M contractor at 18% SDE margin generates $360K. Pay a master-electrician GM/service manager $130K fully loaded and Dan has $230K pre-debt cash flow. At a 3.25x purchase price ($1.17M), debt service may consume much of early cash flow, but the business can still compound through pricing, dispatch, and add-on services.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- 5-15 electricians/techs, not a solo owner truck.
- Residential service + light commercial maintenance, not just bid construction.
- Panel upgrades, generators, EV chargers, heat-pump wiring, smart panels.
- Licensed master electrician willing to stay or be replaced by operator partner.
- Strong local reviews, call tracking, service software, repeat customer list.
Avoid:
- New construction subcontractors with GC concentration and low margins.
- Owner holds the only license and will exit immediately.
- Revenue spike from temporary EV incentives without repeat service base.

## 8. Dan Fit — **4.1 / 5**
Strong if framed as essential local service, not EV hype. Dan’s edge in diligence and professionalization applies well. Main gap is technical licensing/operations, solvable with operator partner or seller transition.

## 9. 90-Day Roadmap
1. Build New England target list from Google Maps, state license databases, and contractor associations.
2. Interview 10 electricians about tech availability, pricing, and EV charger lead quality.
3. Pull FDDs for Mr. Electric and Mister Sparky only as operating benchmarks.
4. Underwrite acquisition cases by revenue mix: service, construction, EV/panel, commercial maintenance.
5. Identify operator: licensed master electrician or service manager with dispatch discipline.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No durable license coverage post-close.
- >40% revenue from low-margin construction/GC bids.
- EV chargers >50% of revenue with no broader electrical service base.
- Tech turnover high or wages under-market.
- Purchase price >4x SDE without recurring commercial/service revenue.
