## 2. Market Sizing
- US marine construction market estimated at **$3-5B** annually (estimate based on US Army Corps of Engineers permitting data and marine contractor association estimates)
- Coastal states (FL, MA, CT, NY, NJ, ME) represent ~60% of US waterfront residential/marina infrastructure
- FL alone: ~1,200+ miles of coastline with ~500,000 waterfront residential parcels (FL DEP estimate)
- Demand drivers: aging waterfront infrastructure (many seawalls/docks built 1970s-1990s reaching end of life), hurricane/storm damage cycles, rising water levels increasing maintenance urgency, marina/commercial waterfront upgrades
- Recurring maintenance (seawall inspection, pressure washing, pile wrapping, lighting) provides 20-30% of revenue for established operators

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox

**Acquisition target criteria:**
- Revenue: $400K-$1.5M
- SDE: $100K-$350K
- Licensed marine contractor (FL Class A/B, MA Construction Supervisor, CT)
- 50+ recurring maintenance contracts
- 3+ years operating history with tax returns
- Equipment owned (barge, pile driver, excavator) or documented lease
- Insurance: marine general liability, workers comp, pollution liability

**Franchise landscape:** Essentially nonexistent. No national marine construction franchises. This is a pure independent acquisition play.

**Geographic focus:**
- **SE Florida** (Palm Beach to Miami): highest waterfront density, year-round season, hurricane damage cycle
- **Cape Cod / Southeastern MA**: seasonal but high-value, aging infrastructure
- **CT Gold Coast** (Greenwich to Fairfield): high property values, defer-and-replace cycle
- **NJ Shore**: post-Sandy rebuild cycle ongoing

## 5. Competitive Landscape

**Operator types:**
- Local marine contractors (5-25 employees) — the dominant players
- General contractors who take on waterfront work (lower quality, less specialized)
- Engineering firms (high-end, commercial/municipal only)
- Diving services companies (underwater inspection/repair)

**PE / M&A activity:**
- **Limited** — marine construction is too localized and seasonal for traditional PE roll-up
- Occasional acquisitions by regional civil construction platforms expanding into marine
- **Summit Materials, Construction Partners** — adjacent (heavy civil/marine-capable)
- Most transactions are owner-to-owner sales, not PE-backed

**Fragmentation:** Extremely fragmented. Most markets have 5-15 local marine contractors. The best are 10-30 years old with deep relationships with waterfront property managers, HOAs, and marinas.

**Moat:** State marine contractor licensing, waterfront access/permitting knowledge, equipment investment ($200K-$500K minimum), insurance requirements, local reputation and relationships.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- **SBA eligible:** Yes — marine contractors with 2+ years history qualify
- **Typical structure:** Asset sale (equipment, contracts, licenses, goodwill). Seller note 10-20% common.
- **Multiple range:** 2.5-4x SDE. License and recurring contract premium at the top end.
- **Capital discipline:** Verify recurring maintenance revenue by contacting 10+ clients. Deduct owner's field labor at market rate. Value equipment separately — don't pay for depreciated equipment at replacement cost. Budget $25-50K working capital for seasonal cash flow gaps.
