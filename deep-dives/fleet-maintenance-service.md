# Fleet Maintenance Service — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Strong B2B recurring-revenue service if bought as a route/density platform: preventive maintenance, DOT compliance, mobile repair, and uptime pressure create repeat demand.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Fleet maintenance businesses service commercial vans, trucks, trailers and specialty vehicles through scheduled PMs, inspections, tires, brakes, diagnostics, light repair and mobile emergency support. Customers are B2B: delivery fleets, contractors, municipalities, school/van operators, landscapers, HVAC/plumbing fleets and logistics yards. Demand is recurring because fleet owners buy uptime and compliance, not one-off repairs. NE/NY/SFL relevance is strong: dense contractor fleets around Boston/NY/NJ/CT and logistics/service fleets in SFL. Market-size claims should be triangulated from truck repair/fleet maintenance IBISWorld categories, public fleet growth, and local vehicle counts; no single clean TAM captures mobile and shop-based maintenance.
- Who are the customers? B2B/B2C split varies by account mix; underwrite each target from actual invoices, not seller narrative.
- Demand drivers: recurring account relationships, vehicle parc, compliance/uptime, weather, accidents, owner convenience, and local density depending on category.
- Geographic relevance: Boston/NE primary, NY/CT/NJ secondary, SFL tertiary; see category-specific notes above.
- Market size: use cited industry pages and public-company/FDD context as directional only; local revenue density matters more than fake TAM precision.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue $1.2M-$3.5M; SDE $250K-$500K for first platform; 5+ years; 50+ active fleet accounts or 500+ serviced vehicles; mobile vans, lifts/diagnostics, tech certifications, PM schedules and customer concentration. Avoid heavy engine rebuild dependence, one mega-account >35%, weak tech bench, undocumented PM contracts, or receivables >60 days. Franchise candidates: Christian Brothers fleet programs, Grease Monkey/FullSpeed automotive, Meineke fleet services, Midas fleet services, and mobile fleet concepts such as Wrench/YourMechanic-like independents; FDD economics vary and shop acquisition is cleaner than greenfield franchise.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Prefer franchise only where FDD economics are transparent, territory is protected, royalty/ad fund leaves 15%+ manager-run SDE, and franchisee validation confirms owner-absentee feasibility.
- Validate Item 19, royalty, ad fund, initial investment, supplier restrictions, required owner involvement, and resale history.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
Competitors include local diesel/auto repair shops, mobile mechanics, dealerships, Ryder/Penske maintenance networks, Cox Automotive Fleet Services, Holman, Merchants Fleet vendors, and national tire/service chains. PE is active around fleet services and mobile maintenance platforms. Fragmentation remains high below regional scale. Moat is PM data, yard access, SLA response, technician retention, diagnostic capability, and trust with fleet managers.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout tied to customer retention, working-capital peg, equipment inspection, lease assignment, and 30-90 day seller transition.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA 7(a)-financeable when books are clean, licenses/contracts transfer, collateral/equipment is documented, and no unresolved legal/environmental issue exists.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10% of purchase price plus closing costs and working capital; keep total cash at risk below what a downside case can repay in 3-4 years.
- Payback period at base case: generally 2-4 years on equity if base-case SDE holds after debt service.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: assume ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, recurring revenue, documented management depth, and clean add-on pipeline are proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map local competitors in Boston/NE, NY/CT/NJ, and SFL; pull broker listings; request franchise FDDs where relevant; interview 3 operators or former employees; build target list.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator partner; normalize 3 years of financials; verify licenses, lease, equipment, reviews, customer concentration and recurring revenue; conduct site visits.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one disciplined LOI or reject the category; if franchise path survives, compare FDD economics with acquisition model and require franchisee validation.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- No recurring PM schedule; top customer >35% of gross profit; techs would leave with seller; no mobile capability in dense markets; A/R aging masks bad customers; environmental/waste handling sloppy; owner is the only estimator/dispatcher.
