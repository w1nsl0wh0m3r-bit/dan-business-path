# Premium Dessert / Soft Serve / Gelato — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO
Good consumer impulse category for a disciplined single-site or small multi-unit operator, but not a great "pay-up franchise" category. Works if Dan buys/builds in a dense affluent trade area, keeps buildout sane, and treats catering/events + high-margin beverage/packaged add-ons as core revenue, not bonus.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A premium dessert shop selling gelato, soft serve, sundaes, affogato, shakes, novelties, and possibly coffee. It is a small-format food retail concept with high gross margin but meaningful seasonality and labor/lease sensitivity.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C walk-in families, teens, tourists, date-night customers, and office/retail foot traffic; B2B is events, catering, school/community bulk orders, and corporate drops.
- Demand drivers: affordable indulgence, social media-friendly products, premiumization, family outing occasions, tourism/foot traffic, catering. Frequency can be weekly/monthly for locals; tourist markets behave more like high-season transaction volume.
- Geographic relevance: Strong in NY/CT/NJ affluent suburbs, Boston/NE college/tourist towns, and SFL year-round. NE has worse winter seasonality; SFL improves utilization but competition is intense.
- Market size: Sources vary. IBISWorld estimates U.S. ice cream stores at roughly low-single-digit billions; Grand View/ResearchAndMarkets place broader ice cream/frozen dessert markets much larger because they include packaged retail. Treat shop-level TAM as "multi-billion U.S. estimate," not a precise figure.

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment. Approx annual debt service per $100K financed at 11%/10yr is ~$16.5K.
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with a food retail operator; manager-run economics must include a real GM wage.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $600K-$1.2M with POS proof by month/daypart.
- SDE range: $100K-$250K after normalizing a paid GM.
- Years in business: 3+ with stable lease and seasonality history.
- Route density / customer count: 20K+ loyalty/SMS/email contacts or obvious dense foot traffic; catering/events pipeline.
- Key assets to verify: POS data, lease assignment/options, equipment age, commissary rights if any, health inspection history, recipe/process documentation, brand/social following.
- Red flags to avoid: seller cash claims, rent >10%-12% of sales, winter losses hidden by summer spike, owner-as-brand dependence, no manager bench, no food cost controls.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Handel's Homemade Ice Cream: established franchise system; franchise fee and royalties vary by FDD; strong unit economics reported by franchisees but territory availability matters.
- Jeremiah's Italian Ice: franchise fee often cited around $30K; royalty/marketing fees in typical QSR franchise range; strong Florida/Sunbelt fit.
- Rita's Italian Ice: mature franchise; check Item 19 by quartile and closed-unit history.
- Ben & Jerry's / Baskin-Robbins: brand awareness, but royalties, product constraints, and mature territories can cap upside.
- FDD diligence: demand Item 19 revenue distributions, seasonal revenue curve, labor model, transfer/resale performance, and closure rates. Do not rely on AUV alone.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: Van Leeuwen, Jeni's, Salt & Straw, Amorino, Venchi, Gelato-go, Baskin-Robbins, Ben & Jerry's, local scoop shops, bubble tea/dessert cafes, Crumbl/Insomnia-type sweets.
- PE activity: consumer dessert brands have sponsor interest at brand scale, but small-unit acquisitions are mostly independents/franchise resales.
- Fragmentation level: High. National brands matter, but most trade areas still have independent scoop shops and dessert cafes.
- Moat: location, lease, product quality, operations consistency, catering relationships, local brand/social content, and ability to drive repeat traffic outside summer.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with equipment, recipes/IP if transferable, lease assignment, seller transition, and 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to sales retention.
- SBA eligibility: usually financeable if books are clean, lease term covers loan, health permits transfer/renew cleanly, and buyer/operator credibility is adequate.
- Down payment required: base $420K purchase implies ~$42K down plus working capital and closing costs; realistic cash need $90K-$150K.
- Payback period at base case: roughly 2-3 years if $120K SDE holds and no major capex; longer if winter losses require cash support.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy a boring independent at ~3.0-3.5x, professionalize catering/CRM, sell a 2-3 unit mini-platform at ~4x if manager-run.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Pull 20 listings in NE/NY/SFL; request FDDs from 3-4 dessert franchises; visit top local operators at peak/off-peak; map rent/foot-traffic corridors.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build SKU-level food/labor model; interview 3 operators/GMs; test catering/event demand; identify an operator partner with food-retail repetition.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit LOI only on a shop with clean POS + lease or select one franchise for deeper FDD/franchisee validation.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Rent above 12% of sales with no path to renegotiate.
- SDE disappears after adding a market-rate GM.
- Revenue is heavily seasonal and winter cash burn is not funded by summer profit.
- Food cost above 30% without premium pricing power.
- No health inspection discipline or equipment replacement reserve.
- Franchise FDD shows weak median unit economics, high closures, or unhappy franchisees.
