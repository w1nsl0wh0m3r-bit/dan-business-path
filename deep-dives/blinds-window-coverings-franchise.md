# Blinds / Window Coverings — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
One of the better Dan fits: consultative home-service sales, fragmented independents, decent ticket size, light showroom/route requirements, and a franchisable operating playbook. Underwrite lead flow and installer capacity hard.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Mobile or showroom-assisted sales and installation of blinds, shades, shutters, drapery, motorized coverings, and commercial window treatments.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C homeowners/remodelers; secondary B2B designers, builders, property managers, offices, schools, and hospitality.
- Demand drivers: Home remodeling, aging housing stock, energy efficiency, motorization/smart-home adoption, replacement after moves, and design refresh cycles.
- Geographic relevance: Excellent NE/NY/CT/NJ/SFL fit; affluent suburbs with older housing and high home values are attractive.
- Market size: U.S. window coverings market is commonly estimated in the low-to-mid tens of billions by Grand View/IBISWorld-style reports; cite as estimate unless using paid report.

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down; SBA says 7(a) can fund changes of ownership and caps at $5M.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite post-manager cash flow, not owner heroics.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $900K-$2.5M with diversified lead sources.
- SDE range: $200K-$500K after paid sales manager/GM.
- Years in business: 5+ years preferred because referral base matters.
- Route density / customer count: 300+ completed jobs/year, installer bench, designer/realtor relationships.
- Key assets to verify: CRM pipeline, vendor terms, installer/subcontractor agreements, warranty claims, reviews, sample inventory, vehicle/tools.
- Red flags to avoid: one designer/builder >25% revenue, chronic install delays, rebate/vendor concentration, margin leakage from measuring errors.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Budget Blinds: Home Franchise Concepts/Empower Brands; franchise fee often ~$89.9K; royalty commonly ~6.5% with ad/tech fees; mobile showroom model.
- Gotcha Covered: franchise fee often ~$69.9K; royalty around 6%; lower startup range.
- Bloomin' Blinds: sales/repair/install model; franchise fee commonly ~$49K; royalty around 6%.
- Made in the Shade: mobile window treatment franchise; lower overhead; request FDD.
- FDD highlight: compare gross margin after product COGS, installation labor/subcontractors, lead-source costs, and territory saturation.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: Budget Blinds, 3 Day Blinds, The Shade Store, Stoneside, Gotcha Covered, Bloomin' Blinds, local decorators, Lowe's/Home Depot, Hunter Douglas dealers.
- PE/activity: Home Franchise Concepts has sponsor history; Empower Brands consolidates home-service franchisors; Hunter Douglas has global ownership scale.
- Fragmentation: high at local dealer/installer level despite national brands.
- Moat: design consult quality, vendor access, reviews, fast install, accurate measurement, realtor/designer relationships, motorization expertise.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for unverifiable add-backs; stock purchase only if licenses/contracts require it and liabilities are clean.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if profitable, owner-operated or manager-run with qualified operator, and no speculative real estate component; SBA 7(a) supports ownership changes up to $5M.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus working capital; for a $700K-$1.4M enterprise value, plan $90K-$250K cash need.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on equity after debt service.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, manager bench, clean reporting, and repeat revenue are proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Request Budget Blinds/Gotcha Covered/Bloomin' Blinds FDDs; map top 75 dealers in MA/CT/NJ/NY; benchmark Google reviews and lead response.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview franchisees/installers; model gross margin by product; identify operator/sales lead and installer bench.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on independent with clean CRM and $200K+ SDE, or choose franchise if territory white space is real.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Gross margin below 45% without a clear mix explanation.
- Installer dependence on one subcontractor.
- Warranty/remake rate above 5%-7% of jobs.
- Paid leads exceed 25% of revenue with weak repeat/referral.
- No CRM or inability to tie quotes to close rate.
- Vendor concentration with poor terms or long lead times.
