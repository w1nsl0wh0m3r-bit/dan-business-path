# Swedish Death Cleaning / Estate Transition Logistics — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Unsexy, emotional, and fragmented — exactly why it can work. Best path is not a cute “death cleaning” brand; it is an estate-transition logistics operator combining downsizing, cleanout, donation/resale coordination, light moving, and senior-move management.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Service helping older adults/families declutter, downsize, organize, donate/sell/dispose of possessions, prepare homes for sale, and coordinate moves after death, illness or transition to senior living. Customers are adult children, seniors, estate attorneys, realtors, senior living communities and trusts. Demand is driven by aging population, boomer home turnover, aging-in-place transitions, and estate settlement complexity. NE/NY/SFL fit is excellent: wealthy older households, estate attorneys, dense condos/suburbs, and senior communities.
- Who are the customers? Mix varies by category; underwrite the actual revenue split before buying.
- Demand drivers: repeat use, referral channels, demographic tailwinds, and local density.
- Geographic relevance: Boston/NE primary, NY/CT/NJ secondary, South Florida tertiary.
- Market size: cited source context below; where exact segment data is unavailable, estimates are labeled.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, 10%-20% seller note, working-capital peg, 60-120 day seller transition; stock only if contracts/licenses require it.
- SBA eligibility and terms: generally SBA 7(a)-eligible if profitable, legal, transferable, and owner-operated/manager-operated with clean tax returns.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $35K-$280K depending on SDE and multiple, before working capital and diligence costs.
- Payback period at base case: usually 2.0-3.5 years if bought at 3.5-4.0x and manager cost is real.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → ~4x out at this scale; do not assume PE exit multiples.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map NE/NY/SFL operators, scrape reviews, identify 20 targets/franchises, request FDDs where relevant, and build local demand map.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview 5 operators/franchisees, validate paid-manager model, price local leads, and review 2-3 brokered deals or franchise disclosures.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one IOI/LOI or kill; if franchising, validate territory economics and complete SBA prequalification.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Seller cannot reconcile tax returns to P&L or refuses customer/channel detail.
- Manager-run SDE falls below $175K or base case cannot service SBA debt after paid operator compensation.
- Customer concentration >30%, vendor concentration >50%, or founder relationship risk cannot be transitioned.
- Local market has no defensible niche: generic product/service, high CAC, weak reviews, and no referral flywheel.
- Asking price exceeds 4.5x verified SDE without unusually durable recurring revenue.
