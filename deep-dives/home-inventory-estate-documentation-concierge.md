# Home Inventory / Estate Documentation Concierge — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Conditional: excellent downside protection as a low-capex concierge route, but only a GO if B2B referral channels create recurring documentation refreshes rather than one-off consumer projects.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A service documenting home contents, valuables, estate assets and insurance records using photos/video, structured inventories and secure reports for homeowners, fiduciaries and advisors.
- Who are the customers? Affluent homeowners, estate attorneys, trust officers, insurance agents, family offices, property managers and downsizing seniors.
- Demand drivers: climate/disaster risk, insurance documentation gaps, aging households, estate administration complexity and advisor referral loops.
- Geographic relevance: Very relevant in coastal SFL hurricane markets, affluent NY/CT/NJ and Boston suburbs with high-value homes; national if advisor-led.
- Market size: NAIC and insurance-advisor sources recommend home inventories; market size is fragmented/no precise category, so model bottom-up by affluent households and advisor referral partners.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $300K-$1.0M with repeat advisor/referral accounts, not just founder referrals.
- SDE range: $200K-$600K normalized manager-run SDE; below that is too owner-job-like.
- Years in business: 4+ years, with at least 3 years of tax returns/POS data.
- Route density / customer count: 100+ completed households/year or 20+ active advisor/referral sources in contiguous affluent counties.
- Key assets to verify: client database, sample reports, data-security workflow, referral agreements, insurance/E&O coverage, photography equipment, SOPs.
- Red flags to avoid: no data-security discipline, founder-only attorney relationships, no refresh revenue, weak privacy consents.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: No dominant pure-play franchise; analogs include Caring Transitions, Blue Moon Estate Sales, ShelfGenie/organizing-adjacent concepts and NAPO-certified organizers.
- FDD highlight: for adjacent franchises, test whether inventory/documentation is core revenue or just an add-on.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only; if Item 19 is absent, validate with franchisee calls and treat franchisor examples as marketing.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top 5-10 competitors or roll-ups active in this space: HomeZada, Encircle, Sortly, NAIC DIY templates, Caring Transitions, Blue Moon Estate Sales, local senior move managers and estate appraisers.
- PE activity: Limited pure-play PE; estate services/senior move management is fragmented with local roll-up potential.
- Fragmentation level: Extremely high; many solo organizers/appraisers, few professionalized documentation-first firms.
- Moat: advisor trust, privacy/security, repeat refresh cadence, high-quality report templates and local affluent network density.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to retention; inventory/equipment and lease assignments diligenced separately.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if tax returns, lease/licensing and cash flow are clean; underwrite 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $75K-$250K plus working capital for a $750K-$2.0M entry price.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on manager-run SDE; walk above 5 years.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned by professionalizing reporting, de-risking staff/customer concentration and adding repeat revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build NE/NY/SFL competitor and pricing grid.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits; request tax returns, POS/customer cohorts, lease/equipment schedules and labor roster.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if manager-run SDE, debt service coverage and local whitespace survive diligence.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Manager-run SDE below $175K after replacing owner labor.
- Asking price above 4.5x verified SDE without exceptional recurring revenue.
- Seller cannot reconcile P&L to tax returns/POS/bank statements.
- Lease, license, medical/regulatory, insurance or equipment dependency cannot transfer cleanly.
- No credible path to B2B referrals from insurance agents, estate attorneys or wealth advisors within 90 days.
