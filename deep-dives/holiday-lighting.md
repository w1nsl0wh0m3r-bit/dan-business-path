# Holiday Lighting — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Seasonal but highly lucrative per-job business with strong recurring dynamics (customers rehire annually). Best executed as a winter add-on to an existing exterior services company (landscaping, gutter, pressure washing) rather than a standalone. Franchise option (Christmas Decor, Blooms Today) provides SOPs and supplier access. Standalone acquisition is risky due to 4-month revenue concentration.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- **What is this business?** A seasonal service business that designs, installs, maintains, and removes holiday lighting and decorations for residential and commercial properties. Jobs range from $500-$3,000+ for residential, $2,000-$15,000+ for commercial (shopping centers, HOAs, office parks). The season runs October-January in NE/NY, with some year-round demand in SFL for event and commercial installations.
- **Who are the customers?** Affluent homeowners (HHI $150K+), property managers, HOAs, retail centers, restaurants, and commercial real estate owners. Customers value convenience, safety (roof/ladder work), and professional appearance over DIY.
- **Demand drivers:** Aging population (fewer homeowners willing to climb ladders), dual-income households (time-scarce), commercial property competition for foot traffic during holidays, social media-driven expectation of elaborate displays, HOA compliance requirements for uniform aesthetics.
- **Geographic relevance:** NE and NY are prime markets (strong seasonal tradition, older housing stock, high-income suburbs). Boston Metro (Wellesley, Newton, Lexington, Dover), NY suburbs (Westchester, Greenwich, Short Hills), and SFL (commercial and community installations, less seasonal concentration). SFL market is smaller but year-round.
- **Market size:** US holiday lighting installation market estimated $1.5-$3B (industry estimates; no single authoritative source). Christmas Decor reports 300+ franchise territories. Highly fragmented — dominated by local landscaping companies offering it as an add-on service.

|------|---------------|-----|-----------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| Bear | $150K | $30K | 20% | $90K-$105K | 3.0-3.5x | Low — seasonal only |

| Bull | $600K | $210K | 35% | $840K-$945K | 4.0-4.5x | 60%+ (annualized) |

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3.0-4.5x SDE for established book of business with recurring customer list.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down. Lender may require 2+ years of tax returns showing consistent seasonal revenue.
- Critical insight: this is a SEASONAL business. Annualized SDE is the figure, but cash flow is concentrated in 4 months. Dan needs a complementary warm-weather service (landscaping, gutter, pressure washing) or this is a part-time business.
- Operator partner: needs crew management and ladder/roof safety capability. Not complex technically but physically demanding and weather-dependent.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
- **Best path: Add-on to existing exterior services company.** The customer list, crew, trucks, and scheduling infrastructure already exist. Holiday lighting fills the winter revenue gap for landscaping companies.
- Revenue: $200K-$500K seasonal with 70%+ recurring customers
- Established customer list with 3+ years of retention data
- Commercial accounts (higher ticket, stickier, less weather-dependent)
- Storage facility for lighting inventory (off-season)
- Trained crews with safety certifications (ladder, roof work)
- Red flags: revenue is 100% seasonal with no complementary business, owner is sole installer, customer list is not documented, heavy reliance on a few large commercial accounts

**Franchise option:**
- **Christmas Decor:** 300+ territories, initial investment $75K-$150K, royalty ~7%. Provides supplier pricing, training, marketing materials, design software. On SBA Franchise Registry.
- **Blooms Today:** Flowers and holiday decor, different model.
- **Key franchise diligence:** territory size, supplier pricing advantage vs independent, marketing support quality, Item 19 financial performance data.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- **Landscaping companies (add-on)** — Primary competitors. Already have crews, trucks, and customer relationships. Christmas Decor and similar franchise systems target this segment.
- **Independent holiday lighting specialists** — Seasonal operators who do nothing else. Often owner-operator with 1-3 crews. Can be highly profitable but hard to scale beyond personal capacity.
- **Handyman/contractor side-gigs** — Low-end competition on price. Quality and safety vary widely.
- **DIY homeowners** — Shrinking as population ages. Still significant in mid-market.
- **PE activity:** Minimal at local level. Christmas Decor is owned by the Dwyer Group (now Neighborly), a PE-backed franchise platform.
- **Moat:** Recurring customer relationships, annual contracts, ladder/roof safety capability, commercial account relationships, design portfolio and reputation.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Deal structure: Asset purchase — customer list, inventory (lights, decorations, storage), equipment, vehicle fleet. Seller note tied to customer retention in first rehire season.
- SBA eligible but seasonal revenue scrutiny: lender will want 2-3 years of tax returns and may require collateral beyond business assets.
- Down payment at base case ($400K): ~$40K at 10% down.
- Payback period: 2-4 seasons at base case, IF customer retention holds.
- Multiple arbitrage: Limited — this is a local service business. Value comes from recurring customer list density and complementary service lines.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap
- **Phase 1 (Days 1-30):** Get Christmas Decor FDD. Call 10+ franchisees — ask about average ticket, recurring rate, seasonality, crew management. Map all holiday lighting operators in target NE/NY markets. Identify 5 landscaping companies that offer (or could offer) holiday lighting as an add-on.
- **Phase 2 (Days 31-60):** Build 3-case model. If pursuing acquisition, diligence 3 targets: verify customer list, retention rates, commercial vs residential split, inventory condition, crew availability. If pursuing franchise, compare Christmas Decor territory economics vs independent. Identify potential operator-partner with exterior services experience.
- **Phase 3 (Days 61-90):** If pursuing acquisition, submit LOI conditional on first-season customer retention verification. If franchise, sign territory agreement and begin pre-season marketing. Either path: confirm crew recruitment pipeline before October.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Revenue is 100% seasonal with no complementary warm-weather service line
- Customer retention below 60% (annual rehire rate)
- Crew recruitment is a persistent bottleneck — this is physically demanding seasonal work
- Average ticket is below $800 residential — indicates price-sensitive market
- Commercial accounts are less than 20% of revenue — need diversification from residential-only
- Territory has fewer than 50,000 affluent households in service area
- Insurance costs (roof/ladder liability) erode margins below 20%
