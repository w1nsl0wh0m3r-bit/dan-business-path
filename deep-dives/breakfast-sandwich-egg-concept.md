# Breakfast Sandwich / Egg Concept — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Attractive as a morning counter if rent is disciplined and catering/coffee attach are real; dangerous if treated like a full-service brunch concept with high labor and low throughput.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A consumer retail/foodservice concept serving a specific occasion with small-ticket, repeatable purchases. The investment question is whether the concept has enough local demand density and operating simplicity to support a manager-run unit.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C; B2B exists through catering, uniforms, bulk orders, or resale sourcing partnerships depending on the category.
- Demand drivers: convenience, novelty, social media, local density, workplace/school routines, and in electronics/workwear a replacement/utility need.
- Geographic relevance: Boston/NE primary, NY/CT/NJ secondary, Miami/SFL tertiary; prioritize dense affluent or student-heavy nodes.
- Market size: cited sources below where available; niche subcategory values are estimates when not separately reported.

Specific data points:
- **Market:** IBISWorld/Technavio category reports show US breakfast restaurant/limited-service breakfast demand growing with convenience, coffee, and off-premise; use paid reports for exact values.
- **Consumer behavior:** Circana/NPD has repeatedly cited breakfast as a resilient foodservice daypart, with traffic tied to commuting and coffee routines.
- **Ticket:** Breakfast sandwich $7-$13; coffee attach can lift basket to $11-$17.
- **AUV:** Independent urban counter estimate $650K-$1.4M; top franchised bagel/coffee concepts higher.
- **Food cost:** Egg/bread/cheese/protein COGS target 25%-32%; volatility from eggs can spike.
- **Labor:** Lean counter can run 22%-30% labor; brunch-style service often breaks the model.
- **Hours:** Most revenue 7am-2pm; catering can smooth weekday demand.
- **Real estate:** 600-1,400 sf near transit, hospitals, offices, colleges.
- **Franchise examples:** Egg N Bird, Eggs Up Grill, Another Broken Egg, Wake Up Call/coffee-adjacent concepts; many are not pure egg sandwich.
- **Financing:** SBA financeable if cash flow proven; de novo needs working capital buffer.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $750K-$2.5M with clean POS history and tax returns.
- SDE range: $150K-$500K after normalizing owner labor and manager replacement.
- Years in business: 3+ years, ideally survived one rent renewal.
- Route density / customer count: dense repeat customer base; email/SMS loyalty list; low dependence on paid acquisition.
- Key assets to verify: lease transfer, equipment condition, recipe/SOP ownership, supplier terms, reviews, POS cohort data, inventory quality.
- Red flags to avoid: owner-as-the-business, weak books, no manager, expiring lease, food safety issues, stale inventory, unsourced market-size claims.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top brands/competitors to review: McDonald’s breakfast, Dunkin, Starbucks, Einstein Bros, Bagel Guild/local bagels, Eggslut, Egg N Bird, Eggs Up Grill, local delis/bodegas.
- Typical franchise fee + royalty: food/beverage and resale franchises commonly show $30K-$60K fee and 5%-7% royalty; verify current FDD.
- FDD highlight: require Item 19 data, store-level labor/COGS, closure rates, transfer restrictions, required suppliers, and local territory protections.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: do not sign without 10+ franchisee calls and mature-unit AUV/SDE evidence.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors/roll-ups: McDonald’s breakfast, Dunkin, Starbucks, Einstein Bros, Bagel Guild/local bagels, Eggslut, Egg N Bird, Eggs Up Grill, local delis/bodegas.
- PE activity: limited in single-unit local concepts; more activity at franchisor/platform level and in refurb/ecommerce marketplaces.
- Fragmentation level: high; many independents and small franchisees.
- Moat: location, lease, brand/community, operating system, sourcing, reviews, speed, and manager discipline — not IP.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, seller note 10%-20%, lease assignment contingency, working-capital peg where relevant.
- SBA eligibility: SBA 7(a) supports changes of ownership up to $5M; startups/franchises require more equity and working capital.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: typically 10%-20% plus diligence fees and initial working capital.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years; kill if base case exceeds 5 years.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned only by adding manager depth and cleaner reporting.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build Boston/NE white-space map.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits, franchisee/reference calls.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision; require financing term sheet and lease diligence.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- No verifiable unit economics or FDD Item 19.
- Revenue depends primarily on owner personality, TikTok novelty, or one platform/channel.
- Rent occupancy above 10%-12% of sales for food/retail.
- Cannot support a paid manager while producing acceptable SDE.
- Seller cannot provide POS exports, tax returns, payroll, and supplier invoices.
- Entry price above 4.5x SDE without unusually strong, transferable management.
