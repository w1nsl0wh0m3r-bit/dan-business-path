# Generator Install / Maintenance Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium-High (public company data strong; local dealer economics require calls)*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **GO, WITH OPERATOR-PARTNER**
This is one of the best service businesses in the batch. Demand is driven by grid unreliability, storms, aging homeowners, and affluent suburban willingness to pay for backup power. The attractive part is not one-time installation; it is the installed base that needs annual maintenance, battery checks, transfer-switch service, and emergency response. **Best path:** acquire a Generac/Kohler dealer or electrical contractor with a generator specialty in New England at 2.5x-3.5x SDE, then professionalize recurring maintenance.

Use bottom-up: equipment OEM sales + local electrical/plumbing/HVAC labor + recurring maintenance contracts. New England storm/outage exposure makes the local market meaningfully better than national averages.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Prioritize:
- 500+ active maintenance customers; documented renewal rate.
- OEM certifications and warranty authorization.
- Mix of install + service + emergency calls; not just storm-chasing installs.
- Licensed electricians staying post-close.
- CRM/service software with customer equipment serial numbers and service history.
New England target pockets: coastal MA, RI, CT, NH/ME second-home markets, Vermont ski towns.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No transferable licensed operator / tech bench.
- Maintenance revenue <15% of gross profit or poorly documented.
- OEM relationship non-transferable or at risk.
- Business is owner-dependent for sales, estimating, and technical work.
- Purchase price >3.75x SDE without recurring contract proof.
