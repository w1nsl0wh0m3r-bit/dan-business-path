# Laundromat (Coin-Op / Wash-and-Fold) — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO
This is financeable, durable, and locally fragmented, but the real underwriting variable is lease/site control plus machine capex. Dan should only pursue with utility-heavy diligence and a credible wash-and-fold pickup/delivery growth plan.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map

- Neighborhood laundromats sell self-service washing/drying plus wash-and-fold, pickup/delivery, commercial accounts, vending, and sometimes dry-cleaning drop-off.
- Customers are apartment renters, dense urban households, students, service workers, Airbnb/short-term rental operators, salons, gyms, restaurants, and small hospitality accounts.
- Demand is recurring because laundry is weekly; NE/NY density is excellent, SFL has renter density and hospitality accounts, but utility costs and leases matter.
- CLA and industry sources describe a large U.S. coin laundry base with tens of thousands of stores; exact market size varies by definition, so treat local TAM by trade area and machine turns.

## 3. Unit Economics Model (Three-Case)

| Case | Revenue | SDE | SDE Margin | Entry Price | Multiple | Cash-on-Cash Year 1 |
|------|---------|-----|-----------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| Bear | $450K | $100K | 22% | $320K | 3.2x | ~25% |
| Base | $850K | $240K | 28% | $900K | 3.75x | ~50% |
| Bull | $1.35M | $405K | 30% | $1.82M | 4.5x | ~65% |

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan’s scale ($200K-$500K SDE), not platform multiples.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - annual debt service) / down payment; figures are directional and before taxes.
- Operator partner assumption: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; model includes manager/clinical/GM reality where relevant.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox

- Good target: $700K-$1.5M revenue, $180K-$450K SDE, 7+ years, long lease/options, modern equipment, clean utility bills, card/app payment data, and WDF growth capacity.
- Verify machine age, turns/day, repair logs, utility expense as % of sales, lease assignment, water/sewer exposure, and local demographic density.
- Franchise candidates: Speed Queen Laundry, WaveMAX, LaundryLuv, LaundroLab, Zoom Express Laundry. Diligence franchise fee, royalty/ad fund, equipment vendor economics, real estate support, and Item 19.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

- Competition is mostly local independents plus regional chains; scale players include Tide Laundromat, Speed Queen Laundry, WaveMAX, LaundryLuv, and pickup/delivery platforms like Poplin.
- PE/roll-up activity exists but is less mature than HVAC/restoration; fragmented mom-and-pop ownership remains common.
- Moat comes from lease/site, parking, equipment quality, cleanliness/safety, route density for WDF, and local reviews.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

- Prefer asset purchase with seller note; require landlord consent and lease term/options at least matching SBA amortization comfort.
- At $900K base price, 10% down is ~$90K before fees/working capital; machine replacement reserve must be modeled as real capex, not ignored.
- Base payback is ~3 years if WDF growth lands; no fantasy multiple expansion beyond 3.5x in to 4.0x out unless multi-store platform.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

- Days 1-30: map laundromats in dense Boston/NY/NJ/CT/SFL renter ZIPs; mystery shop pricing, cleanliness, machine mix, and WDF.
- Days 31-60: review broker deals, utility bills, machine age, lease terms, and pickup/delivery economics; interview operator candidates.
- Days 61-90: LOI only if lease, utilities, machine capex, and manager-adjusted SDE all clear.

## 8. Kill Criteria

- Lease term/options insufficient or landlord uncooperative.
- Machines require near-term replacement not reflected in price.
- Revenue mostly cash with weak proof.
- Utility costs rising faster than pricing power.
- Manager wages wipe out cash-on-cash return.
