# Spray Tan / Red Light / Body Contouring — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Conditional: attractive cash-on-cash only if acquired below 4x SDE with recurring memberships and a non-clinical operator; avoid medspa-priced assets and equipment-fad studios.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A neighborhood aesthetic/wellness studio selling UV-free spray tanning, red-light therapy, body contouring and add-on memberships. The investable version is a small multi-service studio with repeat packages, not a single-device fad.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C women 18-55, bridal/event customers, fitness/wellness consumers and some B2B local partnerships with gyms, salons and wedding vendors.
- Demand drivers: event-driven beauty, non-invasive wellness demand, memberships/packages, rising consumer spend on appearance and local convenience.
- Geographic relevance: Strong in affluent NE suburbs, NYC/CT/NJ commuter towns and SFL where year-round appearance/wellness demand is deeper.
- Market size: IBISWorld and Grand View/IMARC place sunless tanning and body-contouring/wellness device markets in the multi-billion-dollar range globally/US directionally; local TAM should be estimated by affluent female population and boutique wellness density.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $500K-$1.5M studio revenue with 150+ active members or clearly repeat package buyers.
- SDE range: $200K-$600K normalized manager-run SDE; below that is too owner-job-like.
- Years in business: 4+ years, with at least 3 years of tax returns/POS data.
- Route density / customer count: 500+ active customers or 150+ members within a 15-minute affluent trade area; no dependence on one influencer/technician.
- Key assets to verify: POS/membership data, device leases, service room utilization, staff certifications, landlord consent, reviews, package liability.
- Red flags to avoid: revenue built on one charismatic owner, disputed device claims, expiring lease, heavy prepaid package liability, Groupon-driven acquisition.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: Palm Beach Tan (FDD franchise fee commonly around $30K-$45K plus royalties), Spray Tan USA/Sunless Inc. concepts, Body20/Hotworx as wellness analogs; verify current FDDs.
- FDD highlight: focus on Item 7 equipment/buildout, Item 19 if provided, membership churn and device refresh obligations.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only; if Item 19 is absent, validate with franchisee calls and treat franchisor examples as marketing.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top 5-10 competitors or roll-ups active in this space: Palm Beach Tan, Sun Tan City, Sugared + Bronzed, Restore Hyper Wellness, Hotworx, Body20, local salons/medspas.
- PE activity: Beauty/wellness franchisors have sponsor interest, but small tanning/body-contour studios remain fragmented and locally owned.
- Fragmentation level: High; brand chains exist but most submarkets have independents.
- Moat: membership base, reviews, staff consistency, device uptime, cross-sell menu and affluent local partnerships.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to retention; inventory/equipment and lease assignments diligenced separately.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if tax returns, lease/licensing and cash flow are clean; underwrite 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $75K-$250K plus working capital for a $750K-$2.0M entry price.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on manager-run SDE; walk above 5 years.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned by professionalizing reporting, de-risking staff/customer concentration and adding repeat revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build NE/NY/SFL competitor and pricing grid.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits; request tax returns, POS/customer cohorts, lease/equipment schedules and labor roster.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if manager-run SDE, debt service coverage and local whitespace survive diligence.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Manager-run SDE below $175K after replacing owner labor.
- Asking price above 4.5x verified SDE without exceptional recurring revenue.
- Seller cannot reconcile P&L to tax returns/POS/bank statements.
- Lease, license, medical/regulatory, insurance or equipment dependency cannot transfer cleanly.
- More than 40% of revenue from prepaid contour packages or a device whose claims/legal standing cannot be substantiated.
