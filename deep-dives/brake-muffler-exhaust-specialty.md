# Brake / muffler / exhaust specialty — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
A buyable cash-flow business, but category quality depends on whether it has broadened beyond commodity mufflers into full undercar/brake/service work.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A local automotive service operator focused on brake / muffler / exhaust specialty, typically delivered through a fixed shop, mobile technicians, or route-based fleet service model. The investable version has repeat B2B accounts, documented workflows, and a non-owner operator layer.
- Customers: B2B fleets/dealers/municipalities/contractors where available; B2C consumers are useful for fill-in demand but should not be the core underwriting pillar unless reviews and location are exceptional.
- Demand drivers: aging vehicle parc, deferred replacement cycles, labor shortage at dealers/shops, fleet uptime pressure, and recurring preventive maintenance. BLS flags ongoing technician demand in auto and diesel mechanics; Fullbay/ATRI report heavy-duty repair labor constraints.
- Geographic relevance: NE/NY/SFL all work, but the best nodes are dealer corridors, logistics hubs, contractor yards, ports, dense suburbs, and high-income commuter markets. NY/NJ and South Florida are especially relevant for fleet/dealer density; Boston/NE works if radius is tight.
- Market size: cite as directional only. IBISWorld covers car wash/detailing; Market.us estimates global detailing services at $43.2B in 2024 and $74.5B by 2034; diesel/heavy-duty repair sources point to tens of billions globally. Use local SAM by count of dealers/fleets, not national TAM theater.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue $1M-$2.5M; SDE $200K-$450K; 10+ years; 4+ bays; strong local reviews; verify technician count, ticket mix, lift condition, parts vendors, inspection/license rules, rent; avoid old muffler-only shops with declining car count and owner behind the counter every hour.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top candidates/adjacent brands: Midas, Meineke, Valvoline Instant Oil Change, Dipstx, Ziebart/Tint World/Maaco depending on subcategory.
- Franchise fee/royalty reference points: Midas and Meineke commonly screen around $35K franchise fee with royalties in the mid-single digits or variable structures; Valvoline fixed-center franchise requires much more capital and is not a mobile oil-change franchise; Dipstx is a mobile oil change concept with lower investment but requires validation.
- FDD highlight: require Item 19 revenue/profit by quartile, transfer/resale rules, local ad fund, fleet-account restrictions, protected territory, and required manager involvement.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
Midas, Meineke, Monro/Mr. Tire, Pep Boys, Firestone Complete Auto Care, local garages, dealers. Monro and Driven Brands demonstrate consolidation appetite. Moat is location, Google reviews, trusted techs, fleet accounts, and honest diagnostics.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
Asset sale or franchise resale. Midas/Meineke franchise resales require FDD review, transfer fee, royalties, brand standards. SBA works if financials are clean. Base case $945K purchase, ~$95K down, ~$144K annual debt service; payback ~3 years.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map 50 local operators across Boston/NE, NY/NJ/CT, and SFL; pull listings; scrape reviews; request franchise FDDs where relevant; run 5 operator calls.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator/lead-tech partner candidates; build ZIP-level route density model; obtain 3 seller CIMs or tax-return-backed broker packages; mystery-shop pricing and response time.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one disciplined LOI only if SDE is tax-return supported, operator coverage exists, and downside case survives SBA debt service; otherwise archive thesis or convert to watchlist.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Kill if car count is falling, paid manager margin <10%, lease renewal is unsafe, royalty burden destroys economics, reputation is below 4.5 stars, or specialty demand is being absorbed by general repair/tire chains.
