# Painting contractor (residential) — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Pursue at Dan's acquisition scale. The decision hinges on transferable cash flow, a real operator/foreman layer, and whether the category has repeat/contract revenue rather than just owner heroics.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Painting contractor (residential) is a local service business where demand is generated by convenience, compliance, repair urgency, or home upkeep. The attractive version is systematized enough for an operator partner to run day-to-day.
- Who are the customers? Mix of B2C households and B2B/commercial accounts depending on category; the best targets have some repeat commercial/property-manager/fleet component.
- Demand drivers: aging assets, convenience expectations, regulatory requirements, weather exposure, and high-trust local reputation.
- Geographic relevance: Strong in Boston/NE, NY/CT/NJ and SFL affluent suburbs; repaint cycles, move-in/move-out and HOA standards support repeat demand.
- Market size: Residential painting is a fragmented local contractor category with attractive lead-to-crew scaling if estimating, scheduling and quality control are systematized. The risk is labor/subcontractor quality, not demand.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: see buybox below; generally $500K-$4M revenue depending on ticket size.
- SDE range: $100K-$500K, with $200K-$500K preferred.
- Years in business: 3-5+ years minimum with reviews and repeat sources.
- Route density / customer count: enough density that a paid manager/dispatcher improves utilization rather than adds dead overhead.
- Key assets to verify: tax returns, job-level gross margin, CRM/call logs, reviews, licenses, insurance, equipment condition, employee/subcontractor retention.
- Red flags to avoid: owner-only technical delivery, unverifiable cash, poor reviews, unsafe/licensing gaps, and revenue that disappears when seller exits.

Good target: $800K-$2.5M revenue, $150K-$400K SDE, 5+ years, estimator/PM separate from owner, subcontractor bench, <2% callbacks, CRM and photo QA. Avoid cash books, owner as only estimator, and low-bid insurance/restoration work.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: CertaPro Painters (fee reported ~$65K; investment ~$171K-$320K; royalty 4%-6% plus ad funds), Five Star Painting/Neighborly, Fresh Coat Painters, 360 Painting. Request current FDDs; franchise ad fees can materially compress SDE.
- FDD highlight: request the current FDD, focus on Item 7 investment, Item 19 financials, Item 20 unit closures/transfers, and required local marketing spend.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use FDD data only as a starting point; normalize for local labor, ad costs, ramp time, and manager salary.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
CertaPro, Five Star Painting, Fresh Coat, 360 Painting, local independents, handyman platforms. Fragmented; PE rolls home services but painting remains local. Moat is estimate speed, review volume, project management and crew retention.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, 10%-20% seller note when possible, earnout only for retained customers/contracts/backlog, working-capital peg for inventory/AR-heavy models.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA 7(a)-financeable if legal, licensed, insurable and cash-flowing; model 10-year amortization at ~11% with 10% down.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $35K-$225K for $350K-$2.25M entry prices.
- Payback period at base case: often 2-4 years before growth, but only if the operator salary is already burdened or realistically added.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; do not underwrite sponsor-level exits.

Asset purchase with seller transition and earnout tied to booked backlog and crew retention. Base $220K SDE at 3.8x = $836K price, $84K down, ~$142K annual debt service; leaves ~$78K before improvements. Multiple arbitrage is 3.5x in to maybe 4x out if scaled professionally.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Gross margin below 35% or true SDE below 10% after PM salary
- Subcontractor churn or undocumented workers create compliance risk
- Lead gen entirely paid ads with weak reviews
- No estimator other than seller
- Callbacks/warranty repaint above 3%-5% of jobs
