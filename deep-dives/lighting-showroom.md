# Lighting showroom — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
CONDITIONAL because demand is real, but the deal only works with clean books, paid management, and evidence of repeatable customer acquisition. Underwrite acquisitions at 3.0-4.5x SDE and assume Dan can partner with an operator.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A retail/design showroom selling decorative lighting, fans, controls, bulbs, consultative design help and trade-builder relationships.
- Who are the customers? Homeowners, interior designers, electricians, builders, remodelers and architects.
- Demand drivers: Home renovation, aging housing stock, premium fixture upgrades, designer referrals and builder repeat accounts.
- Geographic relevance: Strong in affluent NE and SFL renovation markets; U.S. lighting fixture retail/showroom TAM must be proxied by fixture manufacturing/retail data and local renovation spend.
- Market size: cited sector proxies below; otherwise build local TAM bottoms-up from households, projects, trade accounts, tickets and frequency.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: base-case revenue band above, with stable or growing trailing-36-month sales.
- SDE range: $150K-$500K, cleanly proven after manager/operator salary.
- Years in business: 5+ years with transferable staff, reviews and vendor/customer relationships.
- Route density / customer count: concentrated local demand, repeat accounts, no single customer >10%-15% of revenue.
- Key assets to verify: POS/CRM exports, bank/tax reconciliation, leases, licenses, employee retention, vendor terms, reviews and lead sources.
- Red flags to avoid: owner-as-product, cash leakage, unverifiable add-backs, lease cliff, churn, weak staff bench or revenue dependent on paid ads only.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top franchise/brand candidates: Lighting One/CCA Global affiliated showrooms, Ferguson Bath/Kitchen/Lighting, electrical distributor showrooms; few classic franchises, so acquisition is favored.
- FDD highlight: require Items 5, 6, 7, 19 and 20; compare closures/transfers and local marketing requirements.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only when current; otherwise validate through franchisee calls and resale P&Ls.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors or roll-ups active in this space: Ferguson, Capitol Lighting, Visual Comfort galleries, Lamps Plus, local electrical distributors, Wayfair/Amazon, designer showrooms.
- PE activity: sponsor interest exists in scaled franchisors, home services and specialty retail platforms, but local independents remain fragmented.
- Fragmentation level: high locally; chains set pricing/service expectations but mom-and-pops still dominate many ZIPs.
- Moat: reviews, staff/vendor retention, trade/referral relationships, memberships/contracts, local SEO, installation/service quality and operating systems.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, seller note/earnout tied to retention, working-capital peg and training transition.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally eligible if operating for profit in the U.S., creditworthy and not ineligible; SBA 7(a) can fund ownership changes.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: 10%-20% equity plus working capital/reserve.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.5 years after debt service and manager salary.
- honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; value creation is systems and de-risking, not multiple fantasy.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests, review scraping and 10 operator/franchisee calls.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, local wage/vendor checks, site visits and lender pre-screen.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if source-level financials and operating proof clear kill criteria.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Seller cannot reconcile tax returns, bank deposits and POS/customer data.
- Manager-run SDE falls below $150K or DSCR below 1.25x after normalized labor.
- Revenue depends on owner relationships, unsustainable discounts, or one vendor/customer/channel.
- Lease/territory/franchise transfer terms are unfavorable.
- Reviews, employee churn or compliance/licensing issues suggest hidden service-quality risk.
