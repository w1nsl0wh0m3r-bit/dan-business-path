# Metal Supermarkets — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium-High*

## Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
Metal Supermarkets is a strong concept: B2B-ish, unsexy, small-order convenience, fragmented customer base, real gross margin disclosure, and better Dan fit than most retail franchises. The condition is price and territory. New-build economics may be fine, but Dan should prefer an existing resale or multi-unit development only after validating local small-quantity metal demand.

## Market Sizing
- Metal Supermarkets positions itself as the world's largest supplier of small-quantity metals, serving customers that traditional distributors often underserve: machinists, maintenance shops, manufacturers, schools, hospitals, artists, hobbyists, and small fabricators (Metal Supermarkets positioning summarized in web search results from company/customer materials).
- Broader market is industrial metals distribution/service centers, but Dan's relevant niche is small-quantity, cut-to-size local fulfillment — more comparable to specialty distribution + counter service than commodity metal wholesaling.
- Market size claim: no clean public TAM located for the small-quantity subniche; treat as local demand density underwriting rather than top-down market sizing.

## Unit Economics
- 2024 FDD initial investment: $340,500-$592,500; initial franchise fee $44,500 ([Franchise Chatter, citing 2024 FDD](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2025/01/24/fdd-talk-metal-supermarkets-franchise-costs-fees-average-revenues-and-or-profits-2024-review-2/)).
- Royalty: tiered monthly gross sales royalty of 6% up to $140,402, 5% from $140,403-$207,794, and 3% above $207,797; brand fund currently 2% of gross sales subject to annual cap; required local advertising after year one greater of $10K or 1% of gross sales ([Franchise Chatter](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2025/01/24/fdd-talk-metal-supermarkets-franchise-costs-fees-average-revenues-and-or-profits-2024-review-2/)).
- System growth: franchised outlets increased from 64 to 83 between 2021 and 2023; company-owned declined from 4 to 2 ([Franchise Chatter](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2025/01/24/fdd-talk-metal-supermarkets-franchise-costs-fees-average-revenues-and-or-profits-2024-review-2/)).
- Item 19 includes store gross margin, invoices/month, invoice value, gross profit/store, and gross sales for 55 stores operating 2020-2023, but detailed table is behind paywall in the public article. Need current FDD before final underwriting.
- Entry multiple if buying a resale: 3.0x-4.0x SDE for a stable franchise resale; avoid paying strategic distribution multiples.
- Manager-run economics: store requires inside sales/customer service + warehouse/cutting operations. If owner-operated SDE is $300K, deduct $90K-$130K GM/ops manager = $170K-$210K pre-debt cash flow.
- Payback: new-build all-in cash likely 4-6 years unless mature revenue ramps quickly; resale at <=3.25x manager-adjusted SDE can be 3-5 years.

- Online Metals / Metals Depot: ecommerce metal suppliers.

- Metal Supermarkets: retail/counter model, no minimum, cut-to-size.

## Operator Economics
This can be semi-absentee only after the counter/warehouse team is stable. The operator is not just a cashier; they manage inventory, saw operations, customer quotes, receivables, safety, and B2B repeat relationships. A weak manager destroys service reliability and shrink/margin.

## Acquisition Targets
Best path:
- Existing Metal Supermarkets resale with 3+ years operating history.
- Independent small-quantity metal supplier/cut-to-size shop where Dan can professionalize sales and inventory.
- Markets with dense small manufacturing, trades, universities, maker/hobbyist demand: Greater Boston outer ring, Worcester, Hartford, Providence, southern NH.

## Dan Fit — 4.2/5
Strong. It has PE-underwritable distribution economics, working-capital discipline, repeat B2B customers, and less food/labor chaos. Dan's consumer/multi-unit diligence maps well. Main watchout: inventory management and cyclicality tied to manufacturing/construction.

## Kill Criteria
- Item 19 shows weak median store-level gross profit after labor/rent.
- Franchisees report >50-hour/week owner dependence.
- Territory too large/weak or already picked over.
- Inventory turns poor; working capital materially above FDD estimate.
- Manager-adjusted SDE below debt service threshold.
