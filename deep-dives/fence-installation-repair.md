# Fence Installation / Repair — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Fencing is a fragmented, locally dense trade with storm, pet, pool-code, privacy, and commercial security demand. It works if bought as a sales/estimating + subcontracted installation engine, not as a hero-carpenter job.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A local contractor/service business selling inspection, estimating, repair, replacement, and maintenance for fence installation / repair. Delivery is field-labor heavy, but sales, scheduling, procurement, and QA can be systematized.
- Who are the customers? Mostly B2C homeowners and landlords, with B2B upside from property managers, HOAs, municipalities, retail centers, schools, and light commercial accounts.
- Demand drivers: aging housing stock, deferred maintenance, storm damage, insurance/remodel triggers, safety/liability concerns, and homeowner willingness to pay for curb appeal and asset protection.
- Geographic relevance: NE/NY are attractive because old housing stock, freeze/thaw, storms, dense suburbs, and affluent homeowners support demand. SFL is attractive where hurricane/HOA/commercial exterior maintenance matters; climate changes the service mix.
- Market size: use category-specific public/private reports as directional, not precise. For Dan's purposes, the actionable market is a 20-40 mile local service territory with enough $200K-$500K SDE owner-operated targets.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1.0M-$2.8M, with 3+ years of tax returns and job-level gross margin history.
- SDE range: $200K-$500K before acquisition financing; must still work after a paid GM/ops lead.
- Years in business: 7+ years, strong reviews, repeat/referral base, owner willing to transition relationships.
- Route density / customer count: concentrated county-level service area; no long deadhead routes; diversified homeowner and property-manager accounts.
- Key assets to verify: trucks/equipment, supplier terms, CRM/job history, Google Business Profile, licenses/insurance, warranties, crew/subcontractor relationships.
- Red flags to avoid: cash-heavy books, owner-only estimating, undocumented subs, warranty disputes, lead-gen dependency, no job costing, revenue concentration, or uninsurable work.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top brands/analogues: Top Rail Fence, Superior Fence & Rail, Budget Blinds-adjacent outdoor living, local fence contractors.
- Typical economics: franchise fees often $40K-$70K; royalties commonly 5%-8% of gross revenue plus 1%-3% brand/ad fund, but exact terms require FDD review.
- FDD highlight: demand Item 19 by cohort, not system average; compare gross profit before and after royalties, local ad spend, and owner/manager compensation.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors or roll-ups: Top Rail Fence, Superior Fence & Rail, Budget Blinds-adjacent outdoor living, local fence contractors; plus local Google-map incumbents, exterior remodelers, roofers/pavers/foundation contractors, and one-truck specialists.
- PE activity: home-services consolidation is active, but this exact niche is still mainly local/regional. Strategic roll-ups value clean reporting, density, and manager-run branches.
- Fragmentation level: high. Most markets have dozens of small operators, a few polished local brands, and limited true institutional competition.
- Moat: review density, fast estimates, documented crews/subs, supplier pricing, commercial relationships, before/after proof, warranty process, and disciplined job costing.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase, 10%-20% seller note, transition services agreement, working-capital peg, and earnout only if tied to retained gross profit/revenue quality.
- SBA eligibility: generally strong if tax returns support cash flow; include vehicles/equipment and avoid overvaluing unverifiable goodwill.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: about $75K-$200K for a $750K-$2.0M enterprise value with 10% down plus closing costs and working capital.
- Payback period at base case: roughly 2-4 years on equity if SDE holds and debt service is covered; longer if a full-time GM is added immediately.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy at ~3.5x verified SDE, professionalize reporting/marketing, and maybe exit at ~4x. Do not underwrite fantasy platform multiples.

- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Find operator partner or GM candidate, complete 3-5 operator calls, review sample job files, and model SBA debt with paid management.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Seller cannot reconcile tax returns to P&L and job-level gross margins.
- More than 35% of revenue depends on paid leads that cannot be repeated at current CAC.
- Owner personally sells/estimates/manages every meaningful job with no second layer.
- Warranty callbacks or bad reviews suggest poor workmanship.
- Base case fails SBA debt service after paying a real manager/operator.
- Local market has high ad costs, low review whitespace, and no commercial route-density angle.
