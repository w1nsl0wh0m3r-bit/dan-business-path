# Holiday Lighting Installation — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
CONDITIONAL — seasonal cash machine (Oct-Jan) with 60-70% gross margins and low barriers to entry, but pure-play requires off-season revenue or a complementary service business (landscaping, irrigation, outdoor lighting). Best as an add-on service to an existing home services platform, not a standalone acquisition.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- **What is this business?** Residential and commercial holiday lighting installation, removal, and storage. Crews of 2-4 installers deploy temporary LED lighting systems on homes, businesses, and municipal buildings from October through January. Some operators sell/own the lights; others use client-owned product. Many franchise systems bundle design, installation, removal, and storage into an annual contract.
- **Who are the customers?** B2C affluent homeowners ($150K+ HHI) who value convenience and safety over DIY. B2C HOA communities for common-area installations. B2B commercial properties, strip malls, car dealerships, office parks seeking curb appeal during holiday season.
- **Demand drivers:** Consumer time scarcity, safety concerns (roof/ladder injuries), LED adoption making permanent/reusable lighting viable, HOA mandates, commercial property competition for foot traffic. Demand is inherently seasonal but highly predictable — same clients re-sign annually.
- **Geographic relevance:** Strongest in cold-weather markets where homeowners don't want to freeze on ladders (NE, Midwest). NE/NY/CT suburban markets are prime — high income, older housing stock, strong holiday decoration culture. SFL is weaker (less seasonal urgency, flatter architecture). Boston Metro, Fairfield County CT, Long Island, North Shore Chicago are top markets.
- **Market size:** Estimate $3-5B US seasonal holiday lighting services (estimate). No clean NAICS code. Christmas Decor reports 400+ franchise territories. IBISWorld estimates Christmas/holiday decoration services at ~$2.5B including product (estimate). Growing 4-6% annually with LED and smart-lighting adoption.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- **Christmas Decor** — 400+ franchisees, national footprint, strongest brand in the space. Founded 1986. Backed by no major PE firm (family-owned).
- **Independent operators** — Vast majority of the market. Local landscapers, handymen, and seasonal crews dominate. High fragmentation = consolidation opportunity.
- **Landscaping companies** — Natural off-season service. Many landscapers already offer holiday lighting as winter revenue. These are your primary competitors AND potential acquisition targets.
- **DIY / Home Depot / Lowe's** — Lower-end consumer segments will always DIY. Service targets the convenience buyer, not the price buyer.
- **PE activity:** Minimal pure-play PE interest in holiday lighting. Some landscaping platforms (Yellowstone Landscape, Terracare) bundle holiday services as add-on revenue.
- **Moat:** Client relationships and re-sign rates, geographic density (crew routing efficiency), Google Maps visibility, insurance/compliance barriers (workers comp for elevated work), proprietary design software.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note (common — 20-40% seller financing). Working capital adjustment for seasonal inventory.
- SBA eligibility: Challenging for pure seasonal business. Lender wants to see year-round revenue or a complementary service. Best combined with landscaping, irrigation, or outdoor lighting business.
- Down payment at Dan's scale: $25K-$77K (10% of $250K-$770K entry).
- Payback period at base case: 2.5-3.5 years (high-margin seasonal business pays back fast if recurring).
- Multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3x SDE, build to multi-crew platform, sell to landscaping roll-up at 4-5x.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- **No viable off-season revenue.** A pure seasonal play without landscaping, outdoor lighting, or another spring/summer service is an 8-month cash drain. If you can't fill Feb-Sep, the economics don't work.
- **Re-sign rate below 55%.** If the target's historical client retention is under 55%, the business is a churn machine disguised as recurring revenue.
- **Insurance/workers comp is unobtainable or prohibitive.** Roof and ladder work carries real injury risk. If workers comp premiums exceed 8-10% of revenue, margins compress below viability.
- **Owner is the crew.** If the seller personally installs 50%+ of jobs, you're buying a seasonal job, not a business. Must have transferable crews or documented hiring pipeline.
- **Market with <6 months operating season.** Warm-weather markets (SFL, Gulf Coast) don't have enough seasonal urgency to sustain the model. Stick to cold-weather NE/Midwest.
