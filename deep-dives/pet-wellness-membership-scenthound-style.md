# Pet Wellness Membership / Scenthound-Style Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: High for franchise economics; Medium for independent replication.*

## 1. Executive Verdict — GO, WITH DISCIPLINE
This is one of the cleaner consumer-services concepts for Dan: recurring revenue, simple service menu, non-veterinary wellness framing, and a large resilient pet market. Scenthound’s Item 19 shows real AUV dispersion, but the model is legible. The right play is either a de novo franchise in a dense affluent market with strong operator, or buying an independent grooming shop and converting it toward membership hygiene. Avoid paying franchisor-level fees if local membership conversion can be executed independently.

## 2. Market Sizing
- APPA reports U.S. pet industry expenditures of roughly **$152B in 2024**, with 2025 projected around **$157B**; APPA’s “other services” bucket includes grooming, boarding, insurance, training, sitting, and walking ([APPA industry stats](https://americanpetproducts.org/industry-trends-and-stats)).
- APPA/industry summaries cite **94M U.S. pet-owning households** in the 2025 State of the Industry context ([Petfood Industry](https://www.petfoodindustry.com/pet-food-market/market-trends-and-reports/news/15741428/report-pet-ownership-expands-as-gen-z-shifts-trends)).
- Capital One Shopping’s APPA-derived summary estimates other pet services at **$13.0B in 2024** and **$13.5B in 2025**, including boarding, grooming, insurance, training, sitting, and walking ([Capital One Shopping](https://capitaloneshopping.com/research/pet-spending-statistics/)).

## 6. Operator Economics
This concept can work manager-run, but not passively. A GM must handle scheduling, membership conversion, retention, labor utilization, and angry pet-parent edge cases. Pay $65K-$90K plus bonus. If unit EBITDA before GM is below $120K, Dan owns a job-shaped annuity with franchisor fees attached.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Best buy path: independent groomer doing $400K-$900K revenue with 2+ groomers, recurring clients, and room to add monthly bath/nail/ear/teeth plans.
- Multiples: 2.0-3.0x SDE for small salons; 3.0-3.5x for clean books and manager in place.
- Look for: low owner grooming hours, groomer retention, appointment backlog, customer list/SMS/email consent, affluent dog density.
- Deal sources: BizBuySell, local brokers, vet referrals, pet industry Facebook groups, retiring groomers.

## 8. Dan Fit — 4.3 / 5
Strong fit. It is recurring consumer services with franchise/FDD diligence, fragmented acquisition path, and good demographic alignment. Main weakness: labor quality and execution details matter more than spreadsheet elegance.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- Actual GM-run EBITDA below $75K at average unit.
- Groomer turnover >40% annually or chronic unfilled staffing.
- Membership churn >6-8% monthly with heavy promo dependency.
- Franchisor requires local ad spend that prevents unit-level profitability.
- Acquisition seller is the lead groomer and plans to exit immediately.
