# Mobile Drug Testing / DOT Compliance Screening Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium (market sources public; franchise unit economics opaque)*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **CONDITIONAL / SMALL GO**
This is a gritty B2B compliance service with recurring demand, low capex, and decent Dan-fit if sold into fleet operators, construction, logistics, schools, municipalities, and healthcare staffing. The concern: many locations are small owner-operator jobs, not manager-run assets. **Best path:** acquire or build a local B2B compliance platform with recurring employer accounts, not a walk-in lab dependent on owner sales.

## 2. Market Sizing
- Grand View Research reports the U.S. drug testing industry is expected to grow; its public snippet notes consumables were the largest product segment at **33.21% in 2025** and urine samples were **57.15%** of sample revenue in 2025.
- Research and Markets reports the **U.S. drug-of-abuse testing services market** is expected to reach **$1.29B by 2030**, growing at **4.65% CAGR from 2025-2030**.
- Coherent Market Insights pegs the global drug testing market at **$15.90B in 2026** and **$22.95B by 2033**; this is global, not U.S. services only.
- DOT compliance creates non-discretionary demand in transportation and safety-sensitive industries.

## 6. Operator Economics
A $600K revenue unit at 25% SDE margin generates $150K. Hire a combined sales/ops manager at $80K fully loaded and Dan’s cash flow is only ~$70K before debt service. This becomes attractive when there are **contracted employer accounts** and the operator can also sell. Purely passive ownership is weak; operator-partner structure is better.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- 100+ active employer accounts; revenue concentration <20% top customer.
- DOT consortium/random testing administration, not just one-off collections.
- Mobile capability with route density around trucking, construction, ports, logistics hubs.
- Clean compliance records and documented chain-of-custody SOPs.
Avoid:
- Retail walk-in labs with no B2B recurrence.
- Owner as only salesperson.
- Revenue dependent on a single occupational-health contract.

## 8. Dan Fit — **3.5 / 5**
Good B2B recurring compliance angle and low capital intensity. Less good: small scale, sales grind, and limited brand equity. This is a practical cash-flow niche, not a sexy platform unless rolled up regionally.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No recurring employer accounts / random testing pools.
- Manager-run cash flow below $75K after debt service.
- Compliance documentation is sloppy.
- Customer acquisition relies on Dan/operator cold-selling forever.
- Franchise FDD shows weak Item 19 or high churn.
