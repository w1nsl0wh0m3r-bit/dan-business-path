# Tennis / Padel Academy Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium — tennis data is strong; padel U.S. unit economics are still thin.*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
This is attractive for Dan only as an **asset-light academy/operator partnership** or acquisition of an existing profitable tennis instruction business, not as a ground-up padel real-estate build. Tennis participation has real secular momentum, affluent Northeast demand fits Dan’s network, and coaching businesses can throw off solid cash if court access is secured. The red flag is facilities: owning/leasing dedicated courts can turn a good instruction business into a capex-heavy real estate bet. Padel is exciting but early and likely overcapitalized in premium metros.

## 2. Market Sizing
- USTA reported U.S. tennis participation has grown for five consecutive years, with 23.8M players in 2023 and 33M+ total players/returning players in broader participation reporting. Source: USTA participation reporting ([USTA](https://www.usta.com/)).
- SFIA/USTA reporting has repeatedly highlighted tennis as one of the fastest-growing traditional participation sports post-COVID.
- Padel U.S. market sizing is less reliable. Public commentary describes rapid court growth from a tiny base; treat any U.S. TAM claims as **estimates** unless verified through court-count datasets or club-level financials.
- New England demand should skew favorable: high-income suburbs, private club density, youth sports spend, and long indoor season supporting premium indoor clinics.

## 3. Unit Economics
**Instruction-led tennis academy model, leased court blocks:**
- Revenue: $300K-$900K per academy depending on court access, lesson density, camps, and private lesson mix.
- Gross margin: 45-60% after coach pay if court rental is variable; lower if fixed lease commitments are high.
- SDE: $80K-$250K for local owner-operated academies; manager-run SDE after a $70K-$100K program director likely $40K-$150K.
- Entry multiple: 2.0-3.0x SDE for small local instruction businesses; 3.0-4.0x for sticky junior academy with contracted court access and coach bench.
- Payback: 2.5-4.5 years if acquired at 2.5-3.0x and no major lease capex.

**Padel club model:**
- Franchise/club investment can be far above Dan’s target. PeerSense lists Conquer Padel Club at ~$1.1M-$3.0M total investment, $60K franchise fee, 7% royalty ([PeerSense](https://peersense.com/franchise/conquer-padel-club)).
- Dedicated padel clubs require court buildout, zoning, landlord cooperation, and demand ramp. SDE can be strong at mature utilization, but downside is ugly because fixed rent and buildout amortization dominate.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **Local tennis academies / club pros** — incumbent relationship businesses; often fragmented and personality-led.
- **USTA Net Generation / local clubs** — ecosystem partner/competitor; drives youth pipeline.
- **Lifetime / Equinox-style racquet clubs** — premium integrated facilities in affluent markets.
- **Reserve / Padel Haus / Conquer Padel** — emerging padel club/franchise operators; more real-estate-heavy.
- **Private country clubs** — not direct acquisition targets but compete for affluent juniors and adult clinics.

## 6. Operator Economics
A qualified program director/head pro will cost $70K-$120K plus incentives. If the business only earns $150K owner-operated SDE, manager-run economics compress badly. The buy box needs either: (i) $250K+ owner-operated SDE with transferable coach bench, or (ii) seller willing to stay through transition and train operator.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for tennis academies with:
- 3+ years revenue history, $400K-$1M revenue, $150K-$300K adjusted SDE.
- Contracted court access or long relationship with clubs/schools.
- Multiple coaches, not founder-only lessons.
- Camps/clinics/private lessons mix; prepayment packages; junior pipeline.
Sources: BizBuySell/local broker searches, club-owner referrals, USTA section contacts, accountant/lawyer networks.

## 8. Dan Fit — 3.8 / 5
Strong fit on affluent consumer services, recurring youth spend, and diligence pattern recognition. Weak fit if he has to solve coaching culture or facilities. Best version: partner with a charismatic head pro and buy/roll a small academy.

## 9. 90-Day Roadmap
1. Call 10 New England tennis directors/head pros; ask about court access, camp economics, coach compensation.
2. Pull BizBuySell + local broker listings for tennis instruction/racquet clubs.
3. Build a unit model with three scenarios: leased courts, revenue-share club partnership, dedicated facility.
4. Identify one operator candidate; test willingness to run day-to-day for salary + profit share.
5. Verify padel supply/demand by visiting Boston/NYC/CT clubs and asking about utilization by time block.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No transferable court access or landlord/club contract.
- >60% revenue tied to owner’s personal lessons.
- Dedicated facility requires >$500K equity before proof of demand.
- Coach labor market cannot support non-owner delivery.
- Padel pro forma assumes >55% prime-time utilization within year one without waitlist evidence.
