# Garage Living / Home Organization — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Good affluent-home demand and visual selling, but discretionary remodel cyclicality and lead-gen dependence mean Dan should prefer buying a profitable installer/dealer over paying full freight for a new franchise territory.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Garage flooring, cabinets, slatwall, overhead storage, closets/pantries, mudrooms, and broader home organization design/install.
- Who are the customers? Affluent homeowners, downsizers, new movers, luxury property managers; B2B builders/designers as referral partners.
- Demand drivers: Aging-in-place decluttering, high home equity, remote-work/home-improvement spend, EV/tool/sports storage, and garage-as-room trend.
- Geographic relevance: Best in Boston/NE affluent suburbs, NY/CT/NJ density, and SFL where property/consumer demand supports premium pricing.
- Market size: Home organization is a large but fuzzy category; underwrite by affluent household count, remodel permits, Google search volume, and franchise/dealer AUVs.

- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; the model deducts enough management burden to avoid buying a job.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1M-$3M
- SDE range: $200K-$450K
- Years in business: 3+ with installer crews and designer/estimator
- Route density / customer count: Affluent zip clusters; 300+ completed installs; designer/referral base
- Key assets to verify: Showroom/van/tools, install crews, supplier contracts, before/after portfolio, CRM, reviews
- Red flags to avoid: Paid-lead CAC dependence, no installer retention, warranty/floor coating failures, showroom lease overhang

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
Garage Living, Tailored Living/PremierGarage, Closets by Design, California Closets, The Tailored Closet. Verify FDD investment, royalty, lead support, protected territory, Item 19, and required showroom costs.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors / roll-ups: California Closets, Closets by Design, The Container Store custom spaces, local cabinet/epoxy installers, Tailored Living/PremierGarage, GarageExperts
- PE activity: Home-services PE interest exists in remodel/installation platforms; category still locally fragmented.
- Fragmentation level: Moderate/high: national closet brands plus many local garage/flooring installers.
- Moat: Design trust, install quality, reviews, local showroom/referrals, supplier access, fast quote-to-install.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note/holdback, transition services, employee retention, and working-capital peg.
- SBA eligibility and terms: Usually eligible if profitable, transferable, and clean books; use 10-year / ~11% / 10% down.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: $60K-$180K for a $600K-$1.8M EV target.
- Payback period at base case: Base case 3-5 years.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3-4x SDE, professionalize, and exit around 4x if growth/manager depth is proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Request FDDs, map affluent zip competition, mystery-shop 10 quote flows.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Analyze resale vs new franchise; interview franchisees and local installers.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Proceed only if CAC and installer retention support manager-run cash flow.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- No Item 19 or weak franchisee validation
- CAC payback longer than 12 months
- Installer subcontractor churn or warranty defects
- Showroom/buildout exceeds model
- Revenue tied to pandemic remodel pull-forward
