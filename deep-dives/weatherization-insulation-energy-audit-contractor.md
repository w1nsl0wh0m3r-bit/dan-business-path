## 2. Market Sizing
- **Weatherization services:** FMI estimates U.S. demand at **$12.8B in 2026**, growing to **$18.1B by 2036** at **3.5% CAGR** ([FMI](https://www.futuremarketinsights.com/reports/united-states-weatherization-service-market)).
- **Service mix:** FMI states insulation upgrades represent **29%** of service-type demand and spray foam **36%** of material segment share in 2026 ([FMI](https://www.futuremarketinsights.com/reports/united-states-weatherization-service-market)).
- **Demand drivers:** aging housing stock, high energy costs, federal/state efficiency programs, and consumer utility-bill pressure. DOE's Weatherization Assistance Program is a durable public-sector demand anchor, but private-pay and Mass Save-style utility programs are more relevant for a small acquirer ([DOE WAP](https://www.energy.gov/scep/wap/weatherization-assistance-program)).

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **USA Insulation** — insulation franchise focused on residential retrofit; sells injection foam, attic insulation, air sealing. Franchise route may help brand/lead-gen but adds royalty drag.
- **Koala Insulation** — home-service insulation franchise, often paired with Attic Pros / property services ecosystem; attractive model but young-unit dispersion risk.
- **Dr. Energy Saver** — dealer/franchise-like building-science network under Contractor Nation; strong diagnostics, basement/attic cross-sell.
- **Installed Building Products / TruTeam** — national/semi-national insulation installers; more new construction and large-account oriented.
- **Local Mass Save / utility-program contractors** — real threat and acquisition pool; program approval can be a moat.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- 8+ years operating history; 2+ crews; foreman/production manager not seller-dependent.
- Utility-program certification / approved contractor status.
- Mix of attic insulation, air sealing, duct sealing, audits; avoid pure spray-foam commodity shops.
- CRM/job-costing data; warranty/complaint history clean.
- Low concentration in one utility program or one general contractor.

Avoid:
- Seller as only estimator/building-science brain.
- Revenue inflated by one-time federal/utility rebate spike.
- Unresolved worker classification, OSHA, or moisture/mold claims.
