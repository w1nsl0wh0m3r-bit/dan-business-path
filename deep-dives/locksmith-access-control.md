# Locksmith / Access Control — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Category rank: 24 | Blended score: 3.53*

## 1. Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
Traditional locksmithing alone is too fragmented, low-ticket, and polluted by spammy lead-gen operators. But **locksmith + commercial access control** can be a real small-business platform: higher tickets, recurring service relationships, credential management, doors/hardware, cameras/intercom adjacency, and commercial property-manager relationships.

**Dan-specific take:** Avoid emergency residential locksmith roll-ups. Buy or build around commercial accounts: multifamily, schools, medical offices, small offices, property managers. The acquisition target should look more like a light security integrator than a guy in a van.

## 2. Market Sizing
- Locksmiths are tracked as a distinct industry by IBISWorld ([IBISWorld catalog](https://www.ibisworld.com/)).
- Electronic access control is part of the broader security systems/services market; demand is supported by commercial property turnover, workplace security, multifamily credentialing, and migration from mechanical keys to cloud/mobile credentials.
- New England has a dense base of older commercial buildings and multi-family properties where doors/hardware upgrades can be recurring.

**Working local market estimate:** a city/suburban territory can support multiple $1-3M revenue locksmith/security integrators; pure emergency locksmithing stays much smaller unless lead-gen heavy.

**Operator economics:** $1.5M revenue / 18% SDE = $270K. Replace owner with $90K ops/sales lead and true cash flow may be **$150-210K** if tech bench is intact. If owner is master locksmith and salesperson, cash flow can collapse.

## 4. Competitive Landscape
- **Pop-A-Lock** — national locksmith franchise; consumer/emergency heritage, automotive/residential/commercial mix.
- **The Flying Locksmiths** — franchise emphasizing commercial locksmithing and access control.
- **Local institutional locksmiths** — old-line shops with property manager/school/municipal relationships.
- **Security integrators** — compete on electronic access, cameras, alarms, intercoms.
- **Hardware/door companies** — overlap on commercial doors, closers, panic bars.

## 6. Operator Economics
Need either (a) licensed master locksmith/technical manager, or (b) commercial security project manager. Budget **$85-120K** in New England. Dan can own the KPI layer: booked backlog, service gross margin, project gross margin, quote-to-close, AR, tech utilization, recurring commercial customers.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
**Look for:** commercial mix >60%, access-control revenue >20% or clear capability, property manager relationships, recurring service contracts, multiple licensed techs, supplier relationships, strong local SEO without spam tactics.

**Avoid:** residential lockout SEO arbitrage, automotive-only key fob dependence, owner-held licenses, poor online reputation, project gross margins below 25%, receivables >60 days.

**Multiple:** 2-3x for traditional locksmith; 3-4x for clean commercial/access-control shop with staff and backlog.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- Residential emergency >50% revenue.
- Owner is only licensed/qualified technical person.
- Lead-gen dependency or fake-location SEO footprint.
- Access-control projects have no job costing.
- Purchase price >3.5x SDE without commercial recurring base.
