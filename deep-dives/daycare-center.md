# Daycare Center — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Demand is real and recurring, but this is a licensing/labor/real-estate business masquerading as education. Pursue only with an experienced director/operator and a center with enrollment upside, clean licensing, and defensible rent.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A daycare center provides licensed care for infants, toddlers, preschoolers, and sometimes after-school children. Revenue is weekly/monthly tuition; quality, staffing ratios, and licensing compliance determine capacity.
- Who are the customers? Customers are dual-income families, single working parents, employers/benefit partners, and subsidy programs. B2C recurring tuition dominates; subsidy exposure varies by state and target market.
- Demand drivers: working-parent labor participation, high cost/scarcity of care, universal pre-K policy shifts, and preference for center-based care. Child Care Aware documents affordability/supply constraints, and BLS tracks childcare worker labor pressure.
- Geographic relevance: Boston/NY suburbs with high income and childcare scarcity are attractive; SFL demand exists but tuition/wage/rent tradeoff must be local.
- Market size: large national childcare market, but underwrite at the catchment level: licensed capacity, waitlists, household income, employer density, and state subsidy/pre-K impact.

| Case | Revenue | SDE | SDE Margin | Entry Price | Multiple | Cash-on-Cash Year 1 |

| Bear | $900K | $90K | 10% | $270K | 3x | ~185% after ~$40K debt service |
| Base | $1.80M | $270K | 15% | $1.01M | 3.8x | ~118% after ~$151K debt service |
| Bull | $3.00M | $600K | 20% | $2.70M | 4.5x | ~73% after ~$402K debt service |

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE, with larger bull cases shown where the category can stretch). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - estimated debt service) / down payment; assumes 90% SBA debt and no large incremental capex shock.
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — the question is whether the business has a durable manager/producer/director bench.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Buy $1M-$3M revenue, $200K-$600K SDE, 5+ years under clean license, 70-150 capacity, 75%-90% enrollment with room to improve, director not seller, stable teacher roster, long lease or owned RE, and strong parent reviews. Avoid weak licensing history, infant-room staffing losses, bad lease, subsidy-only customer base, and seller-director dependency.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Franchise candidates: The Goddard School, Primrose Schools, Kiddie Academy, Lightbridge Academy, Creative World School, Celebree School. FDDs often show significant initial investment and royalties; new-build franchising is capital-heavy, so Dan’s path is more likely acquisition of an existing center or director-led partnership.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- KinderCare, Bright Horizons, Learning Care Group, Primrose, Goddard, Kiddie Academy, Cadence Education, Busy Bees, local Montessori/childcare centers. PE/platform ownership exists at larger chains, while local centers remain fragmented. Moat is licensed capacity, reputation, waitlist, director/teacher retention, curriculum, and facility location.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Deal structure should include license-transfer condition, lease/landlord consent, seller note, enrollment true-up, staff retention plan, and working-capital peg for prepaid tuition. SBA can finance acquisition and sometimes real estate via 504/7(a). A $1.0M base price needs ~$100K down; payback often 3-5 years because capex/staffing reserves matter. Do not underwrite like a passive annuity.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map local competitors, licenses/regulatory requirements, broker listings, franchise FDD requests, and 20-30 possible operators/partners.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build acquisition model, diligence 3-5 targets, verify customer/revenue retention, speak with lenders, and test operator partner fit.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit LOI on first target or reject category; include seller transition, retention/earnout mechanics, and diligence gates.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Any serious licensing violations or abuse allegations
- Director/teachers likely to leave at close
- Enrollment below breakeven with no waitlist
- Lease renewal or zoning uncertainty
- Margins depend on underpaid staff/ratio violations
- Universal pre-K policy will directly cannibalize target classrooms
