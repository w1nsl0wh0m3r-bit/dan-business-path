# Specialty Coffee / Espresso Bar — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Good consumer habit business only if acquired below 4x verified SDE with a strong lease, real morning throughput, and a manager-ready operating model. Avoid beautiful cafés that are actually owner jobs with rent risk.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Independent or small-chain espresso bars selling espresso drinks, drip coffee, pastries, limited food, beans, catering, and sometimes subscription/mobile-order revenue.
- Who are the customers? Primarily B2C neighborhood/commuter customers; secondary B2B office catering, events, wholesale beans, and landlord/office amenity programs.
- Demand drivers: Daily/weekly habit, premiumization, remote-work neighborhood traffic, mobile ordering, loyalty apps, and attach of food/pastry/beans.
- Geographic relevance: Best in dense NE/NY/NJ/CT walkable neighborhoods and affluent commuter nodes; SFL works in office/tourist corridors but lease discipline matters.
- Market size: U.S. coffee shop market is commonly estimated around $45B+ (IBISWorld/industry reports); National Coffee Association reports coffee is the top daily U.S. beverage after water; cite market size as estimate unless using paid IBISWorld.

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down; SBA says 7(a) can fund changes of ownership and caps at $5M.
- Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite post-manager cash flow, not owner heroics.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $750K-$1.5M per store with 3+ years of POS data.
- SDE range: $150K-$350K after normalizing owner labor and manager cost.
- Years in business: 3+ years, ideally survived COVID/post-COVID traffic shifts.
- Route density / customer count: 250-600 daily tickets; 1,500+ loyalty members; morning rush not dependent on owner.
- Key assets to verify: lease/options, espresso equipment, POS by daypart, labor schedule, COGS, loyalty list, wholesale/catering accounts.
- Red flags to avoid: rent >12% of sales, prime cost >70%, no POS export, owner is head barista/customer magnet, short lease, inconsistent hours.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Scooter's Coffee: drive-thru coffee franchise, franchise fee often cited ~$40K; royalties around 6%; strong drive-thru AUV claims in FDD summaries.
- Biggby Coffee: franchise fee commonly ~$25K-$30K; royalty around 6%; lower buildout than large café boxes.
- Black Rock Coffee Bar: larger investment, royalty/ad fees in FDD summaries; more western U.S. footprint.
- PJ's Coffee: franchise fee around $35K; royalty around 5%; New Orleans-style positioning.
- FDD highlight: require current Item 19; compare AUV by store age and drive-thru vs café format, not blended system averages.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: Starbucks, Dunkin', Blue Bottle/Nestlé, Blank Street, Gregorys, Joe Coffee, La Colombe, Tatte, local independents.
- PE/activity: consumer roll-ups exist but coffee has uneven multi-unit returns; Blank Street is venture-backed, Tatte/La Colombe have institutional ownership histories.
- Fragmentation: highly fragmented below national chains; independent cafés dominate neighborhood differentiation.
- Moat: lease/site, speed, barista retention, loyalty data, local brand, daypart discipline, and operational simplicity.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for unverifiable add-backs; stock purchase only if licenses/contracts require it and liabilities are clean.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if profitable, owner-operated or manager-run with qualified operator, and no speculative real estate component; SBA 7(a) supports ownership changes up to $5M.
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus working capital; for a $700K-$1.4M enterprise value, plan $90K-$250K cash need.
- Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on equity after debt service.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, manager bench, clean reporting, and repeat revenue are proven.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map 50 independent cafés in Boston/NYC suburbs; pull broker comps; request Scooter's/Biggby/PJ's FDDs; benchmark rents and daily tickets.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview 5 operators, identify operator partner, build lease/throughput model, inspect POS exports from any target.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit IOI only for clean lease + $150K+ manager-adjusted SDE or reject category until better target appears.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Rent above 12% of sales or lease term/options under 5 years.
- Daily tickets below 225 without clear growth lever.
- Manager-adjusted EBITDA below 8%.
- Seller cannot reconcile POS, bank deposits, and tax returns.
- Equipment capex over $75K in first 18 months.
- Brand depends on seller personality or one star barista.
