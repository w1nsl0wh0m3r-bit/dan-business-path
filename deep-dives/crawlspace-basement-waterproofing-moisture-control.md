# Crawlspace / Basement Waterproofing & Moisture Control — Deep Dive

*Generated: 2026-05-25 ET | Rank: #11 | Blended Score: 3.75 | Confidence: Medium — industry sizing is adjacent-category heavy*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **CONDITIONAL GO**

This is a real “ugly home-services” category with demand rooted in water intrusion, mold fear, resale inspections, New England housing stock, and increasingly intense rainfall. It fits Dan’s preference for local services, modest capital, repeatable selling systems, and operator partnership. The catch: it is **sales-machine-first**, not a cute technical trade. Bad operators overpromise, crews cut corners, lead costs can spike, and warranty liabilities can quietly eat SDE.

**Verdict:** pursue only if buying a company with (i) clean warranty history, (ii) documented close rates by channel, (iii) crew-level gross margin visibility, and (iv) owner role already mostly sales/estimating rather than field labor.

## 2. Market Sizing

- The broad waterproofing systems market is large: Grand View Research publishes a global waterproofing systems market report and frames demand around construction, infrastructure, and water-ingress protection ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/waterproofing-systems-market)). This is broader than residential basement work, so use only as directional support.
- Residential waterproofing demand is tied to the U.S. repair/remodeling market. Harvard JCHS’ LIRA tracks U.S. owner improvement and repair spending in the hundreds of billions annually ([Harvard JCHS LIRA](https://www.jchs.harvard.edu/research-areas/remodeling/lira)). Basement/crawlspace work is a niche within this.
- New England is structurally attractive: older housing stock, freeze/thaw cycles, high water tables in coastal markets, sump pump demand, radon/mold adjacency, and stormwater issues. Treat the serviceable regional market as a local radius business, not a national TAM story.

## 6. Operator Economics

If Dan buys a $1.5M revenue / 18% SDE shop = $270K SDE and pays a qualified GM/sales manager $90K-$130K plus bonus, owner-discretionary cash flow may fall to **$130K-$180K** before debt service. At a $700K purchase price with SBA-style debt, free cash flow can get tight unless growth is immediate.

Better structure: seller stays 6-12 months; hire operations lead first; Dan or fractional sales manager owns estimating cadence until close-rate stability is proven.

## 7. Acquisition Targets

**Look for:**
- 8+ years operating history
- 100+ five-star Google reviews
- Mix of waterproofing, sump, crawlspace encapsulation, dehumidification; avoid heavy structural/foundation jobs unless licensed expertise is deep
- Warranty reserve and claims log
- Crew foreman bench; not just owner + helpers
- Lead source breakdown: Google Local Services, referrals, realtor/home inspector channels

**Avoid:** businesses where the owner personally closes every job, warranty liabilities are undocumented, or revenue is dominated by one insurance/restoration relationship.

## 10. Kill Criteria

- Warranty claims >3%-5% of revenue with no reserve.
- Owner does >50% of sales and no estimator bench.
- Gross margin below 35% after normalized crew labor.
- Heavy structural engineering exposure without licensed staff.
- Revenue relies on storm events or paid leads with CAC not tracked.
