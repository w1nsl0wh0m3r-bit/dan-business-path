# Men's Health / Hair Restoration / TRT Clinic — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Commercially attractive but compliance-heavy. The investable version is an operator/physician partnership with conservative medical governance, not a bro-marketed TRT mill. Hair restoration can be high-ticket; TRT adds recurring revenue and regulatory risk.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
Clinics sell testosterone evaluation/therapy, ED care, weight/body composition, hair restoration/transplants, PRP and prescription hair-loss protocols. Customers are adult men; B2C cash-pay dominates with some insurance. Demand drivers: aging male population, telehealth normalization, GLP-1/body-composition adjacency, aesthetics destigmatization. Market size: Grand View Research estimates global men’s health market at $40B+ and hair restoration services market at multi-billion-dollar scale; U.S. clinic SAM is an estimate. Sources: https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/mens-health-market-report and https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/hair-restoration-services-market

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1.5M base target; avoid subscale unless cheap.
- SDE range: $200K-$500K+ preferred; normalize for paid manager/operator.
- Years in business: 3+ years with tax returns, POS/CRM, lease/territory control.
- Route density / customer count: dense local territory; recurring or repeat customers documented.
- Key assets to verify: tax returns, bank deposits, customer cohorts, leases/territories, equipment, licenses, staff retention.
- Red flags to avoid: seller-as-business, unverifiable add-backs, declining traffic, capex cliff, regulatory ambiguity, franchisor transfer issues.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
Bosley, HairClub, Restore Hair, Gameday Men’s Health, Low T Center-style clinics, Limitless Male, QC Kinetix adjacent. Fees/royalties vary widely; Gameday franchise portals cite franchise fee often ~$49.5K and royalties/marketing to verify; medical FDDs and state CPOM rules are mandatory diligence.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
Bosley, HairClub, Hims & Hers, Ro, Gameday Men’s Health, Low T Center, local urology practices, medspas, dermatology/hair transplant surgeons. PE is active in medspa/derm/urology/MSO platforms. Moat is compliant medical oversight, patient acquisition CAC, retention, outcomes, physician relationships and brand trust.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase where possible, seller note/earnout for transition risk, working-capital peg, non-compete and training agreement.
- SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: SBA 7(a): eligible for changes of ownership up to $5M; source: https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans/7a-loans
- Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus diligence/legal and working capital reserve.
- Payback period at base case: usually 2.5-5 years if bought at 3.5-4.0x verified SDE and manager cost is real.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: underwrite ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; do not assume institutional roll-up multiples.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping in Boston/NE first; scrape brokers/franchise resale listings; request FDDs where applicable; build source-of-truth comp table.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator partner profile; review 5-10 actual P&Ls; site visits/customer mystery shops; build 3-case model.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Pick LOI target or franchise signing decision; negotiate seller note/training; run legal/regulatory review before exclusivity.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- No clean tax-return support for SDE or revenue.
- Paid-manager model fails debt service.
- Customer demand is fad-driven with weak repeat behavior.
- Transfer requires franchisor/landlord consent on unattractive terms.
- Required capex exceeds modeled reserve or creates >5-year payback.
- Legal/compliance structure is unclear or dependent on aggressive interpretation.
