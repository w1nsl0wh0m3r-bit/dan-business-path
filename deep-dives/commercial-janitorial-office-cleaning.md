# Commercial Janitorial / Office Cleaning — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: GO
Boring, fragmented and financeable, with real recurring revenue if contract quality is high. The catch is labor execution and low bid pressure; Dan should only pursue route-density contracts with documented retention and a manager/supervisor layer.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- Nightly/weekly office, medical, school, bank, gym and light-industrial cleaning plus floor care, consumables and occasional specialty disinfection.
- Customers are B2B property managers, offices, medical clinics, schools, banks, HOAs and small facilities.
- Demand drivers: facilities require recurring cleaning, tenant expectations, compliance-sensitive medical/daycare sites and outsourced non-core labor.
- Geographic relevance: strong in dense NE/NY/SFL office/medical corridors; suburban route density matters more than macro office headlines.
- Market size: BLS tracks hundreds of thousands of janitors/cleaners; IBISWorld/Statista describe commercial cleaning as a large U.S. market. Treat exact size as directional.

- Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — don't penalize lack of direct experience


## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $1.5M-$5.0M with 50+ recurring accounts.
- SDE range: $250K-$700K after paid operations manager and field supervisors.
- Years in business: 7+ years; 3+ years of customer retention data.
- Route density / customer count: accounts clustered by city/corridor; no customer >15% revenue.
- Key assets to verify: signed contracts, cancellation terms, payroll taxes, worker classification, cleaning checklists, supervisor bench, bid history.
- Red flags to avoid: underbid contracts, cash payroll, uninsured subs, owner does nightly inspections, high office-vacancy exposure.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- JAN-PRO, Jani-King, Coverall, ServiceMaster Clean, Anago Cleaning Systems, Stratus Building Solutions.
- FDD focus: master/subfranchise economics, royalty/lead fees, account assignment rules, Item 19 retention and litigation history.
- Franchise fees vary materially by master vs unit franchise; verify FDD and avoid economics where franchisor owns the customer relationship.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: ABM, ServiceMaster Clean, Jani-King, JAN-PRO, Coverall, Anago, Stratus, local independents and facility-management firms.
- PE activity: facility services roll-ups and route-based service platforms are active; larger platforms seek dense recurring contract books.
- Fragmentation: high below national accounts.
- Moat: contract retention, supervisor quality, route density, vertical specialization, insurance/compliance and reliable staffing.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal: asset purchase, seller note/earnout tied to account retention, working-capital peg and assignability/customer-consent condition.
- SBA eligibility: generally yes; lender will scrutinize contract transferability, payroll compliance and customer concentration.
- Down payment required: ~10%-15% plus payroll float.
- Payback period at base case: 3-4 years if retention holds.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy 3.0-3.75x; exit ~4x if recurring book is diversified and professionally managed.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map NE/NY/SFL janitorial targets; request FDDs; build customer-vertical thesis around medical/schools/banks.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Review contracts, churn, payroll, supervisor structure and nighttime QA process; interview operator partner.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI with retention earnout and customer-consent diligence before close.

## 8. Kill Criteria
- Contract book not assignable or mostly month-to-month with weak retention.
- Payroll/tax/worker classification exposure.
- Owner is de facto operations manager and night supervisor.
- Gross margin too thin after market wages.
- Customer concentration or office-vacancy risk breaks base case.
