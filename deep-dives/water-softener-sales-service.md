# Water Softener Sales & Service — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Good recurring-service potential in hard-water markets, but customer acquisition and equipment-sales cyclicality can make it look better than it is. Prefer buying an installed-base dealer with salt/filter routes over starting from zero.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? This business sells, installs, rents, and services water softeners, filtration, reverse osmosis, whole-house systems, salt delivery, filters, and maintenance plans. Customers are homeowners, small commercial users, restaurants, hospitality, and light industrial accounts. Demand drivers are hard water, PFAS/contaminant awareness, aging municipal infrastructure, and recurring consumables. NE/NY is strong for water-quality anxiety/PFAS; SFL is relevant for whole-house filtration and luxury homes; hard-water intensity varies by county. Market size claims vary by research firm; treat national water treatment equipment/services as a multi-billion-dollar market and build local TAM from household count and hardness data.
- Who are the customers? Mix varies, but target underwriting should separate residential emergency demand from B2B/property-manager/municipal accounts.
- Demand drivers: needs-based failure, compliance/inspection triggers, aging infrastructure, and recurring maintenance or consumables where applicable.
- Geographic relevance: NE/Boston exurbs and NY/CT/NJ are primary for this batch; SFL is category-specific and weaker for basement/private-well models.
- Market size: use cited national installed-base indicators where available; otherwise treat as local serviceable-market estimate and do bottom-up ZIP/county mapping.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue $1.2M-$3M, SDE $250K-$500K, 7+ years, 1K+ installed base, clear rental/service recurring book, trained installers, WQA-certified staff, diversified lead sources. Avoid dealer with one manufacturer lock-in, high door-to-door CAC, weak service attach, or aggressive financing complaints.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Culligan, Kinetico, RainSoft/EcoWater-style dealer networks are the main branded routes. Public estimates cite Culligan total investment roughly $125K-$800K and royalties that vary by product/service; current FDD/dealer agreement is mandatory. Item 19 must separate equipment sales from recurring rentals/service.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Culligan, Kinetico, EcoWater/RainSoft dealers, Pentair/A.O. Smith channel partners, plumbing/HVAC contractors, big-box retailers, Amazon/DTC filtration brands, and local independents. PE/strategics have consolidated water-treatment dealers periodically; fragmentation remains high. Moat is installed base, recurring consumables, local water knowledge, brand trust, and fast service.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset purchase or franchise resale; verify inventory, rental equipment ownership, and financing receivables. SBA works but working capital/inventory and warranty obligations need reserves. Base deal at ~$1.3M needs ~$128K down; payback 3-4 years if service mix sticks. Multiple arbitrage only if recurring service book is converted into documented subscription revenue.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Days 1-30: map water hardness/PFAS concern counties, list dealers, request Culligan/Kinetico/RainSoft materials, call WQA-certified pros. Days 31-60: operator partner search, verify service attach, CAC, installer utilization, and cancellation/refund history. Days 61-90: LOI on dealer with installed-base data or pass.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Recurring service under 25% of gross profit
- Lead generation dependent on high-pressure in-home sales
- No installed-base data
- Manufacturer/dealer agreement non-transferable
- Warranty/complaint history ugly
