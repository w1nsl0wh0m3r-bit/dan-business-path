# Mobile Auto Detailing / Ceramic Coating — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Low-capex and high-margin as a startup, but acquisition quality is mixed; buy only a brand with repeat fleet/high-end customer base, not a solo operator job.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? Mobile Auto Detailing / Ceramic Coating is a local services / consumer operating business built around paid, repeatable service delivery rather than a pure ecommerce product.
- Who are the customers? B2C car enthusiasts, busy affluent households, luxury owners; B2B small fleets, dealers, marinas, office parks, property managers.
- Demand drivers: Demand from vehicle ownership, high used-car prices, luxury-car protection, convenience, fleet image, and ceramic coating awareness. NE/NY/SFL relevance: SFL year-round detailing and luxury cars; NE/NY seasonal salt/detailing and affluent suburbs. Market size estimates vary; IBISWorld/Grand View describe U.S. car wash/detailing and ceramic coating as multi-billion categories.
- Geographic relevance: NE/Boston and NY/CT/NJ are primary screens where density/income support premium service; SFL is relevant where weather, demographics, or affluent consumer behavior supports demand.
- Market size: cited sources below or explicitly labeled estimates; no fake precision.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Good target: $500K-$1.2M revenue, $150K-$350K SDE after route manager, 3+ years, 1,000+ customer database, fleet contracts, 2-4 crews, strong reviews/photo portfolio. Verify repeat rate, weather cancellations, coatings warranty, insurance, employee vs contractor status, van/equipment condition. Avoid one-person brands, price shoppers, no CRM, chemical/environmental compliance gaps, or revenue tied to seller charisma. Franchises: DetailXPerts (franchise fee often ~$35K; royalty per FDD), Spiffy (fleet/mobile platform), Tommy’s/No-H2O-style eco detailing, Ziebart (shop/protection, larger capex), Tint World (adjacent automotive styling). Request Item 19 and fleet/territory economics.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- See named franchise systems above; request current FDD, Item 19, closure history, royalty/ad fund, territory rules, and required capex before any commitment.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Competitors: local mobile detailers, DetailXPerts, Spiffy, Ziebart, Tint World, car washes, dealer detail departments, ceramic specialists. PE more active in car wash/tint/protection than tiny mobile detailers. Fragmentation is extreme. Moat: reviews, premium portfolio, fleet contracts, route density, technician training, and memberships.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Asset purchase; seller note tied to customer retention. SBA can finance if books are clean, but do not finance a solo job at 4x. Base down payment ≈$75K plus van/working-capital reserve. Payback ~2 years if recurring/fleet revenue holds. Multiple arbitrage possible only by professionalizing dispatch and adding crews.

## 7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)
- Phase 1 (Days 1-30): map SFL/NY/NE luxury/fleet density, franchise FDDs, fleet pricing, insurance/environmental rules.
- Phase 2 (Days 31-60): find operator partner with detailing/auto-service background; interview 5 operators; model crew productivity.
- Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI only on a multi-crew target with CRM proof, or run a low-capex demand pilot.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- NO-GO if owner performs most work, repeat revenue <25%, reviews weak, weather cancellations crush NE utilization, no fleet contracts, coatings claims/warranties unclear, or paid ads are the only lead source.
