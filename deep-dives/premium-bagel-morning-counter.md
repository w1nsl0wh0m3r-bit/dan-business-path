## Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL / Mostly NO for acquisition dashboard
The customer vibe is excellent and New England can support premium bagels, but as an acquisition category for Dan this is inferior to compliance/service/distribution. Food labor, early hours, buildout risk, freshness waste, and manager dependence are real. Only pursue if buying a beloved, profitable existing shop with an operator-chef/GM and clear multi-unit whitespace.

## Unit Economics
- Franchise search summary: Big Apple Bagels average gross revenue ~$781K, royalty 5%; Manhattan Bagel average revenue per unit ~$536K, investment $330K-$506K; Bagel Boss investment ~$500K-$970K, royalty 4.5%; Jeff's Bagel Run investment $435K-$713K, royalty 6% + 2% brand fund. These need FDD verification before underwriting.
- Independent premium bagel shop model: $700K-$1.3M revenue possible in strong locations; food/paper COGS 25%-35%; labor 25%-35%; occupancy 8%-12%; EBITDA 8%-18% if well-run.
- SDE range for one strong shop: $100K-$250K. Truly great shops can exceed this, but those are usually founder-driven and expensive.
- Entry multiple: 2.0x-3.0x SDE for single-unit food service; 3.0x-4.0x only for clean books, manager-run operations, brand moat, and documented systems.
- Manager-run economics: if owner-operated SDE is $200K, deduct $70K-$100K GM = $100K-$130K pre-debt. At 3x, debt service can wipe out most cash flow.
- Payback: 4-7 years for new buildout; 3-5 years for cheap acquisition with stable GM. Bad locations never pay back.

## Operator Economics
A bagel shop is not passive. The operator must manage overnight/early production, bakers, counter throughput, catering, food safety, local marketing, ordering, and waste. Dan can partner with an operator, but then he owns a low-multiple restaurant job unless the concept scales.

## Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- Existing beloved local bagel/cafe with $150K-$300K SDE.
- Books reconcile to POS/bank deposits; cash leakage resolved.
- Lease assignable with 5+ years control and renewal options.
- Production process documented; baker/GM staying.
- Catering/office breakfast revenue and online ordering.
- Ability to add second unit or commissary without brand dilution.
Deal sources: local restaurant brokers, direct outreach to owner-operated shops, landlord referrals, Toast/POS-adjacent networks.

## Dan Fit — 3.3/5
Vibe fit is high; investment fit is mediocre. Dan understands consumer/multi-unit and brand, but one shop does not justify his opportunity cost. A premium morning-counter platform could work with an operator-founder. Buying a single bagel shop as a semi-absentee PE analyst is a trap unless the numbers are unusually clean.

## Kill Criteria
- Owner is head baker and brand personality.
- No GM/baker retention post-close.
- SDE < $175K unless purchase price is very low.
- Lease has <5 years control or landlord recapture risk.
- Food/labor cost not tracked weekly.
- Concept requires new buildout >$500K without proven AUV.
