# Septic / Sewer Inspection & Maintenance — Deep Dive

*Generated: 2026-05-25 ET | Rank: #12 | Blended Score: 3.73 | Confidence: Medium-High*

## 1. Executive Verdict — **GO, but buy carefully**

Septic and sewer inspection/maintenance is one of the better “boring local infrastructure” categories for Dan. Demand is recurring, regulatory, inspection-driven, and less discretionary than many home services. New England is attractive because many towns outside dense urban cores rely on septic, housing stock is old, and property transactions trigger inspections.

The main problem is labor/equipment intensity. A pump truck is not a Shopify store. This is a dispatch, compliance, and field-safety business. Still, with a real operator, it can work.

## 2. Market Sizing

- EPA notes that more than **one in five U.S. households** depends on an individual septic system or small community cluster system ([EPA Septic Systems](https://www.epa.gov/septic)). That supports a large installed base requiring pumping, inspection, repair, and compliance work.
- IBISWorld publishes a dedicated U.S. septic, drain and sewer cleaning/maintenance category, but full market data is paywalled; cite only as confirmation that the category is separately tracked ([IBISWorld](https://www.ibisworld.com/)).
- State-level regulation matters. Massachusetts Title 5 creates inspection obligations on property transfer and certain system changes ([MassDEP Title 5](https://www.mass.gov/guides/title-5septic-systems)). That is a real demand catalyst in Dan’s target geography.

## 6. Operator Economics

A $1.6M revenue septic/sewer business at 20% SDE produces $320K SDE before manager normalization. Hire a GM/dispatcher-ops lead at $85K-$120K plus bonus and budget $40K-$80K annual normalized fleet capex. Manager-run cash flow may land at **$150K-$210K pre-debt**. That can still work if purchase price is disciplined and seller financing/SBA amortization is sane.

## 7. Acquisition Targets

**Look for:**
- Route density by town/ZIP
- Recurring commercial/municipal accounts
- Pumping + inspection + repair referrals
- Clean environmental/disposal compliance
- Fleet age, maintenance logs, CDL/operator availability
- Transfer-inspection relationships with realtors and inspectors

**Multiples:** 2.5x-3.5x SDE for sub-$500K SDE local shops; lower for owner-operator with aging trucks, higher for dense recurring route books.

## 10. Kill Criteria

- No route density; long windshield time kills margin.
- Owner is only licensed/qualified operator.
- Fleet requires major near-term replacement not reflected in price.
- Disposal contracts unstable or expensive.
- Environmental/compliance record is messy.
