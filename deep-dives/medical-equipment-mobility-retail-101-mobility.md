# Medical Equipment / Mobility Retail / 101 Mobility — Deep Dive
*Generated: 2026-05-25 | Confidence: Medium-High*

## Executive Verdict — CONDITIONAL GO
This is attractive if focused on private-pay mobility/accessibility, not reimbursement-heavy durable medical equipment. 101 Mobility has real scale, a disclosed FDD, and aging-demographic tailwinds. The risk is technician availability, local referral generation, and margin compression from equipment suppliers/online competitors.

## Market Sizing
- Relevant demand drivers: aging population, aging-in-place preference, home accessibility retrofits, stairlifts, ramps, vertical platform lifts, bathroom safety, patient lifts.
- Public search found 101 Mobility at 177 franchised outlets and 16 company-owned outlets at year-end 2023, showing national footprint and category maturity ([Franchise Chatter, citing 2024 FDD](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2024/12/03/fdd-talk-101-mobility-franchise-costs-fees-average-revenues-and-or-profits-2024-review/)).

- Item 19: 2022/2023 average, median, highest, lowest APR sales for 56 franchisees operating 172 franchised businesses; Franchise Chatter headline says average sales per territory of ~$706K; Frandera search result cites median revenue $611,961 ([Franchise Chatter](https://www.franchisechatter.com/2024/12/03/fdd-talk-101-mobility-franchise-costs-fees-average-revenues-and-or-profits-2024-review/), [Frandera](https://frandera.com/franchise/101-mobility/fdd)).
- Gross margin estimate: equipment retail + installation often 35%-50% gross margin depending on supplier mix, stairlift rentals, used/refurbished equipment, and installation labor utilization.
- SDE range: mature territories plausibly $100K-$250K owner-operated at $600K-$1M revenue; top operators higher, but must validate actual EBITDA in FDD/franchisee calls.
- Manager-run economics: if owner-operated SDE is $200K, subtract $80K-$110K GM/sales manager = $90K-$120K before debt. This is thin unless territory is >$750K sales or multi-territory.
- Entry multiple: existing independent mobility/DME business 2.0x-3.5x SDE; 101 Mobility resale 2.5x-4.0x if clean and growing.

## Operator Economics
A qualified operator needs sales discipline and technical install management. The business is referral-driven: hospitals, PT/OT networks, senior living, elder-law attorneys, discharge planners, VA, and family caregivers. Pure retail-store management is not enough.

## Acquisition Targets
Two paths:
1. Buy a 101 Mobility resale or open territory after franchisee calls.
2. Buy an independent accessibility/DME shop and focus away from Medicare reimbursement toward private-pay stairlifts/ramps/lifts.
Look for: high private-pay mix, certified installers, supplier relationships, rental revenue, used-equipment resale, strong Google reviews, referral sources.

## Dan Fit — 4.0/5
Good fit. It has consumer/service overlap, demographic tailwind, and professionalization potential. Dan can hire an operator. It is emotionally positive without being mission-washed. The financial concern is whether one territory can support a paid GM and acquisition debt.

## Kill Criteria
- Revenue relies materially on Medicare/insurance reimbursement complexity.
- Franchisees say owner sales involvement is mandatory.
- Median territory cannot clear $150K manager-adjusted EBITDA.
- Lead cost rising with weak conversion.
- Install tech hiring is a persistent bottleneck.
- Bad supplier concentration or long inventory delays.
