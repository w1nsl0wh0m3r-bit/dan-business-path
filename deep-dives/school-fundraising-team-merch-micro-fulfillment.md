This is a **conditional GO only if framed as a local sales-and-fulfillment platform**, not a generic custom apparel shop. The attractive version owns relationships with athletic directors, booster clubs, PTAs, youth sports leagues, and coaches; uses online stores; outsources or semi-automates production; and repeats seasonal campaigns. ## 3. Unit Economics
**Local merch/fundraising operator estimate:**
- Revenue: **$500K-$2.5M**.
- Gross margin: **30%-55%** depending on in-house print/embroidery vs outsourced production and fundraiser profit split.
- SDE: **10%-25%** if owner-led sales; lower if paying sales reps.
- SDE target: **$150K-$350K** more common than $500K.
- Entry multiple: **1.8x-3.0x SDE** for small owner-led print/fundraising shops; higher only with recurring digital stores and non-owner sales.
- Payback: **2.5-4 years**.

**Campaign math estimate:** 200 items x $35 AOV = $7K gross sales; 40% gross margin = $2.8K gross profit; school/team keeps $1K-$2K; platform keeps $800-$1.8K after production/logistics depending model.

## 4. Competitive Landscape

## 6. Operator Economics
Example: buy $1.2M revenue / 18% SDE = **$216K SDE** at **2.5x = $540K EV**.
- Hire GM/sales operator: **$70K-$100K + commission**.
- Remaining pre-debt cash: **$115K-$145K**.
- Dan's year-1 after debt could be thin, but downside is manageable and capital intensity can be low if production is outsourced.

## 7. Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- Existing school/team accounts with annual repeat campaigns.
- Online store workflow and clean order-level data.
- Minimal inventory risk; made-to-order or batch windows.
- One salesperson/operator who can own local relationships.
- Ability to expand from apparel into signs, banners, awards, uniforms, and corporate local promo.

Avoid:
- Pure job-shop screen printer dependent on owner/artwork heroics.
- Heavy equipment capex with low utilization.
- No repeat revenue data.
- Race-to-bottom commodity T-shirt pricing.

## 8. Dan Fit — 3.3 / 5
Dan's consumer/retail pattern recognition helps, but this is a hustle business. The ceiling may be too low unless rolled up regionally or combined with a youth sports services platform.

## 9. 90-Day Roadmap
1. Map New England school districts, athletic conferences, booster clubs, and youth sports leagues.
2. Interview ADs/coaches: current vendors, pain points, fundraising economics.
3. Pull current FDD for Fundraising University.
4. Source local screen printers with school account concentration.
5. Build prototype online store model with outsourced production to validate sales cycle.
6. Test 5 pilot campaigns via warm local sports connections.

## 10. Kill Criteria
- No repeat account base.
- Gross margin below 35% after fundraiser split.
- Owner personally drives all sales relationships.
- Production complexity overwhelms relationship-sales thesis.
- $500K SDE path requires too many micro-accounts.
