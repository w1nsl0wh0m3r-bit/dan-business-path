# Window Washing — Deep Dive Investment Memo

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL
Attractive for route-density recurring revenue in commercial/upscale residential, but only if Dan acquires an established book with recurring contracts (not one-off residential calls). Operator-partner model works; solo-operator book is a job, not an investment. Best path: buy a commercial + HOA-route window cleaning business with 100+ recurring accounts.

## 1. Category Definition & Demand Map
- What is this business? A window cleaning service provides residential and commercial exterior/interior window washing, often supplemented by gutter cleaning, pressure washing, and seasonal services. Revenue comes from recurring contract accounts (quarterly/biannual for commercial, seasonal for residential) and one-time jobs. Route density and crew efficiency drive margins.
- Who are the customers? Split: 40-60% commercial (retail storefronts, office buildings, property managers, HOAs) and 40-60% residential (homeowners, especially upscale). Commercial accounts are recurring and lower-margin but predictable; residential are higher-ticket but less predictable.
- Demand drivers: Window cleaning is recurring by nature — glass gets dirty regardless of economy. Commercial storefronts require clean windows for presentation; HOAs mandate regular cleaning; upscale homeowners value the service. Recession-resistant compared to discretionary home improvement. Growth driven by new construction (more glass in modern builds) and aging population outsourcing physical tasks.
- Geographic relevance: All Dan-target markets work. NE/Boston has dense suburban residential with older housing stock (high window counts). NY/CT/NJ has commercial corridors. SFL has year-round demand and HOA density. Window cleaning demand scales with property density and income levels.
- Market size: The window cleaning industry in the US is estimated at $9-12B annually (IBISWorld OD4521, Window Cleaning Services). Highly fragmented — no single operator holds even 1% market share. Estimate based on ~150,000 window cleaning businesses in the US averaging $60K-$80K revenue each.

## 4. Path-Specific Buybox
What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?
- Revenue range: $400K-$1.0M with 60%+ commercial/HOA recurring accounts.
- SDE range: $120K-$300K; verify by adjusting for owner labor replacement.
- Years in business: 5+ years with established route territory and customer history.
- Route density: accounts within 20-30 minute drive radius; tight enough for 2-3 crews.
- Key assets to verify: customer list with service history and contract terms, crew and equipment (ladders, water-fed pole systems, vans/trucks), scheduling software/CRM, Google reviews (4.5+ stars, 100+ reviews), recurring revenue schedule.
- Red flags to avoid: owner does all the cleaning himself (job, not business); no documented customer list; revenue is 80%+ one-time residential with no recurring contracts; seasonal-only revenue with no commercial base; crew turnover >50% annually.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top franchise brands: Fish Window Cleaning (largest franchise, ~275 locations), Window Genie (neighborhood home services franchise, now part of Neighborly), ANEW Cleaning & Restoration (smaller franchise).
- Typical franchise fee: $30K-$60K; royalties 5%-8%. Fish Window Cleaning total investment $100K-$200K per FDD.
- FDD highlight: Fish has Item 19 financials showing average unit revenue. Window Genie bundles window cleaning with pressure washing and gutter services for higher ticket.
- Franchise vs independent: franchise provides brand, training, and systems but adds 7-10% royalty drag on margins. For a route-density business with low barriers, independent acquisition may offer better economics if systems already exist.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Top competitors: Fish Window Cleaning (franchise, largest national brand), Window Genie (Neighborly), local/regional independent operators (vast majority of market), handyman services (indirect competition), property management companies (in-house crews for large portfolios).
- PE activity: Minimal at Dan-scale. Neighborly (backed by Harvest Partners / Roark Capital) owns Window Genie but focuses on multi-brand platform. No significant PE roll-up activity below $5M EBITDA.
- Fragmentation level: Extremely high. 150,000+ window cleaning businesses in the US, most are owner-operators with 1-3 employees. Very few regional players exceed $2M revenue.
- Moat: route density and scheduling efficiency, Google reviews and local SEO, crew quality and reliability, customer relationships (especially commercial/HOA). Low barriers to entry but also low switching costs — the moat is execution density, not IP.

## 6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline
- Typical deal structure: asset purchase — customer list, equipment, vehicles, brand/goodwill. Seller note 15-25% tied to customer retention at 6 and 12 months. Transition services agreement 30-90 days with seller on routes.
- SBA eligibility: strong. Route/service businesses with recurring contracts are among the most SBA-friendly categories.
- Down payment required: 10% of purchase price plus closing costs. On $630K base case, roughly $63K-$90K cash.
- Payback period at base case: 2-3 years if retention holds above 80% and crew costs are controlled.
- Honest multiple arbitrage: buy at 3.0-3.5x SDE, professionalize with CRM/routing software, add pressure washing/gutter services, tuck in smaller route, exit at 3.5-4.5x.

## 8. Kill Criteria
What would make this a NO-GO?
- Manager-adjusted SDE below $120K after paying crew leader $60K-$75K.
- Less than 50% recurring/contract revenue — indicates lifestyle business, not investable route.
- Owner is the sole cleaner with no crew — buying a job, not a business.
- No documented customer list, service history, or CRM — you're buying air.
- Local market is dominated by 1-2 scaled operators (Fish franchise or large regional) with no whitespace.
- Google reviews <3.5 stars or <50 reviews — indicates service quality issues that will be hard to fix.
- Seasonality creates >40% revenue swing with no commercial base to smooth cash flow.
