## Executive Verdict — GO
This is one of the cleanest acquisition-first categories for Dan. Demand is code-driven, recurring, local, and fragmented; the business can be run with a licensed technician/operator if Dan owns sales, M&A, finance, and route density. ## Market Sizing
- Fire protection businesses include fire alarms, extinguishers, suppression systems, inspections, and code compliance services. Synergy Business Brokers specifically markets fire-prevention companies with annual net income from $250K to $20M, implying an active lower-middle and SMB M&A market for this niche ([SynergyBB](https://synergybb.com/businesses-for-sale/fire-prevention-companies-for-sale/)).
- The U.S. market size should be treated as an estimate unless purchasing IBISWorld/market-research data. A practical TAM proxy is the installed base of commercial properties required to maintain extinguishers and suppression systems under NFPA/local fire codes.
- Local SAM is the useful number: restaurants, multifamily, offices, schools, industrial, healthcare, and retail accounts within a 60-90 minute route radius.

## Unit Economics
- Target acquisition: $250K-$500K SDE, ideally 60%+ recurring inspection/service revenue.
- Typical entry multiple: 2.5x-4.0x SDE for small route/service companies; pay toward high end only for dense routes, low owner dependence, clean customer lists, and technicians staying.
- Example listing comp: Synergy showed a sold fireproofing/insulation company at $3.74M revenue / $800K net cash flow / $2.8M ask, or ~3.5x cash flow ([SynergyBB](https://synergybb.com/businesses-for-sale/fire-prevention-companies-for-sale/)).
- Revenue mix: annual/semiannual extinguisher inspections, recharges, replacements, kitchen hood/suppression inspection, emergency lights, fire alarm/sprinkler inspection referrals or add-ons.
- Gross margin estimate: route labor + parts/service businesses can support 40%-60% gross margin; alarm/sprinkler install/project work is more volatile.
- Manager-run economics: on $350K SDE owner-operated, budget $85K-$120K for GM/lead technician. Residual pre-debt cash flow: ~$230K-$265K before acquisition debt service.
- SBA structure at 3.25x SDE on $350K = $1.14M EV. With 10%-15% equity and seller note, DSCR likely works only if adjusted EBITDA after manager is above ~$200K.

## Franchise Comp Table
Not a franchise-led category. Dan should buy a local route/service company rather than pay franchisor royalties for a compliance service where local licenses, route density, and technician retention matter more than brand.

## Operator Economics
Best model: Dan buys, retains seller 3-6 months, elevates a lead technician or hires a service manager. The operator must schedule routes, manage compliance paperwork, handle emergency calls, and sell replacement equipment. If the business requires Dan personally to hold the license or be the technical qualifier, kill it unless a long-term qualified manager signs.

## Acquisition Targets
Look for:
- $750K-$2M revenue, $250K-$500K SDE.
- 70%+ service/inspection/recurring revenue.
- 1,000+ customer locations or clearly route-dense territory.
- Service records in software, not paper-only.
- Seller willing to transition customer relationships.
Sources: BizBuySell/DealStream/SMERGERS, SynergyBB, niche brokers, NFPA/state fire-protection associations, direct mail to local extinguisher and suppression shops.

## Kill Criteria
- <50% recurring inspection/service revenue.
- Seller is the only licensed technician and will not stay.
- Customer list is paper-only or materially unverifiable.
- Revenue concentration >20% in one customer.
- Project/install backlog masquerading as recurring cash flow.
- No route density; technicians spend more time driving than servicing.
