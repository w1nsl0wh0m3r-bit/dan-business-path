Golf Simulator / Social Golf — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Acquisition and franchise underwriting memo for Dan-scale small business diligence.

CONDITIONALSBA / Operator Partner3-4.5x SDE

Verdict: CONDITIONAL

Interesting affluent leisure box if utilization and leagues/events are proven; dangerous as a tech-enabled bar with expensive rent and seasonal novelty risk.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Golf Simulator / Social Golf is a local services / consumer operating business built around paid, repeatable service delivery rather than a pure ecommerce product.
  • Who are the customers? B2C golfers, corporate events, bachelor/birthday groups, winter practice users; B2B lessons/coaches and leagues.
  • Demand drivers: Demand from golf participation growth, off-course golf formats, winter practice in NE, corporate events, and TrackMan/Full Swing realism. NE/NY relevance is high; SFL has year-round outdoor golf so indoor must be social/events. NGF reports off-course golf participation exceeding traditional on-course participation and Topgolf/pop-up simulator growth.
  • Geographic relevance: NE/Boston and NY/CT/NJ are primary screens where density/income support premium service; SFL is relevant where weather, demographics, or affluent consumer behavior supports demand.
  • Market size: cited sources below or explicitly labeled estimates; no fake precision.

**Key assumptions:**

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite manager-run cash flow, not founder heroics.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Good target: $900K-$1.8M revenue, $200K-$450K SDE after GM, 4+ bays, 2+ years utilization data, leagues/events booked, alcohol license clean, equipment age known. Verify bay utilization by hour, F&B gross margin, simulator leases, POS event deposits, coach economics. Avoid landlord-heavy leases, novelty opening spike, unprofitable F&B, obsolete simulator tech. Franchises: Five Iron Golf (franchise fee/royalty in FDD), X-Golf America, GolfCave, The Golf Rooms/local brands, Topgolf Swing Suite licensing. Request Item 19, equipment requirements, F&B/alcohol model.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • See named franchise systems above; request current FDD, Item 19, closure history, royalty/ad fund, territory rules, and required capex before any commitment.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: Five Iron, X-Golf, Golfzon Social, Topgolf Swing Suite, local indoor golf clubs, country clubs, teaching pros. PE activity includes Topgolf Callaway and sponsor-backed experiential leisure, but local boxes remain fragmented. Moat: leagues, corporate relationships, prime location, tech quality, coaching community, F&B execution.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Asset purchase preferred unless licenses require entity transfer. SBA 7(a) possible but underwrite equipment replacement and tenant improvements hard. Base down payment ≈$83K plus working capital. Payback ~2-3 years if leagues/events stable. Exit multiple only improves if revenue is recurring and GM-run.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): map golfer income density, winter demand, corporate event buyers, FDDs, simulator costs.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): recruit GM from golf/F&B; mystery shop utilization; build hourly bay model.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI only with 24-month utilization data or signed league/event pipeline.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • NO-GO if utilization under 30% in prime hours, rent+CAM >14% revenue, F&B requires owner heroics, no alcohol path where needed, equipment capex cliff, or SFL concept depends on indoor practice rather than events.
