1. Category Definition & Demand Map

• What is this business? Assisted stretching studios offering one-on-one stretch sessions, memberships, packs, intro offers, and recovery/wellness add-ons.

• Who are the customers? B2C adults 35+, athletes, golfers/tennis/pickleball players, desk workers, seniors seeking mobility, physical-therapy-adjacent consumers.

• Demand drivers: Aging population, active-longevity trend, recovery culture, boutique fitness memberships, and consumers seeking non-medical pain/mobility support.

• Geographic relevance: Best in Boston/NE affluent suburbs, NY/CT/NJ density, and SFL where property/consumer demand supports premium pricing.

• Market size: Assisted stretching is a fast-growing wellness niche but measured data is thin; use FDD AUV/member counts and local boutique fitness density rather than broad wellness TAM.

**Key assumptions:**

• Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE), not platform multiples.

• SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down; assumes acquisition loan, not real estate-backed 25-year debt.

• Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment; estimates use annual debt service of roughly 16.5%-16.8% of principal at 10 years / 11%.

• Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; the model deducts enough management burden to avoid buying a job.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

• Revenue range: $450K-$900K

• SDE range: $90K-$220K after manager/royalties

• Years in business: 2+ with membership cohorts

• Route density / customer count: 300+ active members; affluent fitness/wellness radius

• Key assets to verify: Membership export, staff certifications, lease, intro-offer conversion, churn, review base

• Red flags to avoid: High churn, low utilization, intro-offer addiction, franchisor litigation, owner selling memberships daily

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

StretchLab is the largest; Stretch Zone is key competitor; also YogaSix/Club Pilates recovery adjacency. Verify current FDD, Item 19, royalty/ad fund/local marketing, Xponential/FTC/legal disclosures, and resale economics.

5. Competitive Landscape

• Top competitors / roll-ups: StretchLab, Stretch Zone, LYMBR, Massage Envy stretch add-ons, PT clinics, Club Pilates/YogaSix/recovery boutiques

• PE activity: Xponential owns StretchLab; boutique fitness has public/private capital but recent franchisor scrutiny raises risk.

• Fragmentation level: Increasingly branded/franchised; local independent stretch studios exist but brand trust matters.

• Moat: Membership retention, staff quality, referral partnerships with sports/medical/wellness, convenient location, low churn.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

• Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note/holdback, transition services, employee retention, and working-capital peg.

• SBA eligibility and terms: Usually eligible if profitable, transferable, and clean books; use 10-year / ~11% / 10% down.

• Down payment required at Dan's scale: $60K-$180K for a $600K-$1.8M EV target.

• Payback period at base case: Base case 3-5 years.

• Honest multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3-4x SDE, professionalize, and exit around 4x if growth/manager depth is proven.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

• Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Pull FDDs, interview 8 franchisees/former franchisees, analyze local boutique fitness density.

• Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Review membership cohorts, staff utilization, churn, and manager-led sales process for any resale.

• Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Proceed only on discounted resale with clean cohorts and operator; otherwise kill.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

• Member churn >8%-10% monthly

• No clean Item 19/franchisee validation

• Owner required for sales/community

• FTC/legal/franchisor issues impair lending/resale

• Rent + royalties + labor leave <1.25x DSCR
