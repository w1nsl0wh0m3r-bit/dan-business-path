ABA / Autism Therapy Center — Deep Dive

Demand is powerful, recurring, and reimbursement-backed, but this is a regulated clinical labor business with reimbursement/documentation risk. Dan should not buy a small center without an experienced BCBA/clinical operator and healthcare compliance infrastructure.

NO-GOAcquisition / Franchise Screen

Verdict: NO-GO for Dan unless paired with proven clinical operator

Demand is powerful, recurring, and reimbursement-backed, but this is a regulated clinical labor business with reimbursement/documentation risk. Dan should not buy a small center without an experienced BCBA/clinical operator and healthcare compliance infrastructure.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • Centers provide applied behavior analysis therapy for children with autism, usually delivered by RBTs under BCBA supervision in center, home, school, or hybrid settings.
  • Customers are families, but revenue is largely commercial insurance, Medicaid, and sometimes school districts; B2C demand is filtered through payer authorization.
  • CDC reports about 1 in 31 U.S. 8-year-olds identified with ASD, supporting durable demand; supply is constrained by clinician availability.
  • NE/NY/NJ/CT have dense demand and complex payer/regulatory environments; SFL has demand but Medicaid/commercial rate diligence is critical.

Key assumptions:

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan’s scale ($200K-$500K SDE), not platform multiples.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - annual debt service) / down payment; figures are directional and before taxes.
  • Operator partner assumption: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; model includes manager/clinical/GM reality where relevant.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

  • Good target only with clinical partner: $2M-$5M revenue, $300K-$700K SDE, 5+ years, clean billing audits, diversified payer mix, stable BCBA team, waitlist, low RBT churn, and documented medical-necessity reviews.
  • Verify authorizations, denied claims, recoupments, supervision ratios, licensure, Medicaid exposure, clinical quality metrics, and parent satisfaction.
  • Franchise candidate: Success On The Spectrum is a known ABA franchise; also benchmark non-franchise chains like Action Behavior Centers, Hopebridge, BlueSprig, CARD, and Autism Learning Partners. Pull FDD and independent clinical/legal review before any step.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Scaled chains and PE-backed platforms are active: Action Behavior Centers, Hopebridge, BlueSprig, CARD, Autism Learning Partners, Proud Moments, and regional providers.
  • PE interest confirms demand but also attracts scrutiny; quality failures, aggressive billing, or poor supervision can become existential.
  • Moat is BCBA recruiting/retention, payer contracts, compliance, waitlist, outcomes reputation, and family trust—not marketing alone.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Use asset purchase with healthcare counsel, billing audit holdback, seller note, and indemnity for payer recoupments.
  • SBA can finance changes of ownership, but working capital needs are real because collections lag and payroll is heavy.
  • Base purchase of $1.62M needs ~$162K down plus legal/compliance diligence; do not pay 6x+ EBITDA at Dan’s single-site scale.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Days 1-30: find clinical operating partner first; no operator, no process. Map payer rules in MA/CT/NJ/NY/FL and request SOS FDD.
  • Days 31-60: review de-identified claims/denials, payer mix, BCBA/RBT roster, utilization, waitlist, and compliance policies.
  • Days 61-90: either partner-led LOI with healthcare counsel or formal kill memo.

8. Kill Criteria

  • No committed BCBA/clinical operating partner.
  • High Medicaid audit/recoupment exposure or sloppy documentation.
  • BCBA turnover or RBT churn makes census unstable.
  • Revenue concentration in one payer above 40%.
  • Seller asks healthcare-platform multiple for single-site risk.
