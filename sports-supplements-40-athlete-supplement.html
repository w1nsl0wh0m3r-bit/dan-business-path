Sports Supplements / 40+ Athlete Supplement — Ecommerce Deep Dive

Underwriting a niche DTC supplement brand targeting the 40+ athlete: subscription economics, CAC discipline, regulatory landscape, and brand acquisition path.

Conditional Ecommerce / Brand UW Score: 3.20 Confidence: Medium Date: May 26, 2026

Executive Verdict

Ecommerce brand acquisition — if LTV:CAC and subscription rate are proven.

Best target: a Shopify-based supplement brand with 2,000+ active subscribers, >40% repeat purchase rate, LTV:CAC >3:1, clean supply chain (contract manufacturer, no single-source risk), and 3+ years of financial history.

Why it works: Aging athlete awareness is growing. DTC supplement brands have 55-70% gross margins. Subscription models create predictable revenue. PE interest in the space (Unilever/Onnit, Nestlé/Atrium) validates exit potential.

Why it can fail: CACs rising across Meta/Google. Supplement commoditization is real — formulations are easily copied. FDA regulatory risk (DSHEA violations). Owner-as-brand risk if the founder is the persona.

1. Market Sizing & Demand Drivers

US dietary supplements market: $55-60B (CRN, NBJ, 2024). Sports nutrition segment: ~$15-20B. The 40+ athlete/masters athlete niche: estimated $2-5B based on ~35M Americans 40+ who exercise regularly × $50-150/year supplement spend (estimate).

Key sub-categories: protein powder, creatine, collagen, omega-3, joint support (glucosamine/chondroitin/MSM), electrolyte/recovery, testosterone support, adaptogens.

Demand drivers: aging population staying active longer, longevity/wellness trend, social media fitness content targeting 40+ demographic, GLP-1 adoption creating adjacent muscle-preservation demand.

Moat: Brand trust, NSF/Informed Sport certifications, subscription stickiness. Formulations are weakly protectable. The real moat is audience + trust + habit.

5. Financing & Capital Discipline

Valuation frame
SDE multiple: 2.5-4x for ecommerce brands. Never pay for "brand potential" — pay for demonstrated cash flow.
Financing frame
SBA 7(a) for brands with 2+ years history. Asset sale: inventory, brand, customer list, domain, Amazon seller account.

Capital discipline: Pull Shopify/Amazon data directly — don't trust seller-provided screenshots. If LTV:CAC < 2.5:1, walk away. Verify repeat purchase rate independently.

6. Acquisition Buybox

Target profile:

  • Revenue: $300K-$1.5M
  • SDE: $75K-$300K
  • Subscription/repeat revenue: >40% of total
  • Customer LTV:CAC ratio: >3:1
  • Clean supply chain (contract manufacturer, no single-source risk)
  • Amazon presence with >4-star rating
  • 3+ years operating history

7. 90-Day Roadmap

Days 1-30

Niche Validation & Operator Identification

  • Map 50+ masters athlete supplement brands on Shopify and Amazon
  • Identify 10 acquisition candidates (Empire Flippers, Quiet Light, BizBuySell)
  • Interview 2-3 supplement brand founders about unit economics
  • Research FDA DSHEA, GMP compliance, NSF certification costs
  • Identify operator partner with supplement industry experience
Days 31-60

Financial Modeling & Target Diligence

  • Build 3-case model for $500K-$1M revenue ecommerce brand
  • Pull Amazon seller data (Jungle Scout/Helium 10 estimates)
  • Validate CAC by channel: Meta, Google, influencer, affiliate
  • Model subscription vs. one-time purchase economics
  • Verify supply chain: contract manufacturer MOQs, lead times, backup sourcing
Days 61-90

LOI or DIY Build Decision

  • Submit LOI on strongest candidate if metrics clear buybox
  • If no target: evaluate DIY build ($25-50K startup: contract manufacturing, Shopify, marketing)
  • Alternative: white-label existing manufacturer's formula, build audience via content

Kill Criteria

  • LTV:CAC ratio below 2.5:1 on trailing 6 months
  • Subscription/repeat rate below 25% of revenue
  • FDA warning letters or regulatory issues in target's history
  • Single manufacturing source with no backup
  • Amazon account with policy violations or suppressed listings
  • Owner IS the brand (personal following) — non-transferable
  • Gross margin below 50% (commodity reseller, not brand)
  • CAC trending up >15% QoQ with no path to improvement
