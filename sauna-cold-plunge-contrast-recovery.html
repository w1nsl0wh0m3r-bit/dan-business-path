1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? A recovery studio offering infrared/traditional sauna, cold plunge, contrast therapy, compression, red light and memberships/packages.
  • Who are the customers? B2C wellness consumers, athletes, executives, biohacker/longevity customers and local B2B partnerships with gyms, trainers and sports clubs.
  • Demand drivers: longevity/wellness trend, athletic recovery, social media discovery, memberships and high-income consumers seeking non-clinical routines.
  • Geographic relevance: NYC/CT/NJ/Boston affluent areas and SFL are relevant; dense high-income trade areas matter more than broad regional demand.
  • Market size: Global wellness economy is multi-trillion per Global Wellness Institute; sauna/cold plunge is a niche without reliable local TAM, so model by memberships, room utilization and comparable wellness studios.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $700K-$1.8M with 300+ active members and room utilization history.
  • SDE range: $200K-$600K normalized manager-run SDE; below that is too owner-job-like.
  • Years in business: 4+ years, with at least 3 years of tax returns/POS data.
  • Route density / customer count: 300-800 active members in a 10-15 minute affluent trade area; utilization tracked by room/hour.
  • Key assets to verify: lease/buildout/HVAC/plumbing, equipment warranties, sanitation SOPs, membership data, waivers, staff training, brand/reviews.
  • Red flags to avoid: unproven location, high capex not reflected in price, weak sanitation/liability process, churn hidden by launch promos.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Top 3-5 franchise brands with typical franchise fee + royalty: Restore Hyper Wellness, Perspire Sauna Studio, iCRYO, HOTWORX, Pause Studio/contrast concepts; verify franchise fee/royalty in current FDDs.
  • FDD highlight: Item 7 buildout, Item 19 membership economics, required equipment refresh and territory rules matter most.
  • Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only; if Item 19 is absent, validate with franchisee calls and treat franchisor examples as marketing.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Top 5-10 competitors or roll-ups active in this space: Restore Hyper Wellness, Perspire Sauna Studio, HOTWORX, iCRYO, Othership, Remedy Place, local spas/gyms and boutique recovery studios.
  • PE activity: Wellness franchisors and recovery concepts have institutional interest, but unit economics are not yet as seasoned as gyms or salons.
  • Fragmentation level: Medium-high; branded concepts are expanding but local boutiques dominate many markets.
  • Moat: membership community, premium location, sanitation trust, equipment uptime, programming and partnerships with trainers/athletes.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal structure: asset purchase with 10%-20% seller note/earnout tied to retention; inventory/equipment and lease assignments diligenced separately.
  • SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if tax returns, lease/licensing and cash flow are clean; underwrite 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
  • Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $75K-$250K plus working capital for a $750K-$2.0M entry price.
  • Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on manager-run SDE; walk above 5 years.
  • honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale, earned by professionalizing reporting, de-risking staff/customer concentration and adding repeat revenue.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests; build NE/NY/SFL competitor and pricing grid.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, site visits; request tax returns, POS/customer cohorts, lease/equipment schedules and labor roster.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if manager-run SDE, debt service coverage and local whitespace survive diligence.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • Manager-run SDE below $175K after replacing owner labor.
  • Asking price above 4.5x verified SDE without exceptional recurring revenue.
  • Seller cannot reconcile P&L to tax returns/POS/bank statements.
  • Lease, license, medical/regulatory, insurance or equipment dependency cannot transfer cleanly.
  • Membership churn or utilization data cannot support fixed rent/payroll after promo cohorts normalize.
