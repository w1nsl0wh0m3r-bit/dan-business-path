BonBon

Verdict: WATCH / APPROACH ONLY IF FOUNDERS WANT A STRATEGIC GROWTH PARTNER; otherwise DIY replication is the higher-probability path. Confidence: Medium. The brand signal is real: BonBon appears to have created the U.S. Swedish-candy zeitgeist, has multiple NYC/Sag Harbor stores, national ecommerce, wholesale reach, private-label products, and credible operating infrastructure. The acquisition case is weak because there is no evidence of distress, brokered sale, PE/VC ownership, or capital need; founders still look active, culturally central, and brand-defining. Why now: Swedish candy had a 2024-2026 viral demand shock. BonBon’s own site says #BonBon has been searched over 3.1B times and external coverage links the Bubs/Swedish candy shortage directly to a BonBon-originating TikTok wave. PRNewswire now describes BonBon as seven retail locations across New York/Connecticut, ecommerce, and wholesale globally. Why Dan: This is a founder-led specialty retail/CPG category where a PE-trained buyer could underwrite store-level economics, inventory turns, import/supply-chain risk, lease quality, and whether viral demand converts into a repeatable premium retail concept. Dan’s edge would be disciplined capital + multi-market rollout, not “I know candy.” Biggest reason this could be wrong: the $10.3M revenue / 55 employee baseline is not independently verified. ZoomInfo/Datanyze-style figures are directional. Actual revenue could be materially lower, ecommerce could have normalized post-viral spike, and wholesale could be low-margin.
