Go Yummy

Verdict: WATCH — leaning PASS, conditional on distressed pricing. Confidence: Medium. Go Yummy is a real, fast-moving, 3-unit Swedish candy operator in SFL with a genuinely large-format store, modern ecommerce stack, and viral social traction. But three structural forces make this a weaker bet than the dashboard's #3 ranking implies: 1. Retail commoditization of the core product. Bubs — the hero brand that drives 60%+ of Swedish candy store appeal — is now distributed in 35,000 US retail doors including Target, Walgreens, Kroger, and Costco (Retail Brew, Mar 2026). The premium pick-and-mix model survives only on experience/variety, not product exclusivity. This is the single biggest risk to the category and it is not priced into either Go Yummy's or ScandyCandy's narrative. 2. The SFL market cannot support 3 full-scale Swedish candy operators. Go Yummy (Palm Beach/Broward), ScandyCandy (Miami-Dade, now pushing north into Palm Beach), and BonBon (national, viral) are converging on the same ~6M-person MSA. Three units each, overlapping geographies, identical product. Margin compression is the most likely 12-month outcome. 3. <12 months operating history. Domain registered July 2025. First store ~fall 2025. Three stores opened within ~6 months. There is no financial track record to underwrite. Any valuation is pure projection, and rapid expansion without disclosed capital suggests either capital strain or aggressive founder reinvestment — either way, a diligence red flag.
