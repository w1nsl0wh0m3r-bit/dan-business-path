## Kill Rules

### Kill Backflow If

- No device-level renewal records.
- Certification/licensing sits only with a retiring owner.
- Testing revenue is one-off, not annual route work.
- Route density is scattered across too wide a geography.
- Repair attach is too weak to support manager-loaded economics.

### Kill Insurance If

- Captive, personal-lines runoff or Florida coastal homeowners concentration is the core book.
- Seller owns the relationships and workflows.
- Retention is weak or unprovable.
- Carrier appointments cannot transfer.
- AMS export does not tie policies, commissions, carriers and customers to financials.
- Debt service fails after producer, service staff and E&O costs.

### Kill Water Filtration If

- Installs dominate and recurring revenue is under 25%.
- Active installed base is under 300 systems.
- Salt/filter/service stops are not dense.
- Vendor/dealer rights block transfer.
- The owner is the only technical diagnostician or scheduler.

### Kill Refrigeration If

- PM contracts are under 30% of revenue.
- Emergency/after-hours work exceeds 35% of revenue.
- Fewer than two non-owner techs can perform refrigerant work.
- EPA/refrigerant workflow is undocumented.
- Revenue is install/construction-heavy.
