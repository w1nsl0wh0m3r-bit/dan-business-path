# Water Filtration / Softener / Salt Route Active Sourcing Sprint

**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Objective:** Decide whether water filtration, water softening, salt delivery, RO service, and well-water treatment routes are a credible active-sourcing acquisition lane for Dan in Boston/MA suburbs, CT/Fairfield/New Haven/Hartford, Westchester/Northern NJ, and South Florida.  
**Guardrails:** No outreach, no paid data, no spend. Desk research only. Succession clues are separated into confirmed facts vs inference.

## Executive Take

**Verdict: WATCH-to-GO, with a very tight target profile.** This is a credible active-sourcing lane only when the business is an installed-base service route, not a one-time water-system installer. The attractive asset is a recurring customer file tied to salt delivery, annual/quarterly service, filter/membrane changes, carbon/resin rebeds, softener repair, and well-water maintenance.

The category is not as clean as backflow because demand is not usually legally mandated and many operators are effectively project-sales companies. But the best South Florida, Northern NJ, and private-well CT/outer-MA operators show real route characteristics: scheduled salt/chemical delivery, filter changes, rebeds, rentals, monthly well maintenance, and repair of all brands.

**Top five names for the next no-outreach diligence pass:**

1. **Perfectly Clear Water Filtration** — Broward/Palm Beach, family-owned 30+ years, automatic salt delivery, monthly well-water maintenance, all-brand service/repair, rebeds, filters, pumps.
2. **Seacrest Water** — Palm Beach County, family-owned since 1975, locally operated filtration specialist, salt delivery, broad affluent PBC coverage.
3. **Advanced Water Products Inc.** — Miami-Dade, family-owned since 1956, monthly salt/chemical service, rentals, repairs, scheduled maintenance check with delivery.
4. **All Pumps & Water** — Jupiter/Palm Beach north, 20+ years, softeners/carbon/RO/well pumps, salt delivery, maintenance, repair, strong local review signal.
5. **SoftSprings Water Conditioning** — Northern NJ, since 1960, automatic scheduled salt delivery, rentals, service/maintenance, broad Bergen/Morris/Essex/Passaic coverage.

**Buybox in one sentence:** buy a dense installed-base route with salt/filter/service recurrence and non-owner technicians, not a water-treatment sales shop that happens to sell softeners.

## Target Profile

### Best Target

The best acquisition/JV target is an independent residential/light-commercial water treatment company with:

- 500+ active installed systems in a compact geography.
- Recurring salt route, filter-change route, service contracts, rentals, monthly well-water maintenance, or commercial salt/chemical program.
- All-brand repair/service capability using non-proprietary components where possible: Fleck, Clack, Pentair, Goulds, Stenner, carbon/resin media, RO membranes.
- At least two non-owner technicians who can diagnose, service, rebed, and repair.
- A customer database with system type, install date, service history, salt usage, filter/membrane cadence, media age, and water-test history.
- Repair and replacement attachment: rebeds, tanks, valves, pumps, pressure tanks, UV bulbs, RO membranes, carbon filters, softener resin.

### Acceptable Target

- Kinetico/Culligan/Puronics-style independent dealer if dealer transfer rights are clean and the franchisor/vendor will consent.
- Well-water specialists in CT, Westchester/Putnam, Northern NJ, and outer MA if recurring maintenance and technician bench are strong.
- Plumbing companies only if the water-treatment book is separately identifiable and can be carved out.

### Weak Target

- Lead-gen installation website with no route/service evidence.
- Plumbing contractor with a water-filtration landing page but no salt/filter recurring program.
- Salt-only micro-route with no equipment knowledge or technician capability unless it has exceptional density and churn data.

## Geography Read

### South Florida: strongest active-sourcing geography

South Florida has the best blend of density, hard-water consumer pain, affluent residential demand, and visible salt/service offerings. Palm Beach and Broward are especially interesting because operators can concentrate around high-income single-family neighborhoods, country clubs, well-water pockets, and estate maintenance customers.

Miami-Dade has strong route potential when a company has scheduled salt/chemical service, but the market may skew more competitive and plumbing-adjacent. The best Miami target in this sprint is Advanced Water Products because the monthly service program is explicit.

### Northern NJ / Westchester: credible, but vendor and affluence filters matter

Northern NJ has multiple long-tenured companies with automatic salt delivery, rentals, service, and commercial water conditioning. This is a real lane if the route is dense in Bergen/Morris/Essex/Passaic and the equipment base is serviceable post-close.

Westchester/Putnam has attractive affluence and private-well demand, but the clearest target found, Westfair Water Systems, is an authorized independent Kinetico dealer. That may be valuable, but transfer restrictions are a gating item.

### Connecticut: credible private-well service lane, less pure salt-route lane

CT private wells create real water-treatment demand: arsenic, uranium, radon, iron, manganese, pH, PFAS, bacteria, and hardness. CT DPH says homeowners should test for arsenic at least once, and if arsenic is detected should retest every five years; treated water should be tested at least annually to confirm the system is working. That creates service logic, but many companies are well-pump/pump-service businesses rather than salt-route businesses.

Best CT patterns: Hydro-Pure, Aqua Pump, CTSoftWater, Servisoft, Werner, Clean Water Man. The first-call issue is technician/key-person dependence.

### Boston / MA suburbs: weakest for generic softeners, better in outer suburbs and wells

Inside MWRA-served Boston metro, the generic water-softener thesis is weak. MWRA says its drinking water hardness is about 16 mg/L, or roughly 1 grain/gallon, which is soft. That means Boston-core softener routes are not the right bet.

MA is still viable in outer Boston suburbs, private wells, PFAS/well-water treatment, and NH/MA border towns. Best MA names found: Hanson’s, Culligan Water Filtration of New England, Aquathin Greater Boston, Premier Water Systems, High Purity Water, Aqua Pump in Sturbridge/Southbridge/Hampden.

## Confirmed Facts vs Inferences

Confirmed from operator pages and directories:

- Perfectly Clear advertises one-time/automatic salt delivery, monthly well-water maintenance, all-brand repairs, filter changes, rebeds, and pump repair.
- Seacrest says it is family-owned and operated since 1975 and now offers salt delivery.
- Advanced Water Products describes a monthly Salt/Chemical Service program with scheduled delivery, brine-tank refill, maintenance checks, and softener timer/basic maintenance.
- SoftSprings says it has served Northern NJ since 1960 and offers automatic scheduled salt delivery.
- Westfair says it is family-owned and operated and an authorized independent Kinetico dealer for Westchester/Putnam.
- MWRA says its water is soft, about 16 mg/L or 1 grain/gallon, which weakens the Boston-core softener thesis.
- CT DPH and NJDEP private-well materials support the well-water testing/treatment tailwind in CT and NJ.

Inferred only:

- Long-tenured family-owned companies may have succession relevance.
- Owner-operated companies with dated websites, named owners, or family language may be more acquirable.
- Salt delivery plus maintenance language suggests route density, but the route book is not confirmed until customer count, route logs, and recurring revenue are reviewed.
- Dealer relationships may create transfer restrictions; no transfer rights were reviewed in this sprint.

## Kill Criteria

Kill the lane or a target if any of these are true:

- **Recurring revenue is under 25%** of gross revenue, excluding warranty call-backs. If installs dominate, this is a project-sales shop.
- **Active installed-base count is under 300 systems** unless route density is exceptional and the route is growing quickly.
- **Salt/filter route density is weak:** fewer than 8-10 stops per service day in target ZIP clusters, or technician windshield time dominates labor.
- **No service history database:** if records are paper-only, incomplete, or do not show system type, install date, media age, salt/filter cadence, and customer status.
- **Vendor/dealer transfer blocks the deal:** Culligan/Kinetico/Puronics-style brands require consent, non-compete, territory transfer, or buyer certification that Dan cannot obtain.
- **Key-person dependence:** owner is the only licensed/technical diagnostician, only salesperson, and only route scheduler.
- **No non-owner technician bench:** fewer than two credible field people who can service softeners, RO, pumps, valves, and media.
- **Water chemistry/geography is weak:** Boston-core MWRA soft-water towns without private-well/PFAS/iron/chlorine treatment pain should be deprioritized.
- **Consumables economics are bad:** salt is delivered at thin margin with no service attach, no system check, and no upsell/retention data.
- **Warranty liability is hidden:** lifetime warranties, free service calls, dealer obligations, or installed equipment defects eat the service margin.
- **Customer acquisition is paid-lead dependent:** revenue is driven by Google/Angi/HomeAdvisor install leads rather than installed-base repeat customers.

## First-Call Underwriting Checklist

No outreach should happen until Dan approves it. If approved, the first call should be a route-book verification call, not a generic buyer intro.

### Business Mix

- Revenue last 3 years by installs, salt delivery, filter changes, service contracts, rentals, repairs, rebeds, pumps, commercial accounts.
- Gross margin by line item.
- Percentage of customers with recurring scheduled service.
- Number of installed systems currently in active service database.
- Number of customers served in last 12 months, 24 months, 36 months.

### Route Density

- Salt delivery customers by ZIP code and frequency.
- Average stops/day and average revenue/stop.
- Percentage of salt stops where technician also inspects, changes filters, adjusts timer, or performs repair.
- Commercial route accounts and frequency.
- Churn by cohort.

### Installed Base

- System list export: customer, address/ZIP, install date, system type, brand/valve/media, warranty status, service history, next service due.
- RO membrane/filter replacement cadence and compliance.
- Carbon/resin media age and expected rebed opportunities.
- Water-test records and problem type by customer: hardness, iron, sulfur, arsenic, uranium, PFAS, radon, pH, chlorine/chloramine.

### People

- Number of non-owner technicians.
- Who diagnoses systems, sells jobs, schedules routes, and handles emergency calls.
- Certifications/licenses: plumbing, well contractor, pump/water-treatment, WQA, state vendor status, PFAS vendor status.
- Compensation model and technician retention.

### Equipment / Vendor

- Brand mix: proprietary vs non-proprietary.
- Dealer agreements, territory rights, consent rights, non-transfer clauses, minimum purchases.
- Installed warranty obligations and who pays.
- Inventory: valves, tanks, resin, carbon, salt, RO membranes, pumps.

### Deal Fit

- Seller role post-close.
- Family involvement and key-person dependencies.
- Route assets separable from plumbing/pump/drilling business.
- Customer concentration: HOAs, country clubs, builders, commercial/institutional, property managers.
- Proof of service reminders and renewal workflow: CRM, QuickBooks, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, spreadsheets, paper.
