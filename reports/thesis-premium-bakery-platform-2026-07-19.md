## Executive Summary

The bakery lane has 16 targets on the dashboard — the most of any category after bagels — but no thesis document existed. This fills that gap. The thesis identifies two distinct approaches: a multi-unit operator platform in growth markets (DFW, Philadelphia) and an NYC cultural-brand cluster.

---

## The Two Sub-Lanes

### Sub-Lane A: Multi-Unit Operator Platform (Conviction 7)
**Targets:** Cookie Society (DFW, 5 units), Federal Donuts (Philadelphia, 6 units), Crumbl Multi-Unit Resale (4-7 unit packages)

**The Thesis:**
- These are proven multi-unit operators with real revenue, real systems, and expansion runway
- **Cookie Society** is the strongest DFW target: 5 locations (Frisco, Prosper, Southlake, Addison, Mansfield), founded by Marissa + Jeff Allen (former NFL), largest black-owned cookie brand in Texas. Wholesale + retail. Texas-Sized Cookies format.
- **Federal Donuts** is the strongest NE target: 6 locations across Philadelphia + NJ/PA expansion. Part of CookNSolo (Steven Cook + Michael Solomonov of Zahav). Israeli-influenced fried chicken + donuts + coffee. The catch: it's part of a larger restaurant group and may not be independently acquirable.
- **Crumbl Multi-Unit Resale** has the highest documented returns on the board (43-52% cash-on-cash) but brand-level concerns: average unit revenue declining ($1.30M→$1.16M FY24→FY25). Still, $250-350K net profit per store on a turnkey system.

**The Risk:**
- Cookie Society and Federal Donuts are founder-led and may not want to sell
- Crumbl is a maturing brand — the decline in AUV is a red flag
- Federal Donuts' corporate entanglement with CookNSolo is a complexity

**The Path:**
- Cookie Society: relationship approach, minority growth capital + operating partner
- Federal Donuts: spin-out diligence — can CookNSolo separate it cleanly?
- Crumbl: request CIMs on NJ 4-unit ($4.75M) and NJ 7-unit ($5.5M) packages

### Sub-Lane B: NYC Cultural Brand Cluster (Conviction 3-7)
**Targets:** Sunday Morning Cinnamon Rolls (7), Supermoon Bakehouse (6), Radio Bakery, Sweet Hut, Lysée, Win Son, Somedays, Laurel, Benji's Buns, Mah-Ze-Dahr, La Cabra

**The Thesis:**
- NYC has a concentration of premium bakeries with cultural heat but sub-scale operations (1-2 units)
- Sunday Morning Cinnamon Rolls (2 units, conviction 7) is the standout: simple product with gifting, catering, and kiosk potential
- Supermoon Bakehouse (1 unit, conviction 6) has rare cultural brand equity but needs operational discipline
- The rest (conviction 3) are interesting but not yet actionable — single-unit, founder-dependent, no clear expansion path

**The Risk:**
- NYC bakery economics are brutal: high rent, labor, ingredients
- Cultural brands are founder-dependent — without the founder, the brand equity fades
- The 1-unit bakeries are not acquirable as platforms; they're IP/brand acquisitions at best

**The Path:**
- Sunday Morning Cinnamon Rolls: worth a direct approach — simple product, proven demand, expansion runway via kiosks/catering/gifting
- Supermoon: minority investment, not control
- Others: monitor. Most won't grow past 1-2 units without operational help they can't afford.
