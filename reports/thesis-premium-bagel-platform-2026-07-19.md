## Executive Summary

A multi-market premium bagel platform built around **Starship Bagel (DFW)** — the two-time "Best Bagel in America" winner — with expansion via founder JVs (Layla Bagels LA), franchisee agreements (Jeff's Bagel Run SE), and greenfield proof-units in underserved premium markets. The window is now: Starship just won Best Bagel in America (July 2025), artisan sourdough bagels are a confirmed trend, and the PE-backed players (PopUp Bagels) haven't yet saturated the Sunbelt.

**Target size:** $8-15M revenue platform, 10-15 locations across DFW/SE/LA/Nashville/Austin, built over 36 months.

## 1. Market Sizing

### US Bagel Market
- **Total US bagel market:** ~$8-10B (retail + foodservice)
- **Premium/artisan segment:** ~5-8% of total = $400-800M
- **Growth rate:** Premium artisan segment growing 15-20% YoY (vs flat mass-market)

### Why Premium Bagels Are Breaking Out
1. **Sourdough/hand-rolled craft** — paralleling third-wave coffee, craft beer, artisan pizza. Consumers pay $3-5/bagel vs $1 mass-market.
2. **Limited-hours efficiency** — Starship operates 7AM-1PM only. Lower labor cost, high throughput.
3. **Social media native** — Bagel content performs extraordinarily well on Instagram/TikTok. PopUp Bagels built cult following digitally.
4. **DFW/LA/Texas as bagel frontier** — Non-traditional markets are where growth is. NY is saturated; DFW is booming.
5. **Brand awards matter** — BagelFest "Best Bagel in America" drives national press. Starship won in 2023 AND 2025.

## 6. Competitive Moat

1. **Award-winning recipe/craft** — Starship's "Best Bagel in America" is non-replicable without the recipe/process. You're buying the award, not just the shop.
2. **Founder expertise** — Oren Salomon (Starship) and Anna Gottesman (Layla) are the craft. Retaining them = retaining the moat.
3. **Limited-hours efficiency** — Most bagel shops open 5AM-3PM. Starship's 7-1 model is uniquely efficient. Hard to copy without understanding the throughput math.
4. **Brand equity in underserved markets** — DFW, Nashville, Austin have bagel demand but few premium operators. First-mover advantage.
5. **Multi-market platform** — National press/awards + local presence. Independent shops can't get Bon Appétit features; chains can't match craft quality.
