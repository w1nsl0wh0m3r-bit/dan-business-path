## Buyer Thesis

Water filtration is attractive only when it is an installed-base service route. The target asset is the recurring customer file tied to salt delivery, filter and membrane changes, rentals, annual tune-ups, rebeds, well-water maintenance, and all-brand repair.

This is not a blanket endorsement of water-treatment installers. The category gets bad quickly when revenue is driven by one-time in-home sales, financed equipment markups, paid lead-gen, exclusive dealer restrictions, or an owner who is the only technical diagnostician.

## Buybox

| Item | Target |
|---|---|
| Geography | South Florida first; Northern NJ/Westchester second; CT private-well corridors third; outer MA/NH selectively |
| Installed base | 500+ active systems preferred; 300+ minimum if density is excellent |
| Recurring revenue | 25%+ from salt/filter/service/rentals/maintenance, not just installs |
| Team | At least 2 non-owner techs who can diagnose, service, rebed, and repair |
| Records | Customer system type, install date, service history, media/filter cadence, salt usage, water-test history |
| Vendor risk | Low vendor lock-in preferred; dealer/franchise transfer rights must be clean if applicable |

## Kill Criteria

- Installs dominate and recurring revenue is under 25%.
- Active installed base is under 300 systems.
- Salt/filter/service stops are not dense.
- Vendor/dealer rights block transfer.
- The owner is the only technical diagnostician or scheduler.
- Paid search or financed-equipment sales are the real economics.

## First 10 Owner Questions

1. How many active installed systems are in the customer file?
2. What percentage of revenue is install, salt delivery, filter/membrane changes, rentals, maintenance, repair, and emergency pump/well work?
3. How many customers are on automatic service, salt, rental, or maintenance schedules?
4. What CRM or dispatch system tracks system type, service cadence, and parts/media history?
5. What brands/components are in the installed base, and are there dealer transfer restrictions?
6. How many non-owner technicians can diagnose, rebed, service, and repair?
7. What is average annual revenue per active system?
8. How many route stops are done per day, and how compact are the routes?
9. What percentage of leads come from referrals/installed base versus paid marketing?
10. What work would stop if the owner took 30 days off?

## Priority Call Sheets

### Perfectly Clear Water Filtration

- **Why first:** Automatic salt delivery, monthly well maintenance, all-brand service, rebeds, filters, pumps, Broward/Palm Beach density.
- **Opening diligence:** Confirm installed systems, automatic service customers, recurring revenue percentage, and tech bench.
- **Red flag:** If the recurring language is mostly marketing and installs drive economics.

### Seacrest Water

- **Why first:** Family-owned since 1975, Palm Beach density, salt delivery, affluent service area.
- **Opening diligence:** Verify active customer file, salt route count, service vs install mix, and succession context.
- **Red flag:** If brand/vendor relationships or owner relationships are the whole business.

### Advanced Water Products Inc.

- **Why first:** Family-owned since 1956; explicit monthly salt/chemical service with maintenance checks, rentals, and repairs.
- **Opening diligence:** Ask whether the monthly service route can be quantified and transferred.
- **Red flag:** If company is too large, too family-entrenched, or too project-heavy for Dan’s first wedge.

### All Pumps & Water

- **Why first:** Jupiter/PBC north, softeners/carbon/RO/well pumps, salt delivery, maintenance, repair, high local trust signal.
- **Opening diligence:** Split recurring maintenance from pump emergencies and owner-led diagnostics.
- **Red flag:** If technical pump work creates after-hours/key-person risk.

### SoftSprings Water Conditioning

- **Why first:** Northern NJ since 1960, automatic salt delivery, rentals, service/maintenance.
- **Opening diligence:** Verify route density across Bergen/Morris/Essex/Passaic and whether customer data is clean.
- **Red flag:** If vendor/dealer rights or family ownership prevent clean transfer.
