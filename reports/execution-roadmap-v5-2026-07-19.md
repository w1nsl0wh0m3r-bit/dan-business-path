### The SBA 7(a) Reality at $10M Scale

**Critical update (July 2026):** SBA now allows combined financing up to $10M:
- **SBA 7(a)**: up to $5M per loan (Prime + 2-2.75%, ~10-11% in 2026)
- **Combined structure**: SBA 7(a) + SBA 504 can reach $10M+ for real-estate-heavy deals
- **As of July 4, 2026**: eligible businesses can access up to $10M in combined SBA financing
- **Terms**: 10-year amortization for business, 25-year for real estate component
- **Buyer equity**: 10-15% typical (20-25% for first-time buyers)
- **Personal guarantee**: required for any 20%+ owner
- **Seller financing**: required in 30-50% of deals (5-15% of purchase price typical)
- **Approval timeline**: 60-120 days from LOI to funding

**Top SBA acquisition lenders 2026:**
1. **Live Oak Bank** — #1 SBA 7(a) lender, franchising/hospitality focus
2. **Newtek** — broad small-business acquisition lending

### Recommended Capital Structure

**Scenario S3 (3-4 Deal Blended) is the optimal structure at $10M:**

Why not one $10M bet?
- Single-asset concentration risk
- $10M deals attract PE competition, compressing returns
- Harder to get SBA financing on a single deal above $5M
- One operational failure = total portfolio failure

Why 3-4 deals?
- Diversifies across category and geography
- Each deal fits within single SBA 7(a) loan limit ($5M)
- Allows blended portfolio: brand/operator + land-backed
- Builds operating muscle through repetition
- If one underperforms, others carry the portfolio
