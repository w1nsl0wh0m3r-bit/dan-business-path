# Swim School Acquisition / Franchise Underwriting Memo

**Date:** 2026-06-08  
**Prepared for:** Dan / Business Transition Engine  
**Scope:** Swim school and year-round learn-to-swim as a consumer multi-unit lane after bagels. No outreach, no paid data, no spend. Public-source research only.

## Bottom Line

**Recommendation: WATCH, with a narrow validation sprint. Do not greenfield a dedicated-pool franchise.**

Swim schools have real parent-pay demand, safety-driven recurrence, affluent-suburb fit, and strong category leaders. The problem is not demand. The problem is that dedicated-pool economics put Dan into a heavy leasehold/construction bet before the local demand, instructor bench, utilization, and manager-run operating model are proven.

The right path is:

1. **Partner / hosted-pool validation first**: British Swim School-style economics or independent pool-partnership model. This is the only greenfield-ish path worth validating because it avoids $1.5M-$6.0M of pool buildout.
2. **Independent acquisition second**: buy a mature independent or small local chain only if the seller already solved pool access, enrollment, instructor hiring, scheduling, safety, and local reputation.
3. **Franchise resale third**: Goldfish / Big Blue / Aqua-Tots resale only if priced off actual cash flow, not replacement cost or brand hype.
4. **Kill dedicated-pool new build**: if the only available path is a new Goldfish/Big Blue/Aqua-Tots buildout with multi-million-dollar capex, this lane should lose to bagels, backflow, insurance books, and other lower-irreversibility opportunities.

**Verdict after bagels:** Swim deserves a **2-week validation sprint**, not active sourcing yet. The sprint should prove pool access and local waitlist pressure before any franchisor call or LOI path. If those two are not provable, pass.

## Why This Lane Is Tempting

Demand quality is genuinely strong:

- CDC says drowning is the leading cause of death for children ages 1-4, and formal swim lessons can reduce drowning risk. CDC's 2024 Vital Signs reported more than 4,500 U.S. drowning deaths per year during 2020-2022 and called for more accessible swim lessons. Sources: CDC prevention page, updated 2025-09-29; CDC Vital Signs, updated 2024-06-18.
- The customer is usually a parent buying safety, not discretionary entertainment. That makes swim more durable than many kids-enrichment fads.
- The best models bill monthly or perpetually. Examples: Charles River Aquatics in Wellesley charges monthly for school-year group lessons and requires 30 days' notice to discontinue; SwimLabs Westchester lists monthly perpetual group lessons at $189-$209 plus an annual service fee.
- Affluent suburbs have the right customer density: Wellesley/Newton/Natick/Winchester; Fairfield/Westchester/Northern NJ; Boca/Parkland/Weston/Fort Lauderdale.

But the category is also a facility business wearing a kids-enrichment costume. The hardest diligence question is not "do parents want swim lessons?" It is "can a local unit fill enough 30-minute class slots at high utilization to cover rent, payroll, pool maintenance, HVAC/dehumidification, insurance, royalties, and debt service without the owner teaching?"

### Goldfish

Goldfish is the strongest dedicated-pool franchise benchmark. It has brand equity, Item 19 disclosure, low closure signals, and a clear warm-water kids safety positioning. Public FDD summaries show the issue: this is a $1.7M-$6.0M project depending on source/year/state. FranchiseDepth cites the 2026 WI FDD at **$2.584M-$5.968M** and 6% royalty plus 2% marketing; VetMyFranchise cites **$1.663M-$3.747M**, $50K fee, and 6% royalty plus 2% ad fund.

The revenue signal is real: FranchiseDepth reports **$1.994M average revenue** for franchised schools open at least 12 months as of 2025 year-end. But at this capex level, average revenue is not enough. Underwriting must clear: rent, debt service, labor, dehumidification/HVAC, chemical/maintenance, manager payroll, insurance, local marketing, royalty/ad fund, and ramp losses. A $2M revenue unit can still be mediocre if it cost $3M to open and requires a full facility staff.

**Dan take:** Goldfish is a benchmark, not a first move. Only pursue a resale if the seller has already taken the construction/ramp pain and the purchase price is based on verified EBITDA/SDE.

### Big Blue

Big Blue is more tech-forward and institutionally backed. The official FAQ says the initial investment typically ranges from **$2.108M-$3.7605M**. IFPG lists a 6% royalty and says the FDD includes an Item 19. Older FranchiseGator material says Item 19 covered gross revenue, expenses, and EBITDA for affiliate-owned schools, but the exact current dataset needs the latest FDD before underwriting.

**Dan take:** If Goldfish is "mature expensive," Big Blue is "modern expensive." That can work for a strong multi-unit operator with real estate/construction muscle. It is not the right first validation path for Dan unless a discounted resale appears.

### Aqua-Tots

Aqua-Tots is a sizable system but the public economics are less attractive for Dan. The official franchise investment page shows **$1.619M-$2.639M**, including tenant improvements of **$1.2M-$1.8M** and pool design/build/aquatic supplies of **$250K-$400K**. FranchiseOverview's 2026 page says total investment can reach **$3.015M**, with 6% royalty and 2% marketing, and says the FDD has **no Item 19**.

**Dan take:** No Item 19 plus large buildout equals pass for new units. This is a "buy stabilized if cheap" brand, not a validation sprint candidate.

### British Swim School

British Swim School is the most interesting because it avoids owned pool construction. FranchiseOverview's 2026 summary lists **$94.6K-$168.4K** initial investment, **10% royalty**, **2% marketing**, **258 franchised units**, and an Item 19 with **$461K average / $335K median revenue** across 119 units. Its Item 20 summary shows 2024 growth of 70 openings and 5 closures.

This lower capex comes with a different fragility: pool access. If the business rents hotel, gym, school, JCC, apartment, or club pools, the lease/license is the enterprise value. Control over temperature, locker rooms, parking, water quality, schedule, and customer experience is limited.

**Dan take:** British is the only franchise path that deserves validation after bagels. But validate pool partner supply first. Without assignable, durable pool slots, this is a lead-gen business with a fragile venue dependency.

## Independent / Operator Pass

The independent target universe is more interesting than the pure franchise universe because local reputation and facility access can be defensible. The best target is not necessarily the biggest school. The best target is the operator with:

- existing year-round pool access or dedicated facility;
- recurring monthly/perpetual billing;
- clear class ratios and slot economics;
- non-owner instructor bench and aquatics lead;
- strong review density / parent trust;
- expansion constraint caused by pool capacity rather than weak demand;
- seller fatigue or succession need.

## Facility Economics Pass

### Dedicated Pool Buildout

The dedicated-pool path is where the lane gets harsh.

Public franchise cost signals imply the same conclusion:

- Aqua-Tots official page: tenant improvements alone **$1.2M-$1.8M**; pool design/build/aquatic supplies **$250K-$400K**; total **$1.619M-$2.639M**.
- Big Blue official FAQ: **$2.108M-$3.7605M**.
- Goldfish summaries: **$1.663M-$3.747M** or **$2.584M-$5.968M**, depending on FDD/source.

Directional dedicated-pool model:

| Driver | Conservative | Base | Strong |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Buildout + opening capex | $1.8M | $2.75M | $4.0M |
| Debt/equity burden | High | Very high | Brutal if delayed |
| Monthly rent / CAM / utilities | $35K | $55K | $80K+ |
| Payroll incl. manager/instructors/front desk | $55K | $90K | $135K |
| Pool maintenance, chemicals, HVAC/dehumidification | $10K | $20K | $35K |
| Insurance / compliance / safety | $5K | $10K | $18K |
| Royalty + marketing if franchised | 8% | 8%-12% | 8%-12% |
| Break-even requirement | high utilization | very high utilization | near-perfect execution |

If a unit reaches $2.0M annual revenue and 18%-22% store-level EBITDA before debt service, it can be interesting. But if it costs $3M+ to open, has ramp losses, and needs a manager, the cash-on-cash profile may still lag easier acquisition categories. The payback math is fragile unless the unit is both very full and financed well.

**Kill rule:** If projected payback exceeds 5 years after including ramp losses, manager payroll, maintenance reserve, and debt service, pass. If underwriting depends on owner labor to clear DSCR, pass.

### Hosted / Pool-Partnership Model

Hosted model has lower capex but a different bottleneck.

Directional hosted-pool model:

| Driver | Conservative | Base | Strong |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Weekly active swimmers | 150 | 325 | 600 |
| Average monthly revenue / swimmer | $125 | $155 | $185 |
| Annual revenue | $225K | $605K | $1.33M |
| Pool rent / rev share | 12%-20% | 10%-18% | 8%-15% |
| Instructor/front desk payroll | 35%-45% | 30%-40% | 25%-35% |
| Royalty/marketing if British | 12% | 12% | 12% |
| Local marketing/admin/software/insurance | 12%-18% | 10%-15% | 8%-12% |
| Owner-independent EBITDA potential | thin | $60K-$125K | $200K+ |

Hosted can work if one territory controls multiple pools with stable peak-hour access and a real instructor bench. It fails if each pool is a one-off relationship and the owner is constantly renegotiating schedules.

**Validation rule:** Before buying/franchising, identify at least 5 plausible pool partners in a target market and 2-3 that have underused weekday afternoon/weekend capacity. No outreach yet, just public mapping: hotels, JCCs, private clubs, gyms, schools, municipal pools, apartment/HOA pools, colleges.

## Demand / Competition Pass

### Demand Signals

- **Safety urgency:** CDC drowning data supports the fundamental demand case.
- **Affluent parent willingness to pay:** Public pricing in Wellesley and Westchester supports $160-$225/month group pricing in dense suburbs. JCC Greater Boston lists group/private rates and explicitly notes year-round weekly lessons as helpful for building skill.
- **South Florida structural demand:** More pools, more boating/beach exposure, longer swim season, and intense infant survival swim culture make Palm Beach/Broward a naturally strong demand market. The issue is more competition, not demand.

### Competition Signals

Competition is meaningful in every target geography:

- **Boston suburbs:** Goldfish, British, Little Flippers/Emler, Charles River Aquatics, JCC Greater Boston, YMCA, town pools, private instructors. Reddit local threads show parent waitlist frustration around Goldfish/Little Flippers, but that is anecdotal and should be treated as a signal, not proof.
- **Fairfield / Westchester / Northern NJ:** Children of the Sound, SwimLabs Westchester, British Swim School Fairfield Route 46, Into The Swim, Y/JCC/private clubs, local swim teams.
- **Palm Beach / Broward:** Quality Swimming, AquaChamps, The Swim Academy PBC, Palm Beach Safety Swimming, Just Swim, Aqua-Tots, Goldfish Royal Palm Beach, ISR/mobile instructors, municipal programs.

### Paid Search / CAC Read

No paid tools were used. Public evidence still suggests the category is locally competitive:

- Brand pages target city-specific "swim lessons" SEO aggressively.
- National franchises have location pages in the exact corridors.
- Parent forums repeatedly ask for infant/toddler swim recommendations, especially in Broward/Westchester/Fairfield.

Validation should include free Google SERP/manual local pack checks for "kids swim lessons [town]" and "infant swim lessons [town]" across 10 towns. If the local pack is crowded with 4.8+ rated incumbents and no waitlist evidence, de novo should be downgraded.

## Path Decision

### Franchise

**Only British Swim School deserves a real sprint.** It is low enough capex to test and uses a model that avoids new pool construction. But 10% royalty + 2% marketing is heavy, and the median revenue signal of $335K means many territories are not large enough to be meaningful unless multi-pool / multi-territory scale is plausible.

Dedicated-pool franchise path is a **pass** unless buying a resale.

### Independent Acquisition

This is the best long-term path if targets exist. Quality Swimming, AquaChamps, Children of the Sound, The Swim Academy PBC, Charles River Aquatics-like operators, and smaller owner-led schools are more interesting than a new franchise. The hurdle is availability: strong independent schools with pools and waitlists may not sell cheaply.

### Facility Partnership

This is the right validation path. It lets Dan answer the key question: can he access water time in affluent family markets without building a pool? If yes, British/hosted or independent JV can move forward. If no, kill.

### Pass / Kill

Kill if:

- only path is $2M+ new buildout;
- no assignable pool contracts;
- no way to recruit instructors at planned wages;
- acquisition target depends on owner-instructor magic;
- unit economics require 80%+ utilization from year one;
- lease term / pool license term is shorter than payback;
- insurance is unavailable or materially higher than modeled;
- franchisor requires area development schedule before first-unit proof.

## Recommended Validation Sprint

**Sprint length:** 2 weeks.  
**Spend:** $0.  
**No outreach.**

1. **Pool access map:** For Boston west suburbs, Fairfield/Westchester/Northern NJ, and Boca/Parkland/Weston/Fort Lauderdale, list 30 potential host pools: hotels, JCCs, private clubs, schools, YMCAs, colleges, apartment/HOA pools, municipal pools. Mark likely underused hours.
2. **Local competitor map:** 10-mile radius around 12 affluent towns. Count national franchises, independents, Y/JCC/municipal alternatives, and review density.
3. **Waitlist proxy:** Check public class portals where visible, registration pages, "limited openings" language, parent forums, and sold-out class indicators.
4. **Unit model stress test:** Build a hosted model at 150 / 300 / 500 swimmers and a dedicated model at $1.8M / $2.75M / $4M capex. Include manager payroll, royalty, insurance, pool rent, and churn.
5. **Target refinement:** Shortlist 8 acquisition/JV candidates only if they show year-round operations, pool control, and non-owner staff.

**Go/no-go after sprint:** Continue only if at least one geography shows both pool access supply and waitlist/class-fill evidence. Otherwise watch and revisit only for a resale.

## Final Recommendation

**WATCH / VALIDATION SPRINT.** Swim schools deserve a narrow validation sprint after bagels, but only to prove the asset-light or acquisition/JV path. The default answer is **not franchise greenfield**. The likely winning entry, if any, is **independent acquisition or hosted-pool partnership**, with British Swim School as the franchise benchmark.

If the validation sprint cannot find durable pool access or a credible independent acquisition target, kill the lane. The expensive new-pool path is too permanent for the amount of unproven local operating risk.
