Produce a decision-grade thematic analysis of the 5 core acquisition categories to identify which will produce the strongest risk-adjusted returns over the next 5-10 years. Answer: **where should we concentrate capital and time?**

## Analysis Framework (per category)

### Supply Side
- Is the category supply growing, stable, or shrinking?
- Barriers to entry (capital, skill, regulatory, real estate)
- Labor availability and training pipeline
- Fragmentation — is consolidation happening or possible?

### Demand Side
- Consumer demographic tailwinds (who buys, is that cohort growing?)
- Pricing power — can operators raise prices without losing volume?
- Frequency and loyalty characteristics
- Substitution risk (what could kill demand?)

### Macro Tailwinds (5-10 year horizon)
- What broader trends support this category?
- What could reverse them?
- Interest rate / capital cost sensitivity
- Real estate and location economics

### Micro Market (our geographies: NE/NYC/FL primary, DFW secondary)
- Local competitive density
- Real estate cost and availability
- Regulatory environment (health permits, food safety, franchising rules)
- Customer density and willingness to pay

### Moat Analysis
- What makes this category defensible vs replicable?
- Brand power vs operational excellence vs location lock
- Network effects, switching costs, economies of scale
- Can a well-capitalized competitor replicate the moat quickly?
- **Specifically: WHY is luxury leather repair so good?** What structural advantages make Leather Spa a 10 conviction? Can those be replicated in other categories?

### 10-Year Winner Criteria
Rank all 5 categories on:
1. Demand durability (will people still want this in 2035?)
2. Supply scarcity (will it get harder or easier to compete?)
3. Moat strength (how defensible is a leading position?)
4. Capital efficiency (how much does it cost to grow?)
5. Operator dependency (can it run without the founder?)
6. Consolidation potential (can you roll up the category?)

## Output Format
1. **One-page executive summary** with category ranking by 10-year attractiveness
2. **Per-category deep dive** (supply/demand/moat/macro/micro)
3. **Cross-category comparison matrix** (scoring grid)
4. **The "why leather repair" analysis** — structural advantages decoded
5. **Capital allocation recommendation** — where to concentrate
6. **Kill list** — categories to de-prioritize
