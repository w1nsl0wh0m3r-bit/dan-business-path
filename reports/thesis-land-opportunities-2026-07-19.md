# Land Opportunity Thesis: Real Estate-Backed Consumer Businesses
## Date: 2026-07-19 | Agent: Hermes | Status: Diligence-Grade

---

## Executive Summary

Every target on the board so far is a **business** acquisition — you buy the brand, the operations, the cash flow. But there's a parallel category: **businesses where the real estate IS the moat.** Land-backed consumer businesses are attractive because:

1. **Dual return paths** — operating cash flow + land appreciation
2. **Embedded floor value** — the land has standalone resale/redevelopment value
3. **Inflation hedge** — real estate appreciation compounds with business growth
4. **Scarcity** — you can't manufacture more waterfront, more highway frontage, more golf-course acreage
5. **Transition-driven supply** — aging owners selling land-rich businesses for succession/retirement
6. **Lower competition at the small end** — institutional buyers want $50M+ portfolios; the $1-5M segment is still owner-operator territory

But land plays also carry real risks: higher capex, illiquidity, zoning/entitlement risk, weather/climate risk (especially Florida/waterfront), and the "you eat what you kill" intensity of owner-operators.

This thesis filters land-backed categories through the same conviction/CoC/entry-difficulty lens we've used everywhere else.

## The 7 Land-Backed Categories Evaluated

### 🟢 VIABLE — Worth Sourcing

#### 1. Car Wash (Express Tunnel & Self-Serve)
**The thesis:**
- Land-intensive pad sites on high-traffic corners
- Membership model creating recurring revenue (40%+ of wash sales from members at top operators)
- Cap rates: 6.0-8.0% for stabilized assets
- Valuation: 3-6x SDE (owner-operator single site), 5-8x EBITDA (multi-site platform)
- **Dual return:** operating cash flow + pad-site land appreciation (often on commercial corridor that appreciates independently)
- **Transition supply:** aging owners, lots of mom-and-pop single sites

**Numbers:**
- Single-tunnel site with real estate: $750K-$5M depending on market
- Express tunnel capex: $2-4.5M all-in (including land)
- 180ft tunnel example: Oneida County NY, $750K asking
- Three-site El Car Wash portfolio: $26M
- Membership revenue floor creates downside protection

**Risks:**
- Water/weather risk in some markets
- Membership model can mask declining actual wash counts
- Equipment capex cycles (7-10 years)
- Express tunnel format is in aggressive expansion — pricing up

**Verdict:** 🟢 **Pursue.** Strong land + cash flow combination. Focus on **founder-owned single-tunnel sites in affluent secondary markets** (avoid the express tunnel wars).

---

#### 2. RV Park / Campground
**The thesis:**
- Pure land play with operating revenue
- Strong demand tailwinds (outdoor recreation boom, work-from-anywhere, RV sales recovery)
- Cap rates: 7-10% (higher than most real estate)
- Valuation: $1.5M median asking, range $875K-$2.5M+ for established parks
- Seller financing common (eases entry, signals owner alignment)
- **Lowest capex per acre** of any land-backed category
- **Land scarcity is extreme** — zoning for new RV parks is nearly impossible in most markets

**Numbers:**
- Florida RV parks: ~35 properties for sale, ~$7,190/acre average, SE markets up to $1M/acre
- National inventory: active market, many founder-owned
- 8% cap rate on a $625K Cleburne TX example = clean cash flow

**Risks:**
- Seasonality (Florida = year-round, Northeast = May-October)
- Weather/climate (hurricane exposure in FL, freeze risk in NE)
- Operational intensity — guest management, facility upkeep
- "Older, stabilized properties" — i.e. aging infrastructure deferred capex
- Cap rate compression if interest rates fall (financing improves)

**Verdict:** 🟢 **Pursue.** Pure land-and-cash-flow play. Best fit for Florida year-round markets. The scarcity angle is real — they're not making more RV-zoned land.

#### 3. Self-Storage (Secondary Markets)
**The thesis:**
- Land-efficient (you can stack vertically), but still land-backed
- 95% success rate (highest of any small business)
- Cap rates: 5.0-7.5% stabilized, up to 8% for secondary/tertiary markets
- Recurring revenue (monthly rentals), low labor, low touch
- 2026 = buyer's market (overbuilding in primary markets creating opportunities)
- Software/tech layer easy to professionalize

**Numbers:**
- Secondary market facilities: 200-400 units, $500K-$2M asking
- Stabilized cap rate: 6-8% on trailing-12 NOI
- Owner-operator independents are the roll-up prey

**Risks:**
- Overbuilding in primary markets (supply risk)
- Climate-controlled adding to capex
- Rate competition from REIT-owned facilities (Public Storage, Extra Space)
- "Secret is out" — pricing elevated, harder to find mispriced assets

**Verdict:** 🟢 **Pursue (selectively).** Focus on **secondary/tertiary markets** where REITs don't play and cap rates are still 7-8%. Avoid primary markets — REITs have already compressed caps to 5%.

---

#### 4. Marina (Small / Independent)
**Theethesis:**
- Waterfront real estate = non-reproducible scarcity
- Multiple revenue streams: slip rentals, dry storage, fuel, service, restaurant/retail
- 131+ transactions tracked across 38 states (active market)
- Small independents are the roll-up prey (Blackstone bought Safe Harbor for $5.65B — that's the ceiling, small marinas are the floor)

**Numbers:**
- Small independent marinas: $1-5M range
- Cap rates: 6-8% for operating marinas
- Slip revenue + storage + service = diversified cash flow
- Land value often exceeds operating value (redevelopment optionality)

**Ritsks:**
- Climate/water-level risk (long-term)
- Dredging/upkeep capex (waterfront maintenance is relentless)
- Seasonality outside FL/sunbelt
- Institutional money flowing in (Blackstone, Sun Communities) — pricing pressure
- Environmental regulation

**Verdict:** 🟡 **Selective.** Only if the right asset appears in FL or Northeast. Don't force it. The scarcity is real but so are the maintenance costs.

### 🟡 INTERESTING — Monitor

#### 5. Independent Garden Center / Nursery
**The thesis:**
- Land-intensive (5-20+ acres common)
- Founder-owned, multi-generational common (Heartland Nursery KC closing after 54 years — two generations)
- Affluent consumer tailwind (plant passion, gardening boom)
- Land often in path of suburban development → redevelopment optionality
- Nurseries being acquired by PE (Riverbend Nursery, James Greenhouses) — category validation

**Numbers:**
- Range: $500K-$3M for established independent centers
- Land value often 50-70% of asking price
- Seasonal cash flow (spring peak)

**Risks:**
- Seasonality (massive Q2 concentration)
- Competition from Home Depot/Lowe's on commodity plants
- Water/climate dependency
- Successor risk (next gen doesn't want to run a plant farm)

**Verdict:** 🟡 **Monitor.** Interesting land optionality but operating business is less durable than car wash or storage. Only if land value creates a floor close to asking.

---

### 🔴 REJECTED

#### 6. Mobile Home Park
**The thesis looked good on paper:**
- Cap rates 5.5-7.2%
- Low tenant turnover
- Affordable housing demand

**Why rejected:**
- **Negative spread at current rates** — borrowing at 10% to buy at 7% cap = negative cash flow. Math doesn't work until rates fall significantly.
- **Reputation/PR risk** — institutional MHP buyers (Mobile Home University, Keel Team) face public criticism for raising rents on vulnerable populations. "Predatory" optics.
- **Regulatory risk** — rent control expanding (OR, CA, and municipalities). Cap rate may compress further via regulation.
- **Limited upside** — space rent is the revenue, and it's politically constrained.

**Verdict:** 🔴 **Pass.** The math doesn't work at current rates and the regulatory/reputational direction is adverse.

---

#### 7. Golf Course
**The thesis looked good on paper:**
- Large land footprint (100-200 acres)
- EPR Properties $113M sale-leaseback (5 championship courses) = institutional validation
- Family office capital entering ("strategic fit as real estate investment")
- Heritage Golf Group, accountable equity — active buyers

**Why rejected:**
- **Massive capex** — irrigation, greens, bunkers, clubhouses. Deferred maintenance is the norm on anything for sale.
- **Revenue declining** in many markets (golf rounds flat nationally, course closures outpacing openings outside top-tier markets)
- **Labor-intensive** — superintendent, pro, grounds crew, F&B
- **Redevelopment is the real play** — and that's a different business (real estate development), not consumer operations
- **Affluent customers but thin margins** — semi-private/daily-fee courses struggle to cover capex
- **Climate/water risk** — severe in FL, SW US

**Verdict:** 🔴 **Pass** as operating business. Interesting only as a land redevelopment play, which is a different thesis and different operator profile.

## The Sourcing Playbook (Land-Backed)

**Where to find these:**
- **BizBuySell** — `businesses-for-sale` category filters for car wash, RV park, self-storage
- **LoopNet** — commercial real estate, often includes operating business
- **ListSelfStorage.com** — storage-specific listings
- **RVParkSales.com** — RV-specific (Panama City Beach FL based)
- **MarinaDealFlow.com** — tracks 131+ marina transactions
- **SiriusGolf.com** — golf-specific (rejected but monitored)
- **BusinessesForSale.com** — 95 car washes currently listed US

**What to look for (the "transition signal" filter):**
- Owner 60+ years old
- 20+ years in business
- "Reluctant sale" / "health reasons" / "retirement"
- Real estate included in asking price
- Seller financing offered (alignment signal)
- Located in path of development (land appreciation kicker)
- Deferred maintenance (value-add opportunity)
- Below-market rents (storage/RV — raise to market on acquisition)

## Integration with Existing Situation Room

These land-backed categories don't replace the brand/operator acquisition thesis — they're a **parallel allocation**. The math:

- **Luxury repair roll-up** (Leather Spa anchor): $3-5M equity for platform + 2 bolt-ons
- **Premium bagel platform** (Starship anchor): $4-8M equity for platform + proof units
- **Car wash single-tunnel**: $750K-$2M per site (start with one, prove, roll)
- **RV park (Florida)**: $1-2.5M per park
- **Self-storage (secondary market)**: $500K-$2M per facility

**The blended portfolio approach** could allocate 60% to brand/operator acquisitions (higher return, higher execution risk) and 40% to land-backed assets (lower return ceiling, but real estate floor + cash flow).

This gives you the brand-building upside with a real estate anchor that has standalone value.
