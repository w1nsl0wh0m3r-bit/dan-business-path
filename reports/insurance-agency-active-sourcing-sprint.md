# Insurance Agency / P&C Book Active Sourcing Sprint

Date: 2026-06-07  
Prepared for: Dan  
Scope: no-outreach desk screen for small independent P&C agency/book acquisition in MA/Boston suburbs, CT, NY/NJ, and selective Florida where coastal homeowners is not the thesis.  
Deliverables: this memo plus `reports/insurance-agency-top-15-targets.csv`.

## Bottom Line

**Verdict: WATCH-to-GO.** Dan can source and underwrite small independent P&C agencies/books as a first-platform lane, but only with strict underwriting. The attractive version is not “buy an annuity.” It is a transferable renewal book with service staff, real carrier/appointment shelf space, clean AMS data, and a niche where advice/workflow matters: contractors/trades, habitational/landlord/CRE, franchisee/multi-unit small commercial, or affluent personal lines in MA/NY/CT.

This is a credible 30-day sourcing lane because public desk signals show many founder/legacy independent agencies with durable niche books and visible carrier/association relationships. It is not yet a GO for capital because the core facts that make or break a book are private: retention by line, producer dependency, carrier transfer consent, loss ratios, staff stay probability, and AMS cleanliness.

## Best Target Profile

Target the agency/book, not just the legal entity:

- Independent P&C agency or book with $400K-$1.5M commission revenue and $150K-$600K normalized SDE after real producer/operator comp.
- 90%+ client retention and 92%+ revenue retention, measured by line and carrier, not blended marketing claims.
- 50%+ commercial/specialty, or affluent personal-lines book with umbrella/valuables/home/auto cross-sell and advisor-led retention.
- Transferable carrier appointments or network/aggregator access with change-of-control path.
- At least two non-owner employees, including one licensed service/account manager.
- Owner/seller willing to stay 12-24 months, introduce top clients and carrier reps, and carry seller note or retention earnout.
- AMS export ties customer/policy/carrier/commission reports to financials; renewal workflow is visible in tasks, notes, certificate requests, remarketing triggers and account reviews.

## Best Niches

**1. Contractors/trades commercial P&C and surety**  
Best first-platform wedge. GL, workers comp, commercial auto, umbrella, inland marine, builders risk, bonds and certificates create high-service recurring relationships. The book is more valuable when service workflows live with account managers, not only the founder.

**2. Habitational / landlord / CRE / condo associations**  
Strong Dan-fit because boards, landlords and property managers have recurring renewal pain. But it needs loss-history discipline. Kill distressed, coastal, old-building or carrier-scarce books unless pricing is heavily haircut.

**3. Affluent personal lines in MA/NY/CT**  
Attractive if the agency has Chubb/PURE/Berkley One/PCS/Cincinnati-style access, umbrellas, valuables, high-value homes and advisory process. Less attractive if it is just homeowners/auto rate-shopping.

**4. Franchisee / multi-unit small commercial**  
Strategically interesting because of Dan's FDDIQ edge, but hard to buy as a clean micro-book. Better as a post-platform organic niche or hired-producer wedge.

## Kill Criteria

Kill fast if any of these show up:

- Seller-only relationships: owner controls top accounts, top carrier reps, renewal workflows and service knowledge.
- Captive or limited appointment model masquerading as independent shelf space.
- Florida coastal homeowners / Citizens / flood / wind concentration is the core revenue thesis.
- Retention below 85% commercial or below 88-90% preferred personal, unless priced as runoff.
- Revenue growth driven by rate inflation rather than policy count, client count or cross-sell.
- Producer dependency: one producer or referral source drives more than 25-30% of revenue.
- Weak AMS/data: commission reports cannot tie to policy/customer/carrier records and financials.
- No staff bench: seller is producer, CSR, renewal manager and carrier relationship.
- Carrier transfer risk: appointments, aggregator agreements or book ownership cannot transfer cleanly.
- E&O issues: open claims, no prior-acts path, no seller tail/extended reporting coverage.
- Debt service fails after replacing owner comp, producer comp, account-manager comp, E&O, AMS, network fees and staff stay bonuses.

## First-Call Underwriting Checklist

No outreach was performed. If Dan later approves calls, the first call should be surgical:

1. What percent of commission revenue is renewal vs new business vs contingency/profit share?
2. Revenue split by line: commercial P&C, personal lines, benefits/life/other, fees.
3. Top 10 carriers by commission and written premium; any carrier over 25%?
4. Which carrier appointments are direct, network/aggregator, brokered or wholesaled?
5. Does any appointment require carrier approval, production minimums or re-papering on change of control?
6. Retention by line for the last 36 months: policy count, client count and commission revenue.
7. Loss ratios by carrier/class and five-year large-loss report.
8. Top 20 accounts/households: revenue, tenure, producer, service owner, renewal dates.
9. What percent of revenue is produced or serviced by the owner?
10. Staff roster: licensed status, tenure, comp, non-solicits, remote arrangements, stay probability.
11. AMS used; can they export customer/policy/carrier/commission/notes/documents/certificate history?
12. Renewal workflow proof: expiration reports, task histories, remarketing SOP, certificate turnaround.
13. E&O coverage: open claims, prior claims, prior-acts path, seller tail.
14. Seller transition: will seller stay 18-24 months, introduce top clients/carriers and carry retention earnout?
15. Financing reality: normalized SDE after replacing owner/operator comp and paying producer/service comp.

## No-Outreach Next Steps

1. Build a private underwriting workbook with tabs for commissions by carrier/line/customer, renewal cohort, staff map, carrier transfer and DSCR.
2. Use Big I / PIA member directories, state producer-entity lookups and agency websites to expand the target map to 60+ names before any calls.
3. Split targets into three call tracks if Dan approves outreach later: retirement/legacy agencies, niche producer/JV agencies, and broker/listing comps.
4. Pre-identify licensed operator options before LOI. Dan can own capital, but the agency needs a durable licensed responsible producer/agent-in-charge/sublicensee structure.
5. Screen Florida only for commercial contractor or inland small-commercial books. Do not source coastal homeowners as the first platform.

## GO / WATCH / KILL

**WATCH-to-GO for 30 days.** The lane is worth a disciplined no-spend sourcing sprint and first-call prep. It becomes GO only when a target shows: transferable independent appointments, 90%+ retention, commercial/niche mix, staff/service bench, clean AMS, seller transition and retention-based deal structure.

**KILL** any deal that looks like a personal-lines/captive runoff book, a Florida coastal homeowners bet, or a founder-relationship annuity with no staff/process underneath.
