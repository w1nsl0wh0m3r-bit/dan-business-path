## Buyer Thesis

Backflow is the cleanest current acquisition lane because the asset can be a recurring compliance renewal book rather than a generic plumbing contractor. Annual testing is mandated in target jurisdictions, customers often rely on third-party portal/reporting workflows, capex is low, and the best operators have repair/replacement attach when devices fail.

The ideal buy is a dense route book with device-level renewal records, certified non-owner testers, and enough repair margin to support a paid manager. The wrong buy is a retiring owner-technician with a phone, a truck, and no transferable database.

## Buybox

| Item | Target |
|---|---|
| Geography | South Florida first; MA/CT second; NY/NJ only if official-list and route density are clear |
| Revenue mix | Majority annual testing/recertification plus repair/replacement attach |
| Customer base | Commercial, HOA, multifamily, property manager, municipal/utility compliance customers |
| Records | Device-level customer database with location, device type, due date, history, failed tests, repairs |
| Team | At least 2 certified non-owner testers or a retained seller plus a near-term hiring plan |
| Size | Small enough for SBA/operator partner path; large enough to support manager-loaded economics |

## Kill Criteria

- No device-level renewal records.
- Certification/licensing sits only with a retiring owner.
- Testing revenue is episodic, not annual route work.
- Route density is scattered across too wide a geography.
- Repair/replacement attach is too weak to cover callbacks, truck time, admin, and manager load.
- Portal submission workflow is informal or lives only in the owner's head.

## First 10 Owner Questions

1. How many active devices are in the database today?
2. What percentage of devices renew annually without outbound selling?
3. How are due dates tracked: spreadsheet, CRM, BSI/county portal, QuickBooks, paper?
4. What percentage of revenue is testing, repair, replacement, installation, and non-backflow plumbing?
5. How many certified testers are non-owner employees?
6. Which counties/municipalities create the most renewal volume?
7. Who submits reports and manages failed-test follow-up?
8. What is average revenue per device per year, including repair/retest attach?
9. What customer concentration exists by property manager, HOA, municipality, or commercial account?
10. What work would stop if the owner took 30 days off?

### Cape Backflow Systems Inc.

### Everything Flows Inc.

- **Why first:** Strong Broward/Palm Beach geography, family-owned, backflow testing/repair/replacement.

### Boston Backflow LLC

- **Why first:** Backflow-only position in MA/New England with service-contract language.
- **Opening diligence:** Verify MassDEP certification IDs, true Boston-metro density, service-contract count, and non-owner bench.
- **Red flag:** If “New England” service area means scattered windshield time.
