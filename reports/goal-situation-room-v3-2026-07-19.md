# GOAL: Situation Room V3 — Sourcing, Platform Theses, and White-Space Hunting

## Mission
Transform the Situation Room from a curated list into an active sourcing engine and diligence-grade platform. Three parallel workstreams: (1) build out the two platform theses into real diligence documents, (2) systematically expand the target universe in surviving lanes with density analysis, (3) hunt for the "next bathhouse" — buzzy categories still in founder-operator phase before PE arrives.

- 77 real targets / 58 watch / 40 comp
- Two platform theses identified: Luxury Repair Roll-up (Leather Spa + Rago + Cobblers) and Premium Bagel Platform (Starship + Layla + Jeff's franchisee)
- Generational succession identified as the real acquisition signal

## Workstream A: Platform Thesis Deepening (the two big ideas)

### A1. Luxury Repair Platform — full diligence doc
Build a real platform thesis document covering:
- **Market sizing**: Total US market for luxury repair (handbags, shoes, leather goods, watches, jewelry). TAM, SAM, SOM.
- **Market map**: Every premium repair operator in every affluent US market (NYC, Boston, SF, LA, Chicago, Miami, DC, Greenwich, Palo Alto, Hamptons). 30+ operators minimum.
- **Platform economics**: Unit-level P&L model for a luxury repair shop. Revenue per location, margin, labor cost (master craftsman constraint), SDE multiple.
- **Roll-up sequence**: Which operator is the platform anchor, which are bolt-ons, what's the market coverage map (NYC → NJ → SFL → Boston → Chicago → LA → SF).
- **Circular economy narrative**: ESG/sustainability tailwind analysis. Resale market growth (Rebag, Fashionphile, The RealReal) driving repair demand. Luxury brands launching repair programs (Hermès, LVMH).
- **Competitive moat**: Why this is defensible. Master craftsman scarcity, brand trust, luxury house partnerships, geographic density.
- **Risk matrix**: Master craftsman retirement, real estate cost in affluent markets, DTC repair kits, brand in-housing.
- **Acquisition criteria**: What makes a shop platform-ready. Revenue threshold, margin, craftsmanship quality, owner transition readiness.
- **Evidence table**: Every claim sourced.

### A2. Premium Bagel Platform — full diligence doc
Build thesis covering:
- **Market sizing**: US premium bagel market. Artisan/sourdough segment growth. DFW, LA, Nashville, Austin, Miami, Boston porting logic.
- **Market map**: Every premium/artisan bagel operator in target markets. 25+ operators. Identify which are founder-owned vs institutional.
- **Unit economics**: Revenue per location, food costs, labor model (limited-hours efficiency), SDE multiple.
- **Platform sequence**: Starship (DFW anchor) → port to Nashville/Austin → Jeff's franchisee (SE coverage) → Layla (LA) → Boston/Greenwich.
- **Proof-unit sprint**: What a single-location proof-unit looks like (72hr sprint plan — location scout, lease terms, equipment, staffing, recipe transfer).
- **Competitive moat**: Recipe/craftsmanship, location selection, brand equity, limited-hours efficiency model.
- **Risk matrix**: Founder dependency (Oren Salomon IS Starship), real estate, food cost inflation, franchise competition.
- **Evidence table**: Every claim sourced.

## Workstream C: White-Space Hunting (the next bathhouse before PE arrives)

Apply the lesson: institutional capital has already claimed the obvious buzzy lanes. Hunt for buzzy categories that are STILL in the founder-operator phase — too small or too new for PE to notice yet. Filter: founder-owned, growing, consumer-facing, social/experiential, high-margin potential.

Categories to explore (reject if already PE-infested):
- Social run clubs / running event operators
- Bouldering/climbing gym independents (not Movement, not CRG)
- Specialty pickleball (independent operators, not franchises)
- Maker spaces / community workshops / craft schools
- Rage rooms / stress relief entertainment
- Speakeasy-style hidden bar concepts
- Craft/artisan food halls (operator, not just landlord)
- Women's co-working/social clubs
- Boutique fitness recovery (stretch labs, percussion therapy) — check if PE already there
- Mobile pet grooming services
- Specialty children's enrichment (STEM, cooking, art) — independents only
- Premium plant shop / botanical bar (we added 3, expand)
- DTC premium pet food (local makers)
- Social dinner club / supper club operators
- Astrology/tarot/wellness social concepts
- Luxury pet services (pet hotel, spa, transportation)
- Vintage/resale curated shops (not Goodwill, curated premium)

For each, run blocking check: is the lane already PE/VC infested? If yes, skip. If no, identify 3-5 founder-owned operators and add to map.

## Workstream D: Sourcing Engine Layer

### D1. Transition signal monitoring
Build a monitoring framework for acquisition signals:
- Founder aging (public records, LinkedIn tenure)
- Business transition listings (BizBuySell, BusinessExits.com, latitude.ai, acquire.com)
- Local press signals ("after 40 years, owner considering retirement")
- Succession filings (llc dissolution, dba changes)
- Distress signals (reviews declining, store hours reduced, equipment aging)

### D2. Automated density analysis tooling
Build a repeatable methodology for "how many independent operators exist in X lane in Y market" — Google Maps API density scan + Yelp review count proxy + founder-ownership verification.
