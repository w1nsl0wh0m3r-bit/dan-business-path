## Why Candy, Why Now

### The Viral Demand Is Real

Swedish candy went viral on TikTok in 2024. BonBon went from 50 orders/day to 1,000+ overnight. ScandyCandy sold its entire Coconut Grove inventory in 9 days. Lil Sweet Treat scaled to 11 locations in <2 years. The demand is not speculative — it's documented and sustained.

### The Unit Economics Work — If You Import Direct

From the existing landed-cost model (swedish-candy-analysis.html):

| Sourcing Path | COGS/lb | Gross Margin |
|--------------|---------|--------------|
| Direct import (FCL from Sweden) | $4.81/lb | **65.6%** |
| Via US distributor (DDP) | $9.14/lb | **34.7%** |

At $14/lb retail, a store doing 200 lb/week (10,400 lb/year):
- Direct import: **$95,576 gross profit/year**
- Via distributor: **$50,544 gross profit/year**

The spread is $45K/year per store. At 4 stores, that's **$180K/year** in margin difference. Direct import isn't an optimization — it's the moat. The operational complexity (customs, freight, warehousing) is the barrier to entry that keeps margins protected.

### The Barrier to Entry Protects Authentic Brands

The category is fragmented. Most operators are:
- Single-store amateurs buying from US distributors (35% margin)
- TikTok-fueled pop-ups without supply chain depth
- Heritage brands (Li-Lac, Sockerbit) with loyal but local customer bases

Very few have cracked direct-import scale. ScandyCandy and BonBon are the exceptions. That's the edge.

## The Working Capital Edge

Candy has a real WC dynamic that creates a buyer's edge in negotiations:

### Inventory Perishability

Swedish candy has a 12-18 month shelf life. But the WC trap is in the **inventory build cycle**:

1. Direct import requires FCL (full container load) purchases — 18,000 lb minimum
2. That's $80-90K in inventory per container
3. At 4 stores doing 200 lb/week = 3,200 lb/month = container lasts ~5-6 months
4. So the business carries $80-90K of inventory that turns ~2x/year

**The negotiating edge:** When valuing the business, the seller's balance sheet shows $80-120K of inventory. But:
- Some of that inventory is slow-moving SKUs (not all 200+ SKUs sell equally)
- Some is near expiration
- Some is seasonal (Swedish holidays)

A rigorous inventory audit can discount the inventory valuation by 15-25%, reducing effective purchase price. This is the same dynamic as propane or nursery inventory — but with shorter shelf life and more SKU complexity.

### Gift Card / Stored Value Liability

Like restaurants, candy stores sell gift cards. Any unredeemed balance is a liability that transfers to the buyer. For a 4-store chain doing $1.5M revenue, gift card float could be $30-60K — a direct price adjustment.

### Seasonal Revenue Concentration

Swedish candy has lördagsgodis (Saturday candy) tradition — steady weekly demand. But holidays spike: Easter, Halloween, Christmas. A seller presenting TTM EBITDA that includes a holiday quarter will overstate run-rate. Always demand monthly P&L and re-underwrite on non-holiday months.
