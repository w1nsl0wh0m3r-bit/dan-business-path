## PART 2: WORKING CAPITAL MISPRICING IN CONSUMER CATEGORIES

Consumer businesses have different WC dynamics than B2B. Here's where the edges are:

### Consumer WC Traps (Ranked by Buyer Edge)

| Trap | How It Works | Best Consumer Categories | Edge |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|------|
| **1. Gift Card / Stored Value Liability** | Seller collects cash for gift cards, recognizes as "revenue" over time. Outstanding balance is a liability at close but seller wants credit for the cash. | Restaurants, coffee, retail, medspa | 🔴 HIGH — direct price lever |
| **2. Membership Deferred Revenue** | Monthly/annual memberships billed in advance. Cash collected but service owed. Seller books as revenue; buyer inherits obligation. | Car wash, fitness, pet boarding | 🔴 HIGH — "breakage" negotiation |
| **3. Perishable Inventory** | Food/flowers/cosmetics with shelf life. Seller values at cost; buyer marks to NRV. | Bakeries, cafés, pet retail, medspa (injectables expiry) | 🟡 MEDIUM — 10-20% markdown |
| **4. Prepaid Maintenance Contracts** | Service businesses selling annual "unlimited" packages. Cash in, service owed. | Car wash, pet grooming, auto detail, nail/hair | 🟡 MEDIUM |
| **5. Loyalty Program Liability** | Points earned but unredeemed. Accounting liability that sellers forget to discount. | Coffee chains, QSR, retail | 🟡 MEDIUM — often missed |
| **6. Seasonal Peak EBITDA** | Seller prices on trailing-12-month EBITDA but 40% of revenue is in 8 weeks (summer ice cream, holiday retail). Normalized EBITDA is lower. | Ice cream, seasonal retail, marinas | 🔴 HIGH — re-underwrite on monthly basis |

### Where to Push in Negotiation

**Car wash:** "Your $3M EBITDA includes $400K of membership revenue already collected for future washes. That's a liability transfer, not earned revenue. Adjust EBITDA to $2.6M."

**MedSpa:** "Your $3M EBITDA includes $250K of prepaid package revenue (6-treatment packages, 3 treatments undelivered at close). That's deferred revenue, not earned. Adjust to $2.75M."

**Restaurant chain:** "Your $3M EBITDA includes $180K of gift card breakage income (cards sold, never redeemed). That's not recurring. Adjust to $2.82M."

These adjustments directly reduce the effective purchase price 5-15%.
