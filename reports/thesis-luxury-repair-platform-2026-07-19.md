## Executive Summary

A multi-market roll-up of founder-owned luxury repair shops (handbags, shoes, leather goods) anchored by **Leather Spa (NYC)**, with bolt-ons in NJ, SFL, and other affluent markets. The circular economy tailwind, luxury house repair programs (Hermès, LVMH), and generational succession risk at target shops create a rare window to assemble a regional luxury repair platform with non-substitutable service, affluent customers, and durable margins.

**Target size:** $4-6M revenue platform, 5-8 locations across NYC/NJ/SFL/Boston, built through 3-5 acquisitions over 24 months.

## 1. Market Sizing

### TAM — Luxury Repair Services (Global)
- **Leather goods repair services market:** $2.81B (2025) → $6.17B (2035) at 8.15% CAGR (Business Research Insights)
- **Luxury bag repair sub-market:** $820M globally (2025) (Dataintelo)
- **Shoe repair (US):** $315.6M (2025), 3.9% CAGR (IBISWorld)
- **Luxury resale market:** $32.47B (2024) → $50.06B (2030) at 7.48% CAGR (Research and Markets) — **repair is a direct tailwind**

### SAM — US Premium Repair Serviceable Market
- Estimated 15-25% of global luxury bag repair market is US-addressable: ~$120-200M
- Premium shoe repair (affluent markets): ~$50-80M
- Combined SAM: ~$170-280M annually

### SOM — Realistic Capture (5-year platform)
- A 5-8 location platform across NYC/NJ/SFL/Boston could capture $4-8M revenue
- This represents 2-5% of SAM — realistic, not heroic

### Why Now
1. **Resale market exploding** — $32B → $50B in 6 years. Every resale transaction needs authentication, refurbishment, repair. The RealReal, Fashionphile, Rebag all need repair partners.
2. **Luxury houses leaning in** — Hermès circular economy program (2025), LVMH Circularity (Dec 2023), Neiman Marcus Repair & Restoration (Oct 2022). The houses are creating demand for authorized repair partners.
3. **Generational succession window** — Master craftsmen who founded shops in the 1970s-1990s are hitting retirement age. 4th-generation shops (Rago Brothers) face uncertain succession.
4. **DTC "right to repair" momentum** — EU and US regulatory pressure on repairability is creating tailwinds for repair-as-a-service.

## 3. Unit Economics (Proxy Model)

### Per-Location P&L (Mature Premium Repair Shop)

| Line Item | Low | High | Notes |
|-----------|-----|------|-------|
| Revenue | $400K | $1.2M | Location-dependent. NYC premium: $800K-$1.2M. Suburban: $400-700K. |

| Gross Margin | 80% | 85% | Exceptional. |
| Labor (master craftsmen + apprentices) | 35% | 45% | Primary cost. Master craftsman = $60-90K/yr. |
| Rent | 8% | 15% | NYC higher; suburban lower. |
| Other OpEx (marketing, utilities, insurance) | 8% | 12% | Minimal marketing — word of mouth. |
| **SDE** | **$120K** | **$280K** | Per location. |

### Acquisition Multiples
- **Independent repair shop:** 2-3x SDE (smaller, founder-dependent)
- **Multi-location platform:** 3-5x SDE (systems, brand, multiple locations)
- **Platform with luxury house contracts:** 4-6x SDE (enterprise revenue, defensible)

### Roll-Up Economics (5-location platform)
- **Combined revenue:** $3.5-5.5M
- **Combined SDE:** $700K-$1.2M
- **Acquisition cost:** $2-4M total (blended 3x SDE)
- **Integration add:** centralized booking, shared marketing, luxury house partnerships, mail-in expansion
- **Post-integration SDE uplift:** +20-30% from centralization + new revenue
- **Platform value at exit (4x SDE):** $3.5-6M

## 5. Competitive Moat

1. **Master craftsman scarcity** — There are fewer master leather repair craftsmen in the US every year. Training takes 5-10 years. Acquiring the craftsmen = acquiring the moat.
2. **Luxury house partnerships** — Neiman Marcus, Hermès, LVMH programs require vetted partners. Once you're in, switching costs are high.
3. **Brand trust** — Affluent customers don't price-shop luxury repair. They trust the shop that's been there 30 years. Acquiring the brand = acquiring the trust.
4. **Geographic density** — A multi-market platform can offer mail-in/ship-in service nationally while maintaining local walk-in density. Independent shops can't do both.
5. **Circular economy narrative** — ESG/sustainability story creates premium positioning and potential for brand partnerships, corporate accounts, and IP.
