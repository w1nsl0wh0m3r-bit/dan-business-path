1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Mold remediation identifies moisture sources, contains contaminated areas, removes affected material, cleans/HEPA-vacuums, and coordinates rebuild when needed. It sits adjacent to water/fire restoration but has its own health, insurance, and litigation sensitivity.
  • Who are the customers? Customers are split between homeowners, property managers, schools/municipal buildings, realtors, insurers/TPAs, and commercial landlords. Residential emergencies generate leads, but the better target has B2B referral relationships.
  • Demand drivers: older housing stock, humid basements/attics, insurance events after water losses, real-estate diligence, tenant complaints, and climate-driven moisture events. EPA notes mold can grow where moisture is present and CDC emphasizes prompt remediation of dampness/mold.
  • Geographic relevance: NE/NY excellent because older housing + basements; SFL also excellent because humidity/hurricanes but more competitive and catastrophe-driven.
  • Market size: best underwritten bottom-up. IBISWorld/industry reports track restoration services broadly; avoid relying on a single mold-only TAM because public figures vary widely.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Buy $1M-$3M revenue, $250K-$600K SDE, 7+ years, IICRC-trained crews, documented containment/SOPs, referral channels with plumbers/inspectors/property managers, and clean insurance/legal history. Avoid seller-as-estimator bottleneck, claims disputes, weak job costing, and jobs won only by storm-chasing.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Good franchise candidates include PuroClean, SERVPRO, Paul Davis Restoration, 911 Restoration, AdvantaClean. Expect franchise fees commonly around ~$50K and royalties/ad funds that need current FDD verification; Item 19 can be useful but territory/event mix matters more than system averages.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • SERVPRO, ServiceMaster Restore, Paul Davis, PuroClean, Belfor, BluSky, ATI Restoration, First Onsite, and local independents. PE-backed consolidation is active in broader property restoration; Blackstone/ATI, BluSky and First Onsite are examples of scaled restoration platforms. Moat is referral density, IICRC/compliance credibility, job-cost discipline, drying/remediation equipment, and estimator reputation.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Asset purchase preferred with equipment schedule, WIP cut-off, insurance/legal reps, seller note tied to referral retention, and excluded pre-close claims. SBA can work; a $1.5M base deal needs ~$150K down plus working capital/equipment reserve. Payback is usually 2.5-4 years if gross margins and collections are real. Multiple arbitrage is modest: buy 3.25-3.75x and maybe exit 4x if systems are institutionalized.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map local competitors, licenses/regulatory requirements, broker listings, franchise FDD requests, and 20-30 possible operators/partners.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build acquisition model, diligence 3-5 targets, verify customer/revenue retention, speak with lenders, and test operator partner fit.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit LOI on first target or reject category; include seller transition, retention/earnout mechanics, and diligence gates.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • No IICRC/compliance documentation
  • Open insurance/legal disputes or mold liability history
  • Revenue tied to one adjuster/plumber
  • Seller is only estimator/salesperson
  • Poor job costing/AR collections
  • Capex cliff in vehicles/equipment
