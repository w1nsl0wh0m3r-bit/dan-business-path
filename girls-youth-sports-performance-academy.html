Executive Verdict

DIY only through a staged demand test before fixed costs. For Girls/youth sports performance academy, Dan should prove willingness to pay from middle/high-school athletes, parents, clubs, coaches, sell the first cohort before committing to a lease, and kill it if repeat demand is weak.

Recommended action: Run a 30-day proof sprint with landing page, calls, presales, and a tiny operating pilot before signing a lease or hiring fixed staff.

Why this could work: The category has identifiable buyers — middle/high-school athletes, parents, clubs, coaches — and monetizable service lines: small-group training, camps, recruiting prep, injury-prevention clinics. That creates enough structure for disciplined testing instead of pure concept risk.

Why this could fail: The main traps are coach credibility, venue cost, seasonality, parent CAC. If those cannot be measured quickly, the dashboard score is not enough reason to proceed.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

Girls/youth sports performance academy should be evaluated as a local operating system, not an abstract market. The investable question is whether a small operator can repeatedly acquire customers, deliver the service without founder heroics, and retain enough demand to support a paid manager. The customer set is primarily middle/high-school athletes, parents, clubs, coaches. The revenue pool comes from small-group training, camps, recruiting prep, injury-prevention clinics, with upside from better scheduling, reminders, reviews, reactivation, and local SEO.

3. Path-Specific Buybox

Primary path: Venue-light DIY test only.

  • Demand proof: paid deposits from parents/athletes through club, school and coach channels before any lease.
  • Service proof: small-group training, camps, recruiting-prep and injury-prevention clinics can run with documented curriculum and non-founder coaches.
  • Manager-run proof: identify a head coach / program director and model pay, utilization, background checks and retention.
  • Local moat: club partnerships, trusted female coaches, sport-specific cohorts and parent referrals must matter more than generic youth fitness ads.
  • Kill discipline: if coach credibility, facility access, insurance, seasonality or parent CAC remain unmeasured after 30 days, move on.
Underwriteable cash flow = paid cohort gross profit − coach payroll − venue rental − insurance/compliance − parent CAC − founder sales heroics.

5. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

Valuation frame
Do not buy or build a fixed-cost box until demand is proven. Any acquisition must be valued after replacing owner coaching, normalizing rent, and proving memberships/camps are not one charismatic founder.
Financing frame
A lease and buildout are the enemy here. Use sublet gym time, school/club fields, park permits, and weekend camps before any SBA loan or personal guarantee.

The right test is presales and utilization, not TAM. Camps/clinics can show demand; recurring memberships prove whether this is a business. If CAC or coach retention is weak, kill quickly.

6. Regulatory / Legal Diligence

Child-safety requirements
  • Implement SafeSport-style policies even if not legally mandated: two-adult rule, abuse-prevention training, mandatory reporting, incident documentation, and parent communication protocols.
  • Run background checks for all coaches/admins; verify state-specific youth-program obligations in MA, CT, NY, NJ and FL.
  • Written concussion, heat illness, injury escalation, pickup/dropoff, photography/social media, and anti-bullying policies.
Insurance / facility
  • Require GL, participant accident coverage, abuse/molestation coverage, workers comp, hired/non-owned auto if transporting, and COIs from every venue.
  • Review lease/sublease indemnities, field/gym permits, waiver enforceability by state, and ADA/accessibility obligations.
  • Confirm coach classification: employee vs contractor risk, overtime, and payroll tax compliance.

8. Risk Register & Kill Criteria

  • Presale kill: kill if a no-lease landing page + club outreach cannot presell 40 paid athletes or $12,000+ of clinics/camps within 45 days.
  • CAC kill: kill if blended parent CAC exceeds $125 per paid athlete for clinics or $250 per recurring member after referral/channel tests.
  • Facility kill: kill if the model requires a lease longer than 6 months or >$25,000 of upfront buildout before $50,000 of contracted/presold demand.
  • Coach kill: kill if qualified female coaches cannot be hired/retained at 55%+ gross margin per session or if coach retention is below 80% over a season.
  • Utilization kill: kill if weekday after-school/evening and weekend slots cannot reach 60% utilization by month 4 in a pilot.
  • Safety kill: kill immediately if insurance excludes abuse/molestation coverage or if background-check/SafeSport-style controls cannot be operationalized.

10. 30 / 60 / 90 Roadmap

Days 1–30

Run the cheap test

  • Launch a landing page for girls sports performance by sport/age/ZIP with presale deposits for clinics and camps.
  • Call 20 club directors/coaches and 10 school athletic directors; secure 3 partner channels before spending meaningfully.
  • Survey facility options: school gyms, indoor turf, churches, community centers, CrossFit/gym off-hours, and parks permits.
  • Quote insurance, background checks, SafeSport-style training, and payroll costs for a 2-coach pilot.
Days 31–60

Prove coach and unit economics

  • Hire/test 2–3 qualified coaches for paid pilot sessions; model coach retention and session quality control.
  • Compare camp/clinic economics vs recurring membership economics using real presales, show-up rates, refunds, and referrals.
  • Calculate parent CAC by channel: club referral, school partner, Meta/Google, local SEO, parent ambassador.
  • Do not sign a lease; use hourly/sublease space only.
Days 61–90

Watchlist, not core pursue

  • Continue only if presales, coach supply, insurance and utilization clear the kill criteria.
  • If it works, keep it as a venue-light side experiment with seasonal camps and clinics.
  • If it requires a dedicated facility or founder-led sales, pause and redeploy time to compliance-route acquisition.
  • Do not classify this as a top-10 acquisition path until recurring membership economics are proven.

Final Recommendation

Move to the cheap-test / watchlist bucket. This is not a Dan-core compliance-route thesis. It may be personally interesting and potentially low-capex if venue-light, but it should not compete with backflow, fire/life-safety, grease trap, or restaurant compliance routes for diligence time. No lease, no acquisition, no franchise application until presales and coach economics are real.
