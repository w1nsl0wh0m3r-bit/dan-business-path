Running Club / Performance Lab — Deep Dive

Running club, coaching, gait analysis, and community performance facility

CONDITIONAL DIY/Acquisition UW Score: 3.30 Confidence: Medium

Executive Verdict

CONDITIONAL

Strong demographic tailwinds from the running boom, high community stickiness, and diversified revenue. Best as an acquisition of an established club with 200+ active members and $200K+ SDE.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

What is this business? A brick-and-mortar running performance facility that combines group training (track workouts, tempo runs, long runs), individual coaching (custom training plans, race prep), clinical services (gait analysis, VO2 max testing, lactate threshold testing), retail (shoes, nutrition, gear), and community events (race hosting, fun runs, social nights). Models range from bare-bones group-run clubs ($50-$150/month membership) to full performance labs ($2,500-$5,000+ for comprehensive coaching packages).

Who are the customers? B2C — recreational to competitive runners, triathletes, and fitness enthusiasts. Skews 25-55, college-educated, $80K+ HHI. Heavy representation from healthcare professionals, tech workers, and lawyers. Youth/HS runners for coaching and gait analysis.

Demand drivers: Post-COVID running boom (parkrun registrations up 40%+ since 2020, US road race finishers up 20%+ from 2022-2025). Marathon/Half marathon participation growth. Wearable tech adoption (Garmin, Coros, WHOOP) creating data-hungry runners. High school cross-country/track growth.

Geographic relevance: Strongest in affluent suburban markets. NE: Boston Metro (running capital of the US — Boston Marathon halo), Cambridge/Somerville, Newton, Wellesley. NY: Central Park culture, Westchester running clubs, Montclair NJ. SFL: Miami running scene, Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton. Boston is the #1 market.

Market size: US running participation: 50M+ Americans (SFIA). Running retail: $5B+ (NPD Group). Running coaching and performance services: $1-2B US, fragmented and growing. Per-location TAM: $500K-$3M.

2. Economics to Underwrite

3. Unit Economics Model (Three-Case)

**Key assumptions:** SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down. Revenue mix: 40-50% memberships, 20-25% coaching, 10-15% clinical testing, 10-15% retail, 5-10% events. Facility: 3,000-8,000 sq ft, lease $18-$35/sq ft in NE suburbs. Coach compensation: $50K-$80K in NE markets.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

  • Revenue range: $600K-$2M with 3+ years stable/growing membership
  • SDE range: $150K-$500K after head coach/director salary
  • Membership base: 200+ active monthly members with <10% monthly churn
  • Key assets: member database with retention data, coaching staff contracts, facility lease (7+ years), equipment (treadmills, force plates, metabolic cart), social media following, Google reviews (4.7+ stars)
  • Red flags: single-coach dependency, declining membership, poor facility condition, no proprietary methodology, high monthly churn (>15%), heavy discounting

5. Competitive Landscape

Running stores with coaching — Fleet Feet (250+ locations, franchise). Retail-first with coaching as community builder.

Online coaching platforms — TrainingPeaks, FinalSurge, Runna. Threat to low-touch coaching but cant replicate in-person analysis or community.

Big-box gyms with running programs — Equinox, Lifetime Fitness. Lower quality coaching but included in membership.

Free running clubs — November Project, local group runs. Free community but dont monetize. Potential referral source.

PE activity: Minimal. Fleet Feet has had private investment.

Moat: Community lock-in, coaching relationships, clinical equipment barriers (VO2 carts cost $30K-$60K), local race partnerships, brand reputation.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Deal structure: Asset purchase with seller note. Membership agreements transfer with business.
  • SBA eligible: Fitness and sports facilities are SBA-eligible.
  • Down payment at base case: $84K-$96K (10% of $840K-$960K).
  • Payback period at base case: 3-4 years.
  • Multiple arbitrage: Buy at 3.5x, grow membership 30%+, add second location, sell platform at 5-6x.

7. 90-Day Roadmap

Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map running clubs in target NE markets. Visit 3-5 facilities. Interview 2-3 club owners. Scout running store acquisition targets (Fleet Feet franchise resale, Marathon Sports).

Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Build 3-case model. Identify 2-3 acquisition candidates. Recruit potential head coach/director. Assess equipment costs.

Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit LOI or sign lease. SBA pre-approval. Execute only if: 200+ active members, <10% churn, head coach retained, SDE clears $150K after director salary, 7+ year lease.

8. Kill Criteria

  • Monthly membership churn above 15%
  • Head coach IS the brand (transfer risk)
  • No clinical testing capability or path to add it
  • Facility in declining retail location
  • Market with <50,000 population in 15-minute drive time
