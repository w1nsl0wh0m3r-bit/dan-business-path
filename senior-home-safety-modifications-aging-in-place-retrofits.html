Senior Home Safety Modifications / Aging-in-Place Retrofits — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Verdict: GO

Excellent thematic fit: aging demographics, clear pain point, recurring referral channels, and fragmented local installers. The challenge is building clinical/referral trust and avoiding low-margin one-off grab-bar work.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Assessment and installation of home safety upgrades: grab bars, ramps, stair lifts, walk-in showers, lighting, flooring transitions, railings, smart monitoring, and mobility equipment.
  • Who are the customers? B2C seniors/adult children; B2B referral sources include OTs/PTs, discharge planners, senior living communities, elder-law attorneys, VA/Medicaid-adjacent programs, and home-care agencies.
  • Demand drivers: Aging population, falls prevention, preference to age at home, hospital discharge pressure, caregiver burden, and rising institutional-care costs.
  • Geographic relevance: Very strong in NE/NY/CT/NJ affluent aging suburbs and SFL retirement markets; Florida may be the highest-density demand pool.
  • Market size: CDC says 1 in 4 adults 65+ reports falling each year and 14M+ older adults fall annually; aging-in-place/home modification market size is fragmented and should be treated as estimate.

Key assumptions:

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down; SBA says 7(a) can fund changes of ownership and caps at $5M.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite post-manager cash flow, not owner heroics.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $800K-$2.0M with mixed equipment/install/referral channels.
  • SDE range: $180K-$450K after paid operations lead.
  • Years in business: 3+ years with referral-source history.
  • Route density / customer count: 20+ active referral partners; 50+ installs/month; senior-dense zip focus.
  • Key assets to verify: CAPS/OT relationships, contractor licenses, insurance, product/vendor terms, referral CRM, warranty claims, VA/Medicaid reimbursement exposure.
  • Red flags to avoid: revenue only from low-ticket grab bars, unlicensed construction, no referral source repeat, heavy paid leads, poor documentation/photos.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • TruBlue Total House Care: senior-focused home maintenance/aging-in-place; franchise fee often ~$49K-$59K; royalty ~6%.
  • 101 Mobility: stair lifts/ramps/mobility franchise; franchise fee commonly ~$49K-$54K; royalty/ad fees in FDD.
  • Mobility Plus: mobility equipment and accessibility franchise; request FDD.
  • Re-Bath / Bath Tune-Up / bathroom remodel franchises: adjacent for walk-in showers, but larger-ticket remodeling risk.
  • FDD highlight: require revenue mix by equipment/install/service, gross margin by product, referral-source concentration, and working capital cycle.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: 101 Mobility, TruBlue, Mobility Plus, Lifeway Mobility, Amramp, local remodelers, durable medical equipment dealers, stair-lift dealers, home-care agencies' vendor lists.
  • PE/activity: home-care, DME, and home-services platforms all circle the aging-at-home theme; local modification installers remain fragmented.
  • Fragmentation: high, with few trusted local specialists and many general contractors doing one-off work.
  • Moat: referral trust, certifications, fast discharge-ready installs, documentation, insurance/vendor relationships, and senior-sensitive sales process.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for unverifiable add-backs; stock purchase only if licenses/contracts require it and liabilities are clean.
  • SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if profitable, owner-operated or manager-run with qualified operator, and no speculative real estate component; SBA 7(a) supports ownership changes up to $5M.
  • Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus working capital; for a $700K-$1.4M enterprise value, plan $90K-$250K cash need.
  • Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on equity after debt service.
  • Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, manager bench, clean reporting, and repeat revenue are proven.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map senior-density zip codes in MA/CT/NJ/FL; request TruBlue/101 Mobility FDDs; interview OTs/discharge planners/home-care owners.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator/GC partner; build referral CRM; price common bundles; diligence licensing/insurance.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on specialist installer or pilot referral partnerships before buying/building.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • No repeat/referral sources; all paid leads.
  • Unlicensed structural/bath work or insurance gaps.
  • Gross margin below 35% on install/equipment mix.
  • Referral concentration: one hospital/home-care source >30% revenue.
  • Working capital drag from equipment deposits or reimbursement delays.
  • Poor customer reviews around senior trust/safety.
