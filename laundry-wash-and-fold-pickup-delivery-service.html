Laundry / Wash-and-Fold Pickup & Delivery — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Acquisition and franchise underwriting memo for Dan-scale small business diligence.

CONDITIONALSBA / Operator Partner3-4.5x SDE

Verdict: CONDITIONAL

Route-like consumer laundry can work if density, driver productivity, and plant utilization are real; it is a no-go if it is just low-wage operations with churn and no route density.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Laundry / Wash-and-Fold Pickup & Delivery is a local services / consumer operating business built around paid, repeatable service delivery rather than a pure ecommerce product.
  • Who are the customers? B2C apartment dwellers, busy families, students, seniors; some B2B salons, spas, Airbnb hosts, medical offices.
  • Demand drivers: Recurring weekly/biweekly laundry, urban households without in-unit machines, convenience adoption, hotel/Airbnb turnover, and locker/pickup logistics. NE/NY/SFL relevance is high in dense affluent corridors; Boston/NYC apartment stock is especially relevant. Market size is estimate: IBISWorld and Grand View Research describe U.S. dry-cleaning/laundry and online laundry as multi-billion categories; Tide Cleaners reports 200+ locations; Clean Laundry and Lapels disclose franchise expansion.
  • Geographic relevance: NE/Boston and NY/CT/NJ are primary screens where density/income support premium service; SFL is relevant where weather, demographics, or affluent consumer behavior supports demand.
  • Market size: cited sources below or explicitly labeled estimates; no fake precision.

**Key assumptions:**

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite manager-run cash flow, not founder heroics.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $800K-$1.7M; SDE $150K-$350K after paid GM. 3+ years, 1,000+ recurring customers or contracted commercial accounts, documented route maps, POS retention data, washer/dryer age, lease, water/sewer costs, driver productivity. Avoid owner-only pickup, broken equipment, rent+utilities >18%, weak reviews, underpriced per-pound work. Franchise candidates: Lapels Cleaners (franchise fee often ~$50K; royalty/ad fund per FDD), Tide Cleaners (larger investment, strong brand), Clean Laundry (laundromat/wash-fold), Martinizing/1-800-DryClean route models. Request Item 19 and confirm plant vs outsourced production.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • See named franchise systems above; request current FDD, Item 19, closure history, royalty/ad fund, territory rules, and required capex before any commitment.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: Rinse, Poplin, Hamperapp, Tide Cleaners, Lapels, Martinizing, local laundromats and cleaners. PE interest exists in laundry routes and commercial linen, but consumer wash-fold remains fragmented. Moat is density, lockers, pickup reliability, plant throughput, customer habit, and commercial contracts.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Asset purchase preferred; seller note 10%-20% tied to customer retention. SBA 7(a) 10-year/~11%/10% down works if cash flow is tax-return verifiable. Down payment at base ≈$72K plus working capital. Payback base is ~2-3 years. Multiple arbitrage is honest: buy 3.5-4.0x, professionalize routes/recurring revenue, maybe exit ~4x.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): map Boston/NYC/SFL operators, compare route density, request franchise FDDs, scrape menus and reviews.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): operator-partner search from laundromat/dry-cleaning GMs; model plant capacity, labor, route times.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on one route-dense target or kill if no recurring book; do not build a van fleet before demand proof.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • NO-GO if less than 40% repeat revenue, no route density, washer/dryer capex cliff, unresolved environmental/lease issues, seller cannot prove SDE, paid GM wipes out cash flow, or delivery CAC exceeds first-order gross profit.
