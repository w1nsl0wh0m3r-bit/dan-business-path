Pickleball Club — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Dan-scale underwriting memo for pickleball club: buybox, unit economics, SBA financing discipline, competitive landscape and 90-day diligence path.

CONDITIONAL3-4.5x SDESBA 10yr/~11%NE / NY / SFL

Verdict: CONDITIONAL

Pickleball clubs are a CONDITIONAL real-estate operating bet: demand is obvious, but rent, buildout and utilization are brutal. Dan should favor acquisition of an existing profitable club or asset-light leased-court programming before funding a large ground-up indoor facility.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? A local operating company selling pickleball club through a focused retail, service or membership model rather than a venture-scale concept. The investable version is narrow, measurable and manager-runnable.
  • Who are the customers? Mix varies by category: B2C households/consumers are the core, with B2B accounts important where contracts, property managers, teams or employers can create recurrence.
  • Demand drivers: population density, convenience, recurring need, social discovery, aging/wellness trends, facility utilization or property turnover. Recurrence must be proven with customer/account cohorts, not assumed.
  • Geographic relevance: NE/Boston suburbs and NY/CT/NJ work where density and income support premium pricing; SFL is relevant for wellness, STR, sports and immigrant/import demand.
  • Market size: use cited national industry sources as directional only; local trade-area and broker diligence matter more than fake precision. Exact local TAM should be labeled estimate.

**Key assumptions:**

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment; debt service approximates 15%-17% of original principal annually at these terms.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — don't penalize lack of direct experience.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $750K-$3.0M, with enough scale to pay a manager/supervisor and still show $200K+ normalized SDE in the base case.
  • SDE range: $175K-$600K after replacing owner labor with market-rate management.
  • Years in business: 5+ years preferred; 3+ years acceptable if retention/cohorts are clean.
  • Route density / customer count: prove density through store trade-area, court utilization, route maps, account clusters or membership cohorts; no single customer/account >20%-25% of revenue.
  • Key assets to verify: tax returns, POS/booking data, customer list, lease/territory, licenses, insurance, staff roster, vendor contracts, equipment age, digital lead sources and review history.
  • Red flags to avoid: cash-heavy books, heroic owner selling, expiring lease, underpaid labor, weak customer data, platform dependence, regulatory ambiguity or a concept priced like a growth startup.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Franchise/brand analogs: The Picklr, Ace Pickleball Club, Dill Dinkers, PickleRage and local indoor clubs. FDD focus: court buildout, required territory, membership ramp, rent assumptions and franchisor unit-level disclosure.
  • Typical franchise fee + royalty: often $35K-$75K fee and 5%-8% royalty/brand fund for local-service/wellness/fitness concepts; verify current FDD, because fees move and category-specific terms vary.
  • Unit economics from franchise disclosure: rely only on Item 19 if present; if absent, treat franchisor sales claims as marketing until validated with franchisee calls.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors/roll-ups: national franchises where applicable, local independents, PE-backed multi-site wellness/fitness/service platforms, property-management vendors, and specialty retailers with better buying power.
  • PE activity: strongest in home services, medspa/wellness, facility services and youth/adult sports platforms; weakest in tiny specialty retail unless there is a repeatable multi-unit format.
  • Fragmentation level: high at Dan’s target size, but quality assets with clean books are scarce.
  • Moat: route density or trade-area density, recurring memberships/contracts, local reputation, reviews, trained labor, vendor access, compliance documentation and superior operations cadence.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note or earnout tied to revenue retention; inventory/equipment valued separately where material; lease assignment and key-employee retention are closing conditions.
  • SBA eligibility and terms: generally SBA-financeable if cash flow, collateral/equipment, lease, licenses and tax returns are clean; model 10-year amortization, ~11% coupon and 10%-15% equity.
  • Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly $60K-$250K for most $600K-$1.7M entry prices, plus working capital, inventory and diligence costs.
  • Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years; walk if normalized manager-run payback is above 5 years.
  • Honest multiple arbitrage: buy around 3.0-3.75x for owner-operated messiness; exit around 4.0x if professionalized, diversified and still small.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Build NE/NY/SFL target map, pull broker listings, request franchise FDDs, interview 3 operators and build a local pricing/competition grid.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Identify operator partner candidates, request tax/POS/customer cohort samples, inspect leases/equipment/licenses and run SBA lender pre-screen.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit one IOI/LOI only if manager-run SDE survives diligence; otherwise shelf with kill evidence and reuse learnings.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • Manager-run SDE falls below $175K or debt service coverage is fragile under an 11% SBA loan.
  • Owner personally drives sales, sourcing, clinical credibility, coaching/programming or daily route quality with no replacement plan.
  • Lease, license, medical/regulatory or platform dependence cannot be transferred cleanly.
  • Customer concentration, seasonality or churn makes base-case recurrence fake.
  • Multiple demanded is above 4.5x SDE without exceptional recurring revenue and documented growth.
  • Local market is already saturated and the target has no differentiated location, account base, reviews or cost advantage.
