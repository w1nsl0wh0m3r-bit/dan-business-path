1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Adult recreational sports clubs organize paid adult leagues, tournaments, clinics, social events and sometimes facility rentals for soccer, basketball, volleyball, pickleball, softball, kickball and similar sports. The best model is asset-light league administration using rented fields/courts plus sponsorship/bar partnerships; the riskier model owns a facility and behaves like a capital-intensive sports center.
  • Who are the customers? Mostly B2C adults ages 21-45 paying player/team fees; B2B demand from corporate leagues, sponsorships, charity events and bar/restaurant partners.
  • Demand drivers: Post-pandemic demand for offline social connection, wellness/fitness participation, corporate team-building, pickleball growth, and repeat seasonal league behavior. Recurrence comes from 6-10 week seasons, multi-sport players and captains who re-register teams.
  • Geographic relevance: Strong in Boston/Cambridge/Somerville, NYC/Brooklyn/Queens, Hoboken/Jersey City, Stamford, Miami/Fort Lauderdale and other dense young-professional markets. Weather pushes NE operators toward indoor partners in winter; SFL supports year-round outdoor play.
  • Market size: Sports & fitness participation is a large national pool; the specific adult social-sports-league TAM should be built bottoms-up by city using field permits, team counts and price/season. Public proxies include Volo Sports multi-city operations and IBISWorld fitness/recreational sports center coverage, but exact adult-league market size is not public.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $500K-$1.5M with at least two sports and three seasons/year.
  • SDE range: $125K-$350K after market comp for a GM/league director.
  • 5+ years in market with documented team/captain retention.
  • 1,500+ active annual players or 150+ recurring teams; no single venue >35% of capacity.
  • Verify permit/venue agreements, captain lists, email/SMS database, Stripe/POS exports, insurance, waiver process and sponsor contracts.
  • Avoid owner-only scheduling knowledge, handshake-only venue access, city permit fragility, unsafe waiver practices, negative referee/forfeit reputation.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • No dominant national franchise; compare Volo Sports, ZogSports, Sport & Social Club, CLUBWAKA and local pickleball/indoor-sports operators.
  • If franchised opportunity appears, demand Items 5-7/19/20, territory protection, insurance requirements, software fees and venue-control history.
  • Treat franchise economics as unproven until franchisee calls confirm player acquisition CAC and venue availability.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Volo Sports, ZogSports, CLUBWAKA, United Fray, Sport & Social Club, NYC Social, Boston Ski & Sports Club legacy networks, local pickleball clubs and municipal leagues. PE interest is limited in pure leagues but higher in facility platforms, youth sports and pickleball clubs. Fragmentation is high; moat is venue access, captain database, brand/community and operational reliability.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Asset purchase preferred; seller note tied to captain/team retention. SBA works for profitable independents but lenders will haircut add-backs and venue uncertainty. At $600K entry, 10% down implies ~$60K equity plus working capital; base payback can be under 2 years if retention holds. Multiple arbitrage is modest: buy ~3.5-4.0x, professionalize CRM/sponsorships, exit around ~4x unless platform scale emerges.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map adult leagues by sport/venue in Boston, NYC/NJ and SFL; scrape prices, seasons and reviews; identify venue choke points.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview 5 league operators/referees/venue managers; model field rental economics and captain retention; find operator-partner with sports ops DNA.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Pursue one LOI only if venue contracts and player cohorts are diligence-clean; otherwise test a micro-league in one sport/neighborhood.

8. Kill Criteria

  • No transferable venue access or permits.
  • Captain/team retention below 45% by season.
  • Owner personally knows every captain and runs weekly operations.
  • Insurance/waiver practices are sloppy.
  • Facility ownership required at an inflated real-estate multiple.
