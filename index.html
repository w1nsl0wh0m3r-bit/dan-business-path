    The 2×3 decision matrix.

    DIY / build
    Franchise
    Acquisition / buy
    Services
    Selective only

    Works for simple, pre-sellable routes: mosquito, soft wash, holiday lighting, maybe irrigation if a field lead exists.

    Must prove: demand before labor.

    Can work

    Use when SOPs, territory, training, insurance/compliance, or lead gen are worth royalties.

    Risk: paying for a logo.

    Best fit

    Buy customer books, techs, licenses, routes, reviews, and cash flow. Then modernize.

    Risk: owner-dependent job.

    Multi-unit
    Best fit

    Best when brand, product, content, community, and first-unit playbook are the upside.

    Examples: candy, kids STEM, sports academy.

    Selective

    Use only if training, sourcing, curriculum, site selection, or benchmarking changes the odds.

    Example: Metal Supermarkets.

    Selective

    Buy resales when reviews, lease/site, staff, or provider base matter more than concept creation.

    Example: medspa.

    Current strategic take.

    Current priority wedge: property-manager compliance routes + affluent-home systems: Backflow, fire/life-safety, hood cleaning, grease trap, stormwater, and AED compliance are tracked manually by many property managers. The software/calendar layer can become the acquisition channel for the service work.

    Services / acquisition

    Priority vein: property-manager compliance routes — fire/life-safety, backflow, kitchen hood, grease trap, stormwater — plus drainage/irrigation as affluent-home exterior systems.

    Multi-unit / DIY

    The universe now covers 100+ food, beverage, beauty, aesthetics, fitness, kids, pet, retail, specialty-counter, apparel, home, and CPG/light-manufacturing concepts.

    Franchise

    Middle path only when the operating system is worth the drag. Hood cleaning and Metal Supermarkets deserve FDD checks; generic seasonal service franchises need skepticism.
