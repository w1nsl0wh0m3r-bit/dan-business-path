1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Trenchless pipe lining repairs sewer/drain pipes from inside the existing pipe using CIPP/epoxy liners, pipe bursting, cleaning, and camera inspection instead of full excavation. Customers are residential homeowners with sewer laterals, HOAs/multifamily, restaurants/commercial properties, municipalities, and institutions. Demand is driven by aging clay/cast-iron pipe, expensive excavation/restoration, property transactions, and emergency sewer failures. NE/NY is strong because old housing stock and dense hardscape make excavation painful; SFL is viable around multifamily/HOA and older sewer laterals. Market size: use directional estimates only; IBISWorld tracks plumbing/drain/sewer categories and trenchless vendors cite a growing trenchless rehab market, but local TAM must be built by pipe-age and housing-stock mapping.
  • Who are the customers? Mix varies, but target underwriting should separate residential emergency demand from B2B/property-manager/municipal accounts.
  • Demand drivers: needs-based failure, compliance/inspection triggers, aging infrastructure, and recurring maintenance or consumables where applicable.
  • Geographic relevance: NE/Boston exurbs and NY/CT/NJ are primary for this batch; SFL is category-specific and weaker for basement/private-well models.
  • Market size: use cited national installed-base indicators where available; otherwise treat as local serviceable-market estimate and do bottom-up ZIP/county mapping.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue $1M-$3M, SDE $250K-$500K, 7+ years, 2+ crews, camera/jetter/liner equipment owned or clearly leased, municipal/commercial references, documented warranties, low callback rate, seller transition 6-12 months. Red flags: owner is only certified installer, no CCTV records, warranty reserves absent, OSHA/resin safety issues, too much one-time storm/sewer emergency revenue.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • NuFlow, Pipe Lining Supply/installer networks, Mr. Rooter/Roto-Rooter/Zoom Drain as adjacent drain franchises. Public franchise fee/royalty data varies and current FDDs are required; Item 19 should prove mix of drain calls versus lining, warranty claims, and crew productivity.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors include Roto-Rooter, Zoom Drain, Mr. Rooter, NuFlow licensees, local sewer/drain contractors, municipal rehab contractors, and PE-backed plumbing platforms. Fragmentation is high locally; PE consolidators like Apex Service Partners, Wrench Group, and NearU are active in home services/plumbing broadly. Moat comes from certified process, camera proof, reputation with property managers, emergency responsiveness, and avoiding failed-install reputational damage.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Asset purchase preferred with seller note/holdback tied to warranty claims and customer retention. SBA works if cash flow is tax-return supportable and equipment values are verified. At $300K SDE and 3.75x, price is ~$1.1M, down payment ~$110K plus working capital; base payback roughly 2.5-3.5 years if crew utilization holds. Multiple arbitrage is modest: buy 3.25-4.0x, exit 4.0-4.5x only if systems and management depth improve.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Days 1-30: map old-housing towns, sewer lateral ordinances, competitor reviews, broker outreach, request FDDs from drain franchises. Days 31-60: find operator/lead tech, inspect equipment, call property managers and plumbers for reputation checks, build job-level model. Days 61-90: LOI only with warranty holdback, seller transition, and verified crew productivity.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • Callback/warranty claims not tracked
  • No transferable lead installer
  • CIPP revenue less than 25% despite premium valuation
  • Equipment debt/capex understated
  • Owner refuses job-level margin disclosure
