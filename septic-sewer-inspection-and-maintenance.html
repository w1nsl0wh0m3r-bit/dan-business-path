1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • Septic pumping, sewer line camera inspection, drain maintenance, jetting, minor repairs and compliance inspections for residential, light commercial and municipal customers.
  • Customer mix is mostly B2C homeowners plus B2B property managers, restaurants, HOAs, realtors and municipalities.
  • Demand drivers: septic tanks require periodic pumping; EPA says most household tanks are pumped every 3-5 years, and aging sewer laterals create inspection/repair work.
  • Geographic relevance: very strong in Boston/NE exurbs, CT/NJ/Long Island and SFL septic-heavy pockets; national opportunity but density is local.
  • Market size: IBISWorld/industry pages put U.S. septic, drain and sewer cleaning at multi-billion scale; use as directional, not precise.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $1.0M-$3.0M with 2-6 trucks and clean dispatch history.
  • SDE range: $250K-$600K after normalizing owner labor and paid GM.
  • Years in business: 7+ years; strong Google reviews and municipal/property-manager references.
  • Route density / customer count: recurring reminder list of 2,000+ households or B2B contracts; tight service territory.
  • Key assets to verify: truck titles, pump/jetting/camera equipment, disposal relationships, permits, technician retention, call logs, repeat-customer database.
  • Red flags to avoid: unpermitted disposal, environmental claims, one master plumber/license holder leaving, deferred fleet capex, unverifiable cash.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • Roto-Rooter, Zoom Drain, Rooter-Man, Mr. Rooter, Benjamin Franklin Plumbing/drain add-on. Verify FDD Item 7/19, territory protection, national account economics, tech training and royalty/brand fund drag.
  • Franchise fee/royalty: commonly $40K-$60K fee and ~5%-8% royalty in home-services franchising; exact brand FDD required.
  • Unit economics: only trust Item 19, resale P&Ls or bank statements; drain-only models can look high-margin until technician wages and lead-gen are loaded.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: Roto-Rooter, Mr. Rooter, Zoom Drain, Wind River Environmental, Liquid Environmental Solutions, Rooter-Man, local septic haulers, plumbing contractors.
  • PE activity: Wind River is a visible roll-up in septic/grease/environmental services; home-services platforms keep buying drain/plumbing assets.
  • Fragmentation: high; many family-owned operators with 1-5 trucks.
  • Moat: licenses, disposal access, emergency response, route density, reviews, technician retention and property-manager relationships.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal: asset purchase, fleet/equipment schedule, seller note tied to customer retention, working-capital peg and environmental indemnity.
  • SBA eligibility: generally yes; SBA 7(a) supports changes of ownership up to $5M subject to lender diligence.
  • Down payment required: ~10%-15% equity, plus fleet repair reserve and working capital.
  • Payback period at base case: ~3-4 years if manager cost and truck capex are real.
  • Honest multiple arbitrage: buy 3.5-4.0x verified SDE, professionalize dispatch/CRM, exit around 4x to local consolidator.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map NE/SFL counties with septic density; build broker/off-market list; request franchise FDDs.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Review P&Ls, dispatch exports, truck schedules; interview operator/GM candidates and disposal partners.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on one target with equipment inspection, environmental review and seller training package.

8. Kill Criteria

  • Disposal/permitting not clean or insurer excludes key risks.
  • SDE disappears after replacing owner with paid operator.
  • Customer file is not exportable or repeat rate is weak.
  • Fleet requires >$250K immediate capex not priced into deal.
  • Lead flow depends on seller relationships that will not transfer.
