Waxing Studio — Deep Dive Investment Memo

Verdict: CONDITIONAL

Attractive beauty-services repeat model, but only if customer retention, labor productivity, and rent are clean. Franchise systems help demand generation; independent acquisitions need proof that guests stay when the owner is gone.

1. Category Definition & Demand Map

  • What is this business? Specialized body/facial waxing studios offering bikini, brow, face, underarm, leg, and male grooming services with membership/pass packages.
  • Who are the customers? Mostly B2C women 18-55; secondary male grooming and teen/young adult customers; some gift-card/event sales.
  • Demand drivers: Repeat service intervals every 3-6 weeks, beauty-services resilience, membership packages, and high gross margin labor/service mix.
  • Geographic relevance: Strong in affluent dense suburbs, college/young professional corridors, NYC/CT/NJ/MA; SFL has strong year-round demand.
  • Market size: Hair removal/waxing is a multi-billion U.S. personal-care niche; EWC disclosed 1,000+ centers and ~$955M systemwide sales in 2023 per public-company reporting/search result; use industry size as estimate.

Key assumptions:

  • Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
  • SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down; SBA says 7(a) can fund changes of ownership and caps at $5M.
  • Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment.
  • Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator — underwrite post-manager cash flow, not owner heroics.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

  • Revenue range: $650K-$1.2M mature studio.
  • SDE range: $150K-$300K after paying a studio manager.
  • Years in business: 3+ years with technician-level customer retention tracked.
  • Route density / customer count: 2,000+ active clients, 55%+ pre-book/rebook, 5-8 treatment rooms.
  • Key assets to verify: client list, booking history, package liability, esthetician licenses, lease, room utilization, merchant/chargeback history.
  • Red flags to avoid: top two waxers drive >40% of sales, package liabilities under-reserved, weak reviews, rent >12%, discount-heavy revenue.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

  • European Wax Center: franchise fee ~$45K; investment ~$328K-$897K; royalty 6%, marketing 3%; public system with 1,000+ centers.
  • Waxing the City: franchise fee ~$42.5K; investment ~$311K-$646K; royalty 6%, ad fund 2%; FDD summaries cite ~$489K AUV.
  • Uni K Wax: franchise fee ~$40K; royalty ~7%, ad ~2%; smaller system.
  • LunchboxWax/Radiant Waxing: boutique waxing franchises; request FDD to validate AUV and openings/closures.
  • FDD highlight: Item 19 must show mature-studio sales, technician productivity, and EBITDA after royalty/ad fund.

5. Competitive Landscape

  • Competitors: European Wax Center, Waxing the City, Uni K Wax, Radiant Waxing/LunchboxWax, Benefit BrowBar, Sephora/Ulta brow services, local salons/spas.
  • PE/activity: EWC is public; beauty-services franchising has sponsor interest, but individual studios remain fragmented.
  • Fragmentation: high; national chains have brand but many local salons compete on convenience/price.
  • Moat: client retention, esthetician training, booking cadence, membership/pass float, hygiene reputation, and location convenience.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

  • Typical deal structure: asset purchase with seller note/earnout for unverifiable add-backs; stock purchase only if licenses/contracts require it and liabilities are clean.
  • SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally SBA-financeable if profitable, owner-operated or manager-run with qualified operator, and no speculative real estate component; SBA 7(a) supports ownership changes up to $5M.
  • Down payment required at Dan's scale: roughly 10%-20% equity plus working capital; for a $700K-$1.4M enterprise value, plan $90K-$250K cash need.
  • Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.0 years on equity after debt service.
  • Honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale only after systems, manager bench, clean reporting, and repeat revenue are proven.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

  • Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Pull FDDs for EWC/Waxing the City/Uni K; scrape pricing and reviews in Boston/NY/CT/NJ; identify independent studios with 5+ rooms.
  • Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Interview operators; model labor utilization by service; diligence package liabilities and customer cohort retention.
  • Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Choose franchise resale vs independent acquisition; LOI only if retention and manager-adjusted SDE clear hurdles.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

  • Technician churn over 35% annually or top technician dependence.
  • Client retention/rebook under 45%.
  • Package/gift-card liabilities not recorded.
  • Rent + royalties + ad fund exceeds 20% of revenue.
  • No clean booking/POS export by technician and service.
  • Poor hygiene/review trend or licensing issues.
