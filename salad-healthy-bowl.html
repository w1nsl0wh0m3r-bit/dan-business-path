1. Category Definition & Demand Map

• What is this business? Fast-casual salad, grain bowl, wrap, and protein bowl restaurants with lunch/dinner, pickup/delivery, and catering revenue.

• Who are the customers? Mostly B2C office workers, health-oriented suburban consumers, students, gyms/fitness-adjacent buyers; B2B office catering can be 10-25% of sales in strong units.

• Demand drivers: Health/wellness, GLP-1 eating patterns, return-to-office lunches, digital ordering, catering, and repeat weekday behavior.

• Geographic relevance: Good in dense affluent NE/NY corridors and select SFL trade areas; rent/labor makes weak sites unforgiving.

• Market size: Directional: fast-casual and limited-service restaurants are very large; category-specific underwriting should anchor to FDD AUVs and local lunch/catering density rather than national market-size claims.

**Key assumptions:**

• Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE), not platform multiples.

• SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate, 10% down; assumes acquisition loan, not real estate-backed 25-year debt.

• Cash-on-cash = (SDE - debt service) / down payment; estimates use annual debt service of roughly 16.5%-16.8% of principal at 10 years / 11%.

• Operator partner: Dan can hire/partner with an operator; the model deducts enough management burden to avoid buying a job.

4. Path-Specific Buybox

What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category?

• Revenue range: $900K-$1.8M single unit or $2M-$5M small group

• SDE range: $175K-$450K after normalizing owner role

• Years in business: 3+ years with post-COVID stability

• Route density / customer count: Dense office/suburban lunch node; catering accounts; 2,000+ loyalty customers preferred

• Key assets to verify: POS cohorts, delivery mix, food/labor cost, lease, catering list, manager, recipes/SOPs

• Red flags to avoid: Founder-chef dependency, delivery-app dependence, no catering, food cost >33%, labor >32%, lease cliff

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?

Top candidates: Just Salad (verify current FDD; royalty often cited ~6% plus marketing), Saladworks (WoWorks; royalty/marketing commonly cited 6% + fees), Chop Stop (smaller system; lower investment/royalty cited), sweetgreen is not a franchise. Item 19 AUV dispersion matters more than brand deck.

5. Competitive Landscape

• Top competitors / roll-ups: sweetgreen, CAVA, Just Salad, Chopt, Saladworks, Fresh&Co, local poke/acai/bowl shops, Panera, Chipotle lifestyle bowls

• PE activity: CAVA public; sweetgreen public; Woworks owns Saladworks; consumer growth capital active but single-unit resales still trade on SDE.

• Fragmentation level: Moderate: national brands in urban nodes, many independents locally.

• Moat: Site selection, speed, digital ordering, catering relationships, menu execution, healthy brand trust.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline

• Typical deal structure: Asset purchase with seller training, lease assignment, manager retention bonus; avoid stock unless liabilities are clean.

• SBA eligibility and terms: Generally eligible if profitable and books are clean; model 10-year / ~11% / 10% down.

• Down payment required at Dan's scale: $25K-$150K for Dan-sized single-unit/small group depending on SDE and price.

• Payback period at base case: Base case 3-5 years if manager-run SDE holds.

• Honest multiple arbitrage: 3.5x in can maybe exit 4x+ if it becomes a two-to-four unit system; no platform-multiple fantasy.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This)

• Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Map 50 trade areas, request FDDs, visit 20 stores, scrape menu pricing/reviews, find restaurant operator candidates.

• Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Get 3 seller/broker packages, model labor schedules and catering, interview current/former franchisees.

• Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Submit IOI only on profitable resale or shelve for higher-quality service categories.

8. Kill Criteria

What would make this a NO-GO?

• No verified POS/customer cohort data

• Base case cash-on-cash depends on Dan working shifts

• Delivery apps exceed 25%-30% of sales without direct customer ownership

• Lease/buildout requires >$400K before demand proof

• No credible GM/operator partner
