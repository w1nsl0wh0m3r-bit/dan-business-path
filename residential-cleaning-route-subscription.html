Residential Cleaning Route / Subscription — Acquisition Deep Dive

Underwriting residential cleaning subscription routes as a buy-and-build target: recurring revenue quality, labor management, route density, and operator-partner economics.

Conditional Acquisition UW Score: 3.20 Confidence: Medium Date: May 26, 2026

Executive Verdict

Acquire a recurring cleaning route — if the subscription density and labor management are proven.

Best target: a mature, manager-run residential cleaning business with 200+ recurring biweekly clients, documented churn <30%, route density within a 15-minute radius, and a lead cleaner/manager who handles day-to-day operations.

Why it works: $12-20B fragmented market, 90%+ independent operators, recurring weekly/biweekly demand, low capital intensity, SBA financeable. Route density + subscription discipline creates a defensible local position.

Why it can fail: Cleaner turnover runs 50-100% annually — labor is the primary margin killer. Customer churn is price-sensitive. Franchise premium (Molly Maid, MaidPro) rarely justified at Dan's scale vs. independent acquisition.

1. Market Sizing & Demand Drivers

US residential cleaning services market: $12-20B (IBISWorld, Statista). Highly fragmented — no single company holds >1% national share. ~890,000 maids and housekeeping cleaners employed in the US per BLS (2024).

Demand drivers: dual-income households, aging population needing help, Airbnb/STR turnover cleaning, post-COVID hygiene awareness. Recurring subscription models (weekly, biweekly) generate 60-75% of revenue for established operators.

Geographic fit: Boston/NE (high dual-income household density), NY/CT/NJ (apartment-heavy, service demand), SFL (year-round season, snowbird turnover). All three markets are excellent.

5. Competitive Landscape

90%+ independent. Thousands of local operators with <5 employees. No dominant national player.

PE / roll-up: Neighborly (Harvest Partners / KKR-backed) owns Molly Maid. Limited broader PE activity — no dominant national roll-up yet. Flywheel Brands and similar home-service holding companies are adjacent.

Moat: Route density + recurring subscription relationships + local reputation. Low capital barriers mean low individual moat, but a 250-client recurring book is hard to replicate quickly.

6. Financing & Capital Discipline

Valuation frame
SDE multiple: 2-3.5x based on route quality and recurring revenue %. Never pay >3.5x SDE.
Financing frame
SBA 7(a), 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down. Seller note 10-20% common.

Capital discipline rules: Verify recurring revenue by calling 20+ clients. Deduct owner labor at market rate ($55-65K) before calculating true SDE. If owner IS the primary cleaner, haircut SDE by 40-60%.

7. Acquisition Buybox

Target profile:

  • Revenue: $400K-$1.2M
  • SDE: $100K-$300K
  • 200+ recurring clients with documented service history
  • Route density: clients within 15-min drive radius
  • Manager or lead cleaner who runs day-to-day
  • CRM/scheduling system in place (Jobber, ZenMaid, Launch27)
  • 3+ years operating history with tax returns

Red flags: Owner is primary cleaner, no scheduling system, paper records only, revenue concentrated in <10 clients, churn >40%.

8. 90-Day Roadmap

Days 1-30

Market Mapping & Operator Outreach

  • Map top 20 residential cleaning operators in Boston/NE, NY/NJ, SFL by ZIP
  • Contact 3-5 owners age 55+ for informational interviews
  • Interview Molly Maid and MaidPro franchisees about unit economics
  • Build labor cost model by geography (MA $16-20/hr; SFL $13-18/hr)
  • Identify operator partner candidate with cleaning management experience
Days 31-60

Financial Modeling & Diligence

  • Build 3-case model for $500K-$1M revenue route acquisition
  • Source 3-5 broker listings (BizBuySell, Quiet Light, LoopNet)
  • Validate cleaner wages and turnover rates from operator interviews
  • Model customer churn scenarios and break-even route density
  • Verify 3 years tax returns vs. P&L for any LOI-stage target
Days 61-90

LOI or Kill Decision

  • Submit LOI on strongest acquisition target if metrics clear buybox
  • If no target meets criteria, decide: build from scratch (6-12mo ramp) or shelf
  • Kill if: no target at ≤3.5x SDE with <35% owner-dependency

Kill Criteria

  • Cannot find target with <35% owner-dependency at ≤3.5x SDE
  • Cleaner wage floor exceeds $22/hr with no pricing power to offset
  • Customer churn exceeds 40% annually in acquired book
  • Route density below 3 stops/cleaner/day (unprofitable windshield time)
  • Recurring revenue below 50% of total
  • Manager salary ($60-80K) erodes SDE below $100K post-acquisition
  • Cannot recruit/retain cleaners within 90 days in target market
