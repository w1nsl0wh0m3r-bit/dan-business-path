Executive Verdict

HOODZ is the only credible franchisor — but the 10% royalty tax on a compliance commodity makes independent likely superior for Dan.

Best franchisor: HOODZ (BELFOR Franchise Group) — 129+ units, FDDIQ attractiveness score 32, Item 19 present, FBR satisfaction award, SBA eligible with 15 active lenders.

Why now: NFPA 96 mandates quarterly-to-annual cleaning of every commercial kitchen exhaust system. Recurring, non-discretionary demand. Low startup barriers ($10K–$50K independent). Restaurant density is stable.

Why Dan: Manager-run potential, recurring revenue database, compliance scheduling, route optimization — a systems and diligence problem, not a wrench-turning problem.

Biggest risk: The franchise adds ~10–15% revenue uplift through national accounts and brand credibility, but costs 11% of gross revenue. An independent operator at the same revenue keeps $48K+/year more. Single-territory HOODZ, manager-run, yields only ~$32K owner income.

1. Category Definition

Kitchen exhaust hood cleaning businesses clean and maintain commercial kitchen exhaust systems (hood, duct, fan, up-blast) to NFPA 96 bare-metal standard. Services include recurring contract cleaning, filter exchange, grease trap cleaning, fan repairs, access panel installation, conveyor oven cleaning, and pressure washing of dumpster pads and concrete.

Demand driver

NFPA 96 mandates cleaning frequency by cooking type: monthly (solid fuel), quarterly (high-volume 24hr/charbroil/wok), semi-annually (moderate), annually (low-volume). Non-compliance = failed fire inspection, fines, insurance denial, shutdown.

Recurring revenue
Quarterly-to-annual cleaning contracts. Compliance = non-discretionary. Customer retention 85%+ if quality is reliable.
Low startup (independent)
$10K–$50K startup. Pressure washer, chemicals, van, basic tools. No franchise needed to enter.
Brand value: marginal
Restaurants pick cleaners on quality, price, and reliability — not franchise brand. National accounts are the one exception.

Bottom line: HOODZ franchise adds ~10–15% revenue uplift through national accounts and brand credibility, but costs 11% of gross. Net value is approximately break-even to negative for single-territory operators. The $48K/yr royalty difference is the entire owner income gap in a manager-run model.

When franchise wins: Multi-territory (2–3+), $1M+ revenue plan, leveraging national accounts for >25% of revenue, and you need training/SOPs because you have zero service business experience.

6. Risks & Kill Criteria

Structural risks
  • 10% royalty is a margin killer on a commodity service
  • Low barriers to entry ($10K independent startup)
  • Physical, grueling work — nights, heat, grease, confined spaces
  • High technician turnover in low-status trade
  • Restaurant closure rate ~60% within 3 years = constant customer replacement
  • EPA wastewater disposal compliance requirements
Kill the franchise if
  • Single-territory manager-run EBITDA below $50K after royalty
  • Item 19 for single-territory shows median revenue below $350K
  • Franchisee references say royalty "not worth it"
  • Target territory has fewer than 200 commercial kitchens
  • Total all-in investment exceeds $275K for single territory
  • Cannot identify 150+ accounts in territory

7. 30 / 60 / 90 Franchise Roadmap

Days 1–30: Research & Validate

Franchise vs independent decision data

  • Request HOODZ FDD — review Items 5–20 with franchise attorney
  • Call 3 HOODZ franchisees: first-year revenue, national account %, would-you-buy-again
  • Call 3 independent hood cleaning operators: revenue, margins, how they compete with HOODZ
  • Map target territory: count restaurants, hospitals, schools, commercial kitchens
  • Build side-by-side P&L models: HOODZ single, HOODZ multi, independent
  • Check state licensing requirements (MA, NY, CT, FL)
Days 31–60: Decision & Financing

Lock the path

  • Make franchise vs independent decision
  • If franchise: confirm territory availability, attend HOODZ discovery day
  • If independent: complete training/certification, purchase equipment
  • Pre-qualify SBA lender (franchise) or confirm startup capital (independent)
  • Test market: call 20 restaurants for pricing and current provider satisfaction
Days 61–90: Launch or Kill

Execute or exit category

  • If franchise: sign agreement, schedule Ann Arbor training, begin pre-selling territory
  • If independent: complete licensing, buy equipment, launch marketing, book first 20 accounts
  • If neither model clears the bar: kill and move to next category.

Final Recommendation

Primary: Go independent. The franchise math doesn't work for single-territory manager-run. HOODZ takes $48K/year for national account access and training you can replicate for $5K. The compliance demand doesn't require a franchise brand — it requires quality, reliability, and route density.

Backup: HOODZ multi-territory (if franchise is chosen). Target 3 territories, $1M+ revenue, amortize the royalty over a larger base. BELFOR national accounts become more valuable at scale.

Decision Dan needs to make: Is the goal to own a franchise or to own a hood cleaning business? If the business, independent wins. If Dan specifically wants franchise infrastructure and is willing to pay 11% for it, HOODZ multi-territory — not single.
