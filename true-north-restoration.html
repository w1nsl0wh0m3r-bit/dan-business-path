2. Category Definition & Demand Map

What's Included

  • Water damage mitigation and structural drying
  • Fire and smoke damage restoration
  • Mold remediation and containment
  • Biohazard / crime scene cleanup
  • Storm and catastrophic loss response
  • Reconstruction / rebuild services (some operators)

Revenue Streams

  • Insurance claims work — 70-80% of revenue for most operators. Non-discretionary, adjuster-referred.
  • Cash-pay jobs — property managers, commercial clients, self-pay homeowners
  • Reconstruction margins — if licensed for rebuild, significantly higher ticket ($50K-$500K per job)
  • Emergency response — board-up, tarping, temporary repairs ($2K-$8K per call)

Customer Types & Referral Dynamics

  • Insurance adjusters — #1 growth driver. Relationships are king. Adjusters refer to operators they trust.
  • Plumbers / roofers — first on scene, kickback referral relationships (common but ethically gray)
  • Property managers — recurring commercial accounts, less seasonal
  • TPAs — Third Party Administrators (Sedgwick, Gallagher Bassett) route claims to certified operators

7. Operator Reality

Where Operators Win

  • Insurance referral relationships — adjusters who trust you send consistent work. One good adjuster relationship = $200K+/yr in referred jobs
  • Reconstruction licensing — adds 40-60% revenue per job. Mitigation-only operators leave money on the table
  • Multi-crew scaling — operators who hire reliable crew chiefs can run 3-5 jobs simultaneously
  • Commercial accounts — property managers with multiple buildings = recurring revenue, less seasonal
  • Climate frequency — every freeze, hurricane, and flood is a demand spike. TX, FL, Gulf Coast are gold mines

Where Owners Lose Money

  • 24/7 emergency response — if you can't staff reliable on-call crews, you lose jobs to competitors who answer the phone at 2am
  • Labor churn — restoration crews are hard to find ($15-25/hr manual labor, dirty conditions). Turnover is 50-80%/yr at small shops
  • AR / collections — insurance companies pay 30-90 days. Cash flow management is critical. Many new operators go under waiting for checks
  • Equipment costs — dehumidifiers, extractors, moisture meters. $35K-$65K upfront, ongoing maintenance/replacement
  • Kickback trap — plumbers expect referral fees. Ethical operators refuse; losing those referrals costs jobs early on

9. Risks & Kill Criteria

Category Risks

  • 24/7 operational burden: Emergency response never stops. Without a reliable operations manager, the business owns the owner.
  • Insurance relationship dependency: #1 growth driver is also #1 risk. Lose an adjuster relationship = lose 20-30% of revenue overnight.
  • TPA certification: Some TPAs require 2+ years operating history before certifying. New entrants face a chicken-and-egg problem.
  • Climate variance: Drought years = fewer water jobs. Year-over-year revenue can swing 30%+ based on weather.
  • Labor market: Restoration work is hard, dirty, and seasonal. Crew turnover averages 50-80% at small operators.

Franchise-Specific Risks (True North)

  • Rapid growth: 0 to 26 units in 5 years. Support infrastructure may lag unit sales.
  • Founder-led: Donat brothers still running it. Key man risk.
  • Small SBA sample: 7 loans tells us nothing about long-term default risk.
  • Conversion bias: Top performers are likely converted independents, not franchise-created outcomes.
  • Faith-based positioning may limit buyer pool on exit.

Kill Criteria

  • SBA default rate above 10% for any finalist brand
  • No Item 19 (means franchisor won't show you the numbers)
  • Bottom quartile franchisee earning less than $50K (below living wage)
  • Franchisor can't provide 3+ franchisee references
  • More than 10% net unit decline in any year

13. Final Recommendation

Buy Independent. True North as Backup.

The restoration category is a fit — fragmented, insurance-backed, recession-proof, systems-modernizable. But True North's franchise wrapper adds questionable value for a sophisticated operator who can build relationships and implement SOPs independently.

Specific play:

  • Buy existing independent: $500K-$1.2M revenue, established insurance relationships
  • Price: $350K-$700K (3-4x SDE)
  • Structure: SBA 7(a) with 15-20% equity
  • Operator model: Hire project manager for day-to-day. Dan manages growth + insurance relationships.

If no independent deal in 90 days: True North is the best franchise option. Lowest fee burden, fastest growth, values-aligned. But underwrite to $579K, not $985K.
