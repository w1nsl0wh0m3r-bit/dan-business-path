Executive Verdict

Franchise, but only with 101 Mobility as the primary path and only after unit-level franchisee calls validate manager-run profitability.

Best franchisor: 101 Mobility. It has the best blend of Item 19 disclosure, national maturity, aging-in-place fit, and SBA performance.

1. Category Definition & Franchise Landscape

This category covers stairlifts, ramps, vertical platform lifts, porch lifts, patient lifts, pool lifts, home elevators, bathroom safety products, scooters, power chairs, lift chairs, grab bars, and adjacent accessibility products. Revenue comes from product sales, installation, rentals, service/repair, preventive maintenance, warranty work, and home safety assessments.

Demand driver
Aging-in-place, fall risk, surgeries, progressive mobility conditions, veteran needs, and adult children paying for urgent home safety.
Customer channels
Private-pay seniors, adult children, VA/veterans, OTs/PTs, rehab discharge planners, home health, elder-law, property managers.
Franchise field
101 Mobility, Mobility City, Mobility Plus, Next Day Access, AMRAMP, GreenLight, Nationwide Lifts, Stiltz, Liftology.

Business model variants: full mobility/accessibility dealer, ramp/access specialist, mobility retail store, and lift/elevator specialist. This is not pure DME billing and not pure handyman. The best version is a referral-heavy local accessibility dealer with strong installation quality and service/rental recurring revenue.

Treat as a watch candidate only until current and former franchisee calls explain the default pattern.

Next Day Access — Growth Story with Default Kill Flag

Next Day Access shows strong growth and revenue signals: FDDIQ 2024 median revenue $702,876 and net unit change +22. But the SBA signal is severe: 50% default rate on 18 loans. Under this doctrine, that is a hard pass unless the match or denominator is wrong. It should not outrank 101 Mobility for Dan.

8. Risks & Kill Criteria

Kill the franchise path if:

  • 101 Mobility will not provide current FDD, Item 19 definitions, Item 20 tables, and territory details.
  • Fewer than 6 current franchisees will speak candidly before discovery day.
  • Bottom-half franchisees cannot clear paid-manager profit.
  • Franchisees say owner selling/referral networking is mandatory for years 1–3.
  • Available territory lacks senior density, affluent adult children, VA/referral channels, and high-income suburbs.
  • Web leads/national accounts are not protected or lead routing weakens territory value.
  • Startup capital exceeds $350K without clear economics.
  • Current FDD shows ugly litigation, closures, transfers, or fee discretion not reflected here.

11. Winslow Augmentation — What Would Make This Investable

Sharper take: this is not attractive as a generic DME retail store. It becomes interesting only as a local aging-in-place services business: stairlifts, ramps, bathroom safety, lift chairs, scooters, repairs, rentals, and referral partnerships with discharge planners, PT/OT networks, elder-law attorneys, and senior living communities.

Must prove

  • Private-pay mix is high enough to avoid reimbursement grind and working-capital drag.
  • Install/service revenue has real gross margin and repeat/referral behavior.
  • Territory has dense 70+ population, older housing stock, and affluent adult children.
  • Lead sources are diversified beyond paid search and one hospital relationship.
  • Inventory turns and demo/rental economics are clean, not cash traps.

Likely kill flags

  • Medicare/insurance complexity dominates the P&L.
  • Franchisor fees absorb the value of local referrals and service execution.
  • Too much showroom rent/inventory for too little route/service revenue.
  • Customer acquisition depends on urgent, low-repeat consumer transactions.
  • Owner must personally sell every stairlift/home-mod project.

Best next step: compare franchise route against buying an independent stairlift/home-accessibility installer. If independents show stronger service margins with fewer franchise constraints, skip franchise fees and build the referral engine directly.

Final Recommendation

Proceed to diligence with 101 Mobility only. It is the only brand in this set with acceptable SBA performance, useful Item 19 disclosure, mature national infrastructure, and a clean aging-in-place thesis.

Backup: Mobility City only if its 20% SBA default rate can be explained and current/former franchisees validate after-fee profit.

Pass/watch: Next Day Access and Mobility Plus fail the SBA default screen for now. AMRAMP and GreenLight are watch candidates, not primary Dan paths.

Dan decision: Does he want a mission-driven but operationally real sales/installation/service business that likely requires early owner energy in referral development? If yes, 101 Mobility deserves the next sprint. If he wants passive manager-run from day one, move this to Watch until a profitable resale appears.
