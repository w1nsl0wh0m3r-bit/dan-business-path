1. Category Definition & Demand Map - What is this business? A consultative retrofit contractor/concierge that audits homes, designs heat-pump/HVAC, heat-pump water heater, panel and weatherization scopes, then manages rebates and vetted installers. - Who are the customers? Affluent homeowners, oil/propane homes, aging housing stock, landlords and small multifamily owners. - Demand drivers: IRA/state rebates, decarbonization mandates, oil-to-electric savings, aging HVAC equipment, and complexity that creates willingness to pay for handholding. - Geographic relevance: Excellent NE relevance: cold-climate heat pumps now work in Northeast/Mid-Atlantic climates; local TAM tied to oil/propane homes and rebate budgets. - Market size: cited sector proxies below; otherwise build local TAM bottoms-up from households, projects, trade accounts, tickets and frequency.

**Key assumptions:**
- Entry multiples: 3-4.5x SDE at Dan's scale ($200-500K SDE). NOT 8-10x.
- SBA 7(a): 10-year term, ~11% rate (2026), 10% down.

4. Path-Specific Buybox What makes a GOOD acquisition target in this category? - Revenue range: base-case revenue band above, with stable or growing trailing-36-month sales. - SDE range: $150K-$500K, cleanly proven after manager/operator salary. - Years in business: 5+ years with transferable staff, reviews and vendor/customer relationships. - Route density / customer count: concentrated local demand, repeat accounts, no single customer >10%-15% of revenue. - Key assets to verify: POS/CRM exports, bank/tax reconciliation, leases, licenses, employee retention, vendor terms, reviews and lead sources. - Red flags to avoid: owner-as-product, cash leakage, unverifiable add-backs, lease cliff, churn, weak staff bench or revenue dependent on paid ads only.

What makes a good FRANCHISE candidate?
- Top franchise/brand candidates: Aire Serv, One Hour Heating & Air, Mister Sparky/Authority Brands, Healthy Home Energy & Consulting-style independents; verify Item 19 and rebate-admin capabilities.
- FDD highlight: require Items 5, 6, 7, 19 and 20; compare closures/transfers and local marketing requirements.
- Unit economics from franchise disclosure: use Item 19 only when current; otherwise validate through franchisee calls and resale P&Ls.

5. Competitive Landscape - Top competitors or roll-ups active in this space: Local HVAC contractors, electricians, insulation/weatherization firms, Sealed, Elephant Energy, BlocPower, Mitsubishi/Fujitsu dealer networks. - PE activity: sponsor interest exists in scaled franchisors, home services and specialty retail platforms, but local independents remain fragmented. - Fragmentation level: high locally; chains set pricing/service expectations but mom-and-pops still dominate many ZIPs. - Moat: reviews, staff/vendor retention, trade/referral relationships, memberships/contracts, local SEO, installation/service quality and operating systems.

6. Financing, Valuation & Capital Discipline - Typical deal structure: asset purchase, seller note/earnout tied to retention, working-capital peg and training transition. - SBA eligibility and typical loan terms: generally eligible if operating for profit in the U.S., creditworthy and not ineligible; SBA 7(a) can fund ownership changes. - Down payment required at Dan's scale: 10%-20% equity plus working capital/reserve. - Payback period at base case: target 2.5-4.5 years after debt service and manager salary. - honest multiple arbitrage: ~3x in → 4x out at this scale; value creation is systems and de-risking, not multiple fantasy.

7. 90-Day Roadmap (If Dan Pursues This) - Phase 1 (Days 1-30): Market mapping, broker outreach, franchise FDD requests, review scraping and 10 operator/franchisee calls. - Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Operator partner search, financial modeling, local wage/vendor checks, site visits and lender pre-screen. - Phase 3 (Days 61-90): LOI on first target or franchise signing decision only if source-level financials and operating proof clear kill criteria.

8. Kill Criteria What would make this a NO-GO? - Seller cannot reconcile tax returns, bank deposits and POS/customer data. - Manager-run SDE falls below $150K or DSCR below 1.25x after normalized labor. - Revenue depends on owner relationships, unsustainable discounts, or one vendor/customer/channel. - Lease/territory/franchise transfer terms are unfavorable. - Reviews, employee churn or compliance/licensing issues suggest hidden service-quality risk.

Sources - DOE says air-source heat pumps can deliver two to four times more heat energy than electricity consumed and are viable in cold regions. - ENERGY STAR notes HPWH installation constraints including 40°-90°F spaces and 1,000 cubic feet of air volume. - DOE Home Energy Rebates/IRA programs create consumer incentive tailwinds; state rollout varies. - SBA 7(a) loans can fund changes of ownership, working capital, equipment and supplies up to $5 million — [SBA 7(a)](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans/7a-loans) - Small local owner-operated service/retail acquisitions at Dan’s target size should be underwritten at 3.0-4.5x SDE; higher sponsor multiples are not used here — estimate from small-business broker/SBA market practice.
