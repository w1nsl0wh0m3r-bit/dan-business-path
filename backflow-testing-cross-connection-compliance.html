1. Category Definition & Demand Map

Backflow testing sits at the intersection of water safety compliance and recurring route service — two of the most defensible small-business characteristics. Every commercial building, multifamily property, irrigation system, and healthcare facility with a backflow prevention assembly must have it tested annually by a certified professional. This isn't optional: property owners who fail to comply face water service disconnection, fines, and liability exposure. The market is $600M–$800M in the US, deeply fragmented, and growing with new construction and tightening enforcement.

Demand drivers and recurrence: Demand is driven by structural need and local market density. Recurrence varies by service line — some categories generate repeat business naturally, others require active reactivation.

Geographic fit: Target markets: Boston/NE, NY/CT/NJ, Miami/SFL. Evaluate local population density, income levels, and competitive density before committing to any territory.

8. AI / Systems Edge

High-value AI/automation playbook: (1) Automated compliance notice tracking — ingest municipal water purveyor databases, auto-generate renewal reminders to property managers 60/30/14 days before deadline. (2) Route optimization — AI scheduling that minimizes drive time between test sites, accounts for seasonal volume spikes. (3) Digital test submission — mobile app for field techs to capture results, photos, and signatures; auto-submit to water purveyor and customer. (4) Predictive device failure — analyze test history to flag devices likely to fail next cycle, proactively pitch repair/replacement. (5) CRM/reactivation — identify lapsed accounts, auto-sequence outreach to property managers. This is one of the highest AI-edge categories because compliance scheduling and documentation are inherently data-driven.

Where AI creates real advantage vs table stakes: Lead capture and missed-call recovery is table stakes in 2025. Real advantage comes from predictive scheduling, automated reactivation campaigns, and data-driven pricing that most small operators will never implement.

What changes the score
